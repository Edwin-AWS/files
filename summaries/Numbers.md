# Numbers

## Introduction

🆕 Book: Numbers

Introduction to Numbers

✍🏽 Authorship
#️⃣ The Book of Numbers is attributed to Moses, who is believed to have compiled the first five books of the Bible (also known as the Pentateuch)  during the Israelites' wilderness wandering prior to their entry into the Promised Land.

#️⃣ The title "Numbers" is derived from the title   "Arithmoi," a Latin word "Numeri," of which  "Numbers" is a translation.

Background
The book of Numbers opens at Sinai with the taking of a census of all adult males who are twenty years old or more and are able to serve in the army. The book adds further regulations beyond those enumerated in Leviticus, traces the march from Sinai, the wanderings in the wilderness, to the arrival on the steppes of Moab, and ends with a group of regulations.

⏩ Key themes in the book include: 
(i) highlighting and exalting Jehovah as the supreme God in all His holiness, majesty, and care for His chosen people,
(ii) warning of the danger of rebellion and impatience against God and His chosen leaders,
(iii) reminding us that God is a God who delights in order, good planning and organisation for the young nation of Israel,
(iv) depicting, in poignant detail, about God's desire to dwell with and among His people as revealed in His command to the people to erect an earthly Tabernacle (tent of meeting),
(v) recalling God's promise to Abraham in Genesis, and by extension, to all his descendants by faith:"I will bless those who bless you and whoever curses you I will curse." 
(vi) read together with the other books of the Pentateuch (the five books of Moses), the book of Numbers tells a single story of the beginning of God's redemptive work in the world.

 Conclusion 
Numbers is a living book that has (and still does) continue to  minister to the spiritual lives of men throughout history.  The census of the all adult males all according to their tribes, emphasizes that to God "every person counts" and is of great value in His eyes. Finally, Numbers is showing the modern church that God delights in order and good organisation with every member playing the role assigned to them in their spiritual march towards the Promised Heavenly Canaan.

🛐 Prayer
Our Lord, as we begin the study of the book of Numbers we pray for the power of the Holy Spirit to be with us throughout so that we will gain both the  understanding and the zeal to heed its teachings. In Jesus' name we pray. Amen.

## Numbers 1 — A census of all males


### Summary

God commanded Moses to take a census of, and register, all males of the military age of twenty years and above in readiness for Israel's march, as a fighting force, from Sinai to Canaan. The Levites were exempted from the census and enlisting for military service. Leaders from each of Israel's twelve tribes helped with the task of enumerating the people. The total number of all males who were enumerated is 603,550.


### Key Lessons


**The importance of Planning and Good Organisation**


1️⃣ The importance of Planning and Good Organisation

⏩ By commanding a census to register all israelite males before their march onto Canaan, God is teaching the Church leadership on the importance of adequate planning and good organisation before undertaking any major enterprise for without these, there is failure and disaster.(Luke 14:28-32)

⏩ We must, however, always allow God to lead and direct all our planned activities or else all effort will be in vain. (Psalms127:1,2).  The census at Sinai was a success because Moses acted on God's command and supervision (v.1).

⏩ Total Member Involvement- No one or no tribe was left out of the census and enlisting for military duty except the Levites whose unique duties are stated more fully in chapters 3 and 4. Every member of the Church should be involved in the Church's Great Commission.

⏏️ Delegate. Distribute. Involve everyone. heads and leaders from each of the twelve tribes assisted Moses with the task of registering the people (vs. 16-18). Leaders will succeed when they apply this leadership principle. (Exodus 18:17,21).

**God counts every soul because every soul counts.**


2️⃣ God counts every soul because every soul counts.

⏏️ By commanding Moses to take a census of ".... listing every man by name one by one"  (v.2), God is reminding us that He knows each and every one of His children personally (2 Timothy 2:19).

⏩ The divine Shepherd is also reminding us that He is acquainted with each member of His flock (John 10:3).

⏩ Similarly, God's servants to whom He has entrusted His flock, must take a keen interest in each and every church member personally and to minister to their individual spiritual needs accordingly.

**God is faithful always**


3️⃣ God is faithful always

️ From only 70 (seventy) people who  emigrated to Egypt  (Genesis 46:26,27), God multiplied them to the present population of 603,550 (six hundred and three thousand five hundred and fifty). This number excludes women and children - Thus, God is proving His faithfulness to the promise which He had made to Abraham more than four hundred years earlier (Genesis 12:2; 13:16) We must trust God.

⏩ Israel's population grew in spite of all genocidal attempts against them by successive Egyptian Pharaohs. (Exodus 1:6-9)

⏩ "God is ever near to the brokenhearted and those who are crushed in spirit." (Psalms 34:18).

## Numbers 2


### Summary

God commands the camping and marching arrangement of the Israelites in the wilderness according to their tribes and families; ordering the people to keep a safe distance from the tent of meeting. Three tribes are to camp on each of the the four sides around the tent of meeting.


### Key Lessons


**God's Desire to be among His people**


1️⃣ God's Desire to be among His people

⏩ The Sanctuary (Tent of meeting)  was at the centre of the Israelites' camping arrangement.  This was, and still is, a reminder of God's desire to dwell with and among His people.

#️⃣ We must let God be the centre and focus of all our planned programmes and activities.

**Respect for God's Holiness and Majesty**


2️⃣ Respect for God's Holiness and Majesty

⏩ The injunction on the Israelite congregation to pitch their dwelling tents a distant from the Sanctuary is to remind us that while God desires to be near and among His people, we must always behold Him with awe and reverence because of His holiness and majesty.

**Discipline and order vital for success**


3️⃣ Discipline and order vital for success

⏩ Everyone must know and keep to their assigned place and role

⏩ No disorderliness and haphazardness must be entertained in the spiritual march to the Heavenly Canaan; and in all church activities.

⏩ Every church member or church department must keep to, and focus on doing the role which God has assigned them in the Great Commission.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Lord God, we thank you that you desire to dwell among us, your people. Please give us the spiritual discernment to ever recognise and honour your holiness. In Jesus' name we pray. Amen.

## Numbers 3


### Summary

In Numbers 3 and 4, God gives a brief history of the family of Aaron who were to serve in the sacred office of priesthood and of the Levites who were wholly given to Aaron to assist in the sacred duty of taking care of all utensils and furnishings of the Sanctuary under Aaron's supervision. A census of all the Levites is taken and the specific duties for each Levite clan are outlined.


### Key Lessons


**Serving in sacred offices - God's grace**


1️⃣ Serving in sacred offices - God's grace

⏩ The account of Nadab and Abihu (v 1- 4) is a serious warning to all in sacred offices to serve with awe and great reverence. (see also 1 Sam. 2:12-17).

#️⃣ The same Aaron who had caused the Israelites to worship an idol of the golden calf in the wilderness (Exodus 32:1-26) God graciously anointed him and his descendants to serve in the high office of priesthood.

#️⃣ Similarly, Israel, on his deathbed, cursed Levi and Simeon because of their fierce anger and cruelty  (Genesis 49:5-7) but God is calling his descendants to serve in the sanctuary under Aaron's supervision.

❗ This illustrates the great depth of God's love and grace towards all, including the vilest of offenders.

⏩ We must be as loving and as gracious to our erring brethren.

⏩ Besides, we must never write off people based on their past. Rather, we must view everyone in the same way that God views each one of us.

**Organisation. Hierarchy. Order.**


2️⃣ Organisation. Hierarchy. Order.

#️⃣ The Levites were wholly given to be under Aaron, the high priest (v9).

⏩ Just as the Levites were to fully cooperate with Aaron in their ministry in the Lord's Sanctuary, we must acknowledge God's sovereignty by cooperating with Christ who called us into His royal  priesthood with Him as our Most High Priest (1 Peter 2:9; Hebrews 8:1-6).

**Redemption in Egypt**


3️⃣ Redemption in Egypt

#️⃣ The Levites were wholly given to Aaron as redemption for all the  firstborns whom God had redeemed during the Passover in Egypt.

⏩ This must remind us all that we are wholly Christ's who redeemed us from the eternal condemnation of sin with His own blood on the Cross.

⏩ All who have been called by Christ from out of darkness into His  marvelous light, the Church, and live by faith, are enrolled in the books of heaven.

Conclusion

#️⃣ God delights in order. We  must always acknowledge that we are wholly Christ's because He redeemed us with with His blood.

⏩ Our calling into sacred commission of winning souls for God's Kingdom is a great privilege and as such we must discharge our duties  with great awe and reverence.

⏩ Familiarity must never be entertained.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our dear and loving God, we thank you for your endless love and grace which you generously lavish upon us, undeserving as we are.  Please give us the spirit to be just as loving and as gracious to all. In Jesus' name we pray.  Amen.

## Numbers 4


### Summary

God commands Moses to take a census of all Levite men aged between thirty and fifty from the clans of Kohath, Gershon and Merari. God also outlines the specific duties of each clan.  All the men who were counted for sacred temple duty were 8,580.


### Key Lessons


**Total member involvement**


1️⃣ Total member involvement

#️⃣ All able-bodied Levite men were enlisted for sacred duty.

⏩ This serves to remind the church today that no one must be left out in the Great Commission.

⏩ All Levite men who were called to serve in the tent of meeting were in the prime of their physical lives (vs 1,2,47).

⏩ We are each to present the very best of ourselves for God's mission in the prime of our lives.  (Ecclesiastes 12:1,2).

**The importance of Planning and Organisation**


2️⃣ The importance of Planning and Organisation

#️⃣ God commanded Moses to count all the men who were to serve in the work at the tent of meeting (v 2).

⏩ God is hereby teaching church leaders to adequately plan all work and to organise the people and resources accordingly. No haphazardness must be entertained (Luke 14:28).

⏩ Everyone must know and do his/her specific task but they, as one body, are to do so in harmony and in unison, NOT to compete with each other, as members of the body of Christ.(1 Corinthians 12).

⏩ There were some sacred tasks which only Aaron and his sons were commanded to perform. They could not delegate such sacred tasks to anyone else (vs 5-16).

⏩ By this, God is reminding all leaders to be diligent in discharging their work to serve as an example for the rest to emulate.

#️⃣ Aaron and his sons were to assign to each man his assignment (v 19).

⏩ Leaders still retain responsibility even over tasks which have been  delegated to  subordinates. Therefore, leaders must be diligent in their oversight duty.

**Give Reverence to God**


3️⃣ Give Reverence to God

#️⃣ The Levites were strictly forbidden from looking at or into the holy things in the tent of meeting. Their duty was strictly just carrying or transporting them.

⏩ Everyone must serve with great awe and reverence the One who called them into His service.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our God in heaven, thank you for the great privilege that you accord to us in allowing us, sinful as we are, to minister before you and our fellow men in Your Mission.  Give us the spirit of humility so that we will never be casual and careless as we discharge our assigned duties.  In Jesus' name we pray. Amen.

## Numbers 5


### Summary

In Numbers 5, God is commanding the Israelites to rid their camp of any thing or being that defiles (v 1-4). He also gives laws on restitution for wrongs perpetrated against others (v5-10). Finally, God gives laws pertaining to marital infidelity and suspicion thereof (vs 11-31).


### Key Lessons


**God desires that His people enjoy good health**


1️⃣  God desires that His people enjoy good health

#️⃣ By commanding the people to isolate those suspected of contagious diseases, God is encouraging good hygiene practices both at individual and community level.

⏩ We must purposely pray for and pursue physical, spiritual and social health (3 John 1:2).

⏩  We must strive to be our brothers' keepers by avoiding practices or behaviours which will expose those around us to health risks.

⏩ As members of the same body of Christ, we must gently and lovingly pull each other from falling off the slippery cliff into sin and unhealthy lifestyles or behaviours.

⏩ God is holy; and He desires that we also must be holy (1 Peter 1:15,16; Leviticus 19:2).

**Confession is never complete without Repentance**


2️⃣ Confession is never complete without Repentance

#️⃣ When we love God, we will love His people. And when we wrong one another, we wrong God. We can't claim that we love God when we hate others (v.6, 7; 1 John 4:20,21; Proverbs 17:5).

⏩  The words:"I am sorry," are not enough. We must translate those words into tangible actions, where possible, to try to alleviate the pain and loss that we have caused others to suffer (v 7,8).

⏩ True confession must heed the Lord Jesus Christ's command: "Go, but sin no more."  (John 8:11).

**God Greatly values Utmost Faithfulness in Marriages (v.11-31)**


3️⃣ God Greatly values Utmost Faithfulness in Marriages (v.11-31)

#️⃣ Marital infidelity is a sin against God, the One who instituted marriage (Genesis 2:26-28).

⏩ Couples must not only be faithful to each other but they must also live in a way that does not arouse any doubts about their faithfulness. (v 11-14).

**God never forgives unconfessed sin.**


4️⃣ God never forgives unconfessed sin.

#️⃣ A high priest made a "Reminder Offering" at the Lord's Sanctuary during a ceremony which investigated the unfaithfulness of a woman suspected of adultery (v 11-15).

⏩ The "Reminder Offering" was, and still is, a reminder to all mankind that God does not condone iniquity, neither does He forget it until it has been confessed. (Psalms 32: 1-5; Hosea 8:13).


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Lord our God, we thank you that You fearfully and wonderfully made us and that You desire that we enjoy great health. Give us the wisdom to be faithful stewards of these bodies of ours. Also help us to radiate Your love to those around us and beyond. In Jesus' name we pray.  Amen.

## Numbers 21


### Summary

The Lord God  allows venous serpents to terrorise the Israelites for their continued murmuring. But when they confess their error, He commands Moses to make a bronze serpent and mount it on a pole. A bitten person who looks at the bronze serpent would be healed and live. Israel's continued unmerited victories in their march.


### Key Lessons


**The Reality of the Great Controversy**


1️⃣ The Reality of the Great Controversy

#️⃣ Satan, just like the Moabites, is trembling at the triumphant Advent of the Lord Jesus Christ.  (Revelation 1:7; Isaiah 19:1).

⏩ And because he knows that he has but a little time left before his utter and final destruction, Satan is employing every strategem at his disposal, including  sorcery and deception, to entice many into apostasy. (1 Peter 5:8, 9).

⏩ Balaam's feeble response encouraged Balak to double his offer of gifts to entice the renegade prophet.

‼️ This is a serious warning to all believers that half-hearted rejection of Satan's overtures encourages him even more.

⏩ Our response to Satan must be a resolute: "Get behind me Satan."

**Spiritual Light Must Radiate Through Obedience**


2️⃣ Spiritual Light Must Radiate Through Obedience

#️⃣ Balaam knew God's will concerning the blessedness of Israel, but he acted contrary to the light.

⏩ Balaam's actions represent all christians who know the truth but do not live out the truth.

‼️ Faith without good works is dead faith (James 2:17,26).

⏩ We must not only be professors of faith. Rather, our faith must be manifested in our lives.

⏩Our love for Christ must be revealed through obedience (John 14:15).

**Fear Not**


3️⃣ Fear Not

#️⃣ Because Israel was blessed, no curse could prevail against them.

⏩ Because Christ promised to be with His church to end of the age, we should not fear for our lives as we go about on God's mission (Romans 8:31; 2 Tim. 1:7; Matthew 10:28; Luke 12:4,5).

**Don't be "a Prophet for Profit"**


4️⃣ Don't be "a Prophet for Profit"

#️⃣ Satan imperceptibly lured Balaam to indulge with covetousness until it took over Balaam's entire life.

‼️ We can't please God and the "god of money" at the same time (1 Timothy 6:10; Luke 16:12,13; Matthew 6:24).

⏩ We should strive to have a good ending to our spiritual pilgrimage, not like Balaam.

⏩ We should ever be vigilant against Satan's wiles (Ephesians 6:10-17).

‼️ Beware of the first step in the direction of sin.

⏩ "Balaam was once a good man and prophet of God; but he had apostatised, and had given in to covetousness.......; He (Balaam) loved wages of unrighteousness, which God declares to be idolatry, and had made him a time server, and through this one fault, Satan gained entire control of him. It was this that caused his ruin. The tempter is ever presenting worldly gain and honour to entice men from the service of God. He will tell them it is their over- consciousness that keeps them from propsperity. Thus, many are induced to venture out of the path of strict integrity. One wrong step makes the next easier, and they become more and more presumptuous."  (Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 461).


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father in heaven, thank you for your enduring love. Please give us the discernment to avoid the mistakes of the Israelites of old. In Jesus' name we pray. Amen.

## Numbers 25


### Summary

The Moabites resort into seducing Israelite men into sexual immorality and idolatry. Consequently, God is angered, and strikes Israel with a plague. God stops the plague - thanks to one man, Phineas, who single-handedly defended God's honour against daylight immorality and idolatry. But 24,000 lives had been lost when the plague stopped.


### Key Lessons


**Satan's Tenacity and Subtlety**


1️⃣ Satan's Tenacity and Subtlety

#️⃣ Moabites, just like Satan, never gave up on their evil scheme to separate Israel from God Almighty.

⏩ We must ever be vigilant in denying Satan and his minions a foothold, even the smallest inch, into our lives and ranks lest he devours us from within (1 Peter 1:13-15).

**Avoid Unhealthy Friendships.**


2️⃣ Avoid Unhealthy Friendships.

#️⃣ The Moabites concealed their scheme to lure the Israelites into idolatry under the guise of being friendly neighbours.

The people were thus, taught to adhere to the methods which infinite wisdom appointed for their security.  Even so, it is not enough that the sinner believe in Christ for the pardon of sin; he must, by faith and obedience, abide in Him. For if we sin wilfully after we have received the knowledge of the truth, there remaineth no more sacrifice for sins, but a certain fearful looking for judgment and fiery indignation which shall devour the adversaries." (Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 540, 541; also Romans 8:1, 34; Hebrews 6:18).


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Lord God thank You for the provision of salvation You graciously offer to all mankind that no person will be condemned to the eternal death.  Please help us to continually trust and abide in You till Your second coming.  In Jesus Christ's mighty name we pray. Amen.

## Numbers 36


### Summary

This chapter provides clarity to an earlier command which had been given in Numbers 27.  With instructions from God, Moses, now issues a law prohibiting daughters, who had inherited their father's estate, from marrying men from other tribes or clans to ensure that ancestral inheritance perpetually remained within the family, clan and tribe.  The concerned daughters followed this command.


### Key Lessons


**Seek DIVINE Insights**


1️⃣ Seek DIVINE Insights

#️⃣ Descendants of Joseph presented their petition to Moses. And Moses, in turn, sought wisdom from God on the fresh problem that the nation needed to address.

⏩ Leaders must emulate Moses' example not to trust on their own understanding, but to seek and to act on God's wisdom (v. 5; James 1:5-8).

⏩ Leaders must realise that: "no cap will fit all," as each problem or situation will require a unique approach and solution.

⏩ We must be respectful, not rude or a grumbling lot, when we  present our petitions before God or His servants (vv.1-4).

**Know and Treasure what is yours**


2️⃣ Know and Treasure what is yours

#️⃣ God's prohibition against inter-tribal marriages to prevent inter-tribal ancestral property transfers.

⏩ Responsible parents must be diligent at work and be good and faithful stewards who neither bequeath debts, nor  a degraded environment to their children BUT a decent inheritance to their children and  grandchildren (Proverbs 13:22)

⏩ The descendants of Joseph were able to present their inheritance claim because they knew about what truly belonged to them. So must the parents today do: to familiarise their children with Holy Scriptures and the promises contained therein so that they will be able to claim those promises.

**Eternal Salvation: Our Greatest Inheritance**


3️⃣ Eternal Salvation: Our Greatest Inheritance

#️⃣ Just like ancestral inheritance which had to remain within the family in perpetuity, we must jealously guard the greatest inheritance ever: eternal Salvation which was purchased for us by Christ with His own blood (1 Kings 21:1-4; 1 Peter 1:3-5; 23-25).

⏩ Thus, parents must daily live out their faith and hope in God's promises in order to pass a lasting impact of that faith to their children.

⏩ For this reason, we must keep away from anything or being which will seek to separate us from God from whom we obtain our eternal inheritance- not even marriage must be allowed to stand in the way of this inheritance.

**Follow God's Instructions**


4️⃣ Follow God's Instructions

#️⃣ The daughters of Zelophehad about whom this law was made, did as the Lord commanded Moses (v. 10).

⏩ Don't just inquire of the Lord, but commit to do what He tells you to do.

‼️ It is pointless to continually acquaint ourselves with God's truths without the heart to obey. Rather, let us strive be doers of the word (John 14:15).
