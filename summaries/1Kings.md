# 1 Kings

## Introduction

**INTRODUCTION**

**Title**
 The name "Kings" came from the original Hebrew title Melakim/Melechim meaning kings, taken from the first word in 1 Kings 1:1, Vehamelech, "Now king."
 Just like 1st and 2nd Samuel then 1st and 2nd Chronicles, similarly, 1st and 2nd Kings was one book in the Hebrew Bible which later got split into two in the 16th century AD.
 The Septuagint (The Greek Translation of the Hebrew Bible), may have divided these 3 books each into two possibly because Greek needed more scroll space than Hebrew.
 **Authorship**
The author is unknown but evidence favors the Talmud (A Jewish publication), which points to the prophet Jeremiah. Talmud, Baba Bathra, 15a
 **Date**
The two books of Kings cover a period of about 400 years from around 971 B.C. at the commencement of Solomon's reign to 562 B.C. when Jehoichin was released from prison (2 Kings 25:27-30).
Approximately, the two books were written around 646-570 B.C.
 **Historical Setting**
 This was a period when 3 superpowers were vying for dominance of the world, namely Assyria, Egypt and Babylon.
 Israel, having reached the Zenith of her power and prosperity during Solomon's era after his father David's successful reign, divided into 2 nations in 931 B.C. after Solomon's death.
From then on there were a series of many unfaithful kings who led the nations into idolatry with a few of them leading a righteous life. This eventually led both nations into exile, the Northern Israelite kingdom into the hands of Assyria and the Southern one under the Babylonians. 
 **Theme**
 Through the record of the kings and their reign, with most of them setting a very bad example of apostasy, yet the stories were told to prove God's faithfulness even amidst humanity's shortcomings. 
 God's purposes and plan of Salvation were worked out and fulfilled, regardless of the frailties of men's crooked history and failures.
 **Message**
 Many kings led people into sin, we too as leaders and laity alike can influence others to do good or evil in the sight of God.
 The 2 kingdoms of the Israelites reaped the sour consequences of their backsliding. We are not exempt from the same standard. We are bound to reap what we sow.

> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Heavenly Father, imbue us with the unction of the Holy Spirit as we delve in these studies for the salvation of our souls. Amen.

## 1 Kings 1


### Summary

King David is old and Adonijah plans a coup but it is quickly foiled.


### Key Lessons


**Importance of Unlearning Evil Habits**


David being old and failing to get enough warmth, his advisors sought a beautiful virgin to care for him and lie at his bosom. But David did not get intimate with her.

David having been weak in regard to women and amassing for himself many wives, at the end of his life, he seemed to have gained victory over this evil habit

We too can conquer our evil practices

**The Danger of pampered children**


Adonijah had never been rebuked by his father David in his lifetime (vs 6), and the sour fruits of such a course ripened in trying to carry out a coup de tat.

It's important that children be groomed in the fear of the Lord and disciplined according to the dictates of the Word of God, lest they turn out to be thorns and briers to all around them.

**The pain of Betrayal**


Close allies of David defected to Adonijah's side, namely Joab the army commander, and Abiathar the priest. Even though David had stated that Solomon was going to be king after him.

Some of those very close to us may betray us. The pain is greater when betrayal comes from within your circles.

**The Power of swiftness in correcting wrong**


With the Help on Nathan, Bathsheba, and Benaiah, David managed to arrange that Solomon the rightful heir be installed and anointed that same day as the king.

The efficiency and swiftness with which this remedial action was done made it easy to quell the coup.

A stitch in time saves nine. It is expedient to quickly address any rising anomalies before they take root.

**The Loyalty of the Outsiders**


It was those close to David who betrayed him, while those from heathen lands (the Cherethites and then the Pelethites) possibly from the Philistine tribes who had pledged loyalty to David and forsaken their gods in pursuit of the true God of David stood by him.

These 2 tribes formed David's royal body guard team. Is it not a shameful sight to have hypocritical close allies and faithful foreigners?


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father in heaven, help us to be loyal to you and to those around us. Forgive us for the betrayal that we have done to you and to our kindred, Amen.

## 1 Kings 2


### Summary

David's charge to Solomon. The death of David and the establishment of Solomon reign.


### Key Lessons


**1. David's Instructions**


The aging king urges his son and heir to keep God's commands if he was to prosper.

God's promise to David to always have his descendants reigning was conditional and dependent on complete obedience to His will.

We too, can forfeit our status of being heirs of the kingdom if we digress from God's Law.

It is a fallacy to believe that once saved always saved regardless of how we live.

2. Counsel on Judgements to be meted*

David counsels Solomon on those who were befitting punishments, namely Joab his army commander, whom he could not punish himself because of his own guilt in taking Uriah's wife and killing the husband with the help of Joab.

Joab had killed Abner and Amasa in cold blood even though they had been pardoned by the king and hence he had to be executed too.

Shimei had cursed the king in his times of distress, and although pardoned it was highly likely that given an opportunity he would seize it to oppose the king, since he was pro the house of Saul hence an avowed enemy of the house of David

Likewise the Lord will not hold guiltless anyone whose sins have not been forsaken, and He will surely recompense everyone according to their deeds.

3. A call to Social Intelligence*

Adonijah lacked wisdom in bringing his request which led to his death.

Requesting for Abishag, who had been given for wife to David, (though he did not become intimate with her), meant Adonijah still carried hopes of claiming the throne since he was older than Solomon even though he knew God had chosen his younger brother.

It was the custom then that the incoming king would take his predecessor's wives as a sign of his claim to the throne.

Solomon then executed Adonijah, and sensing that he had some advisors behind him, he also dealt with those who had backed him in his attempt in staging a coup.

He demoted Abiathar the priest and executed Joab.

All this could have been avoided had Adonijah used common sense and applied simple social intelligence.

We too need to be as wise as serpents and as harmless as doves

Shimei though not part of the coup, breached his vow and he met his demise.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** To our Heavenly Father, may you grant it that we live within your Word and Law and shew ourselves children of the eternal King, amen.

## 1 Kings 3 — Solomon's Wisdom

### Summary

Solomon's marriage alliance with Pharaoh, his request for wisdom, and the beginning of his fame.

### Key Lessons

**Marriages of Convenience**

Solomon makes an alliance with Pharaoh and seals it with a marriage to his daughter. Some believe the Pharaoh in question is Siamun, the powerful ruler of the 21st dynasty (ruling from 986 to 967 BC), while others believe it was his successor Psousennes II, the last king of the 21st dynasty (ruling from 967 to 943 BC), and a predecessor of Shishak (Sheshonk I), the founder of the 22nd Dynasty.

This marriage was couched in political expedience at the expense of a "thus says the Lord." Alliances with foreign nations had been prohibited, and more so inter-marriages with them.

In as much as the daughter of Pharaoh forsook her religion and followed the Jewish religion *(Prophets and Kings, pg 53)*, and the absence of rebuke for such an act, both did not sanctify Solomon's wrong act in this alliance and marriage.

Many are enjoined in marriages of convenience and are also unequally yoked with unbelievers (this includes those of different beliefs with us). Many try to soothe their troubled conscience by reasoning that at least their unbelieving spouse later became a believer. Yes, sometimes God picks up our mess and produces something good — but that does not sanitise the wrong we would have done in disregarding His Word.

**Twin Evils in Solomon's Life**

Solomon's marriage in this chapter appears to have been a polygamous one. Solomon was already married to Naamah the Ammonitess *(1 Kings 14:21)* before he became king. Solomon reigned for 40 years and his son with Naamah, Rehoboam, was 41 years old when he took over after Solomon's death — implying Rehoboam had been born while Solomon was still a prince.

His first wife was from foreign lands, and his other wife also came from idolatrous nations. These twin evils — polygamy and unequal yoking with unbelieving wives — later turned Solomon's heart away from God. May we flee from these evils.

**Solomon's Request**

Solomon requested wisdom and it was granted to him. His was a selfless request, and God showered him with added blessings he had not even asked for. Due to God's endowment of His gifts on Solomon, the fame of the king began to spread.

> 🙏 **Prayer:** Lord God in heaven, may we have the wisdom to discern between good and bad, and the power to choose that which is right. Amen.

## 1 Kings 5


### Summary

Solomon prepares to build the Jerusalem temple


### Key Lessons


**Dangers of Alliances with Foreign Nations**


Solomon made a treaty with King Hiram of Tyre who was his father's long time friend.

He requested timber and manpower to build the temple

The charges from Hiram made Solomon to raise taxes and labor force to work on the construction.

This brought a burden on people which they later pleaded with his son Rehoboam to lift upon their shoulders after the death of Solomon.

The refusal of the son to listen to the people, led the nation to be forever split into two kingdoms.

With all those raised taskforce and taxes, still there were shortages on the payments leading to a national debt to Hiram. Hence Solomon gave him 20 cities in the region of Galilee **(1 Kings 9;11).**

All this was the bitter result of union with unbelievers.

Notes on Solomon's error in relying on unbelievers*

*"Chosen men were specially endowed by God with skill and wisdom for the construction of the wilderness-tabernacle...See, the Lord hath called by name Bezaleel . . . of the tribe of Judah... and Aholiab . . . of the tribe of Dan. "Then wrought Bezaleel, . . . and every wise-hearted man, in whom the Lord put wisdom and understanding." Heavenly intelligences cooperated with the workmen whom God Himself chose."* **{Bible Commentary Volume 2, 1027.3}**

*"The descendants of these men inherited to a large degree the skill conferred upon their forefathers. In the tribes of Judah and of Dan there were men who were regarded as especially "cunning" in the finer arts. For a time these men remained humble and unselfish; but gradually, almost imperceptibly, they lost their hold upon God and His truth. They began to ask for higher wages because of their superior skill. In some instances their request was granted, but more often those asking higher wages found employment in the surrounding nations. In place of the noble spirit of self-sacrifice that had filled the hearts of their illustrious ancestors, they cherished a spirit of covetousness, of grasping for more and more. They served heathen kings with their God-given skill, and dishonored their Maker."* **{Bible Commentary Volume 2, 1027.4}**

*"The Phenician king Hiram responded by sending Huram, "a cunning man, endued with understanding, . . . the son of a woman of the daughters of Dan, and his father was a man of Tyre." This master workman, Huram, was a descendant, on his mother's side, of Aholiab, to whom, hundreds of years before, God had given special wisdom for the construction of the tabernacle. (1 Kings 7:13,14) Thus at the head of Solomon's company of workmen there was placed an unsanctified man, who demanded large wages because of his unusual skill.* **{Bible Commentary Volume 2, 1027.6}**

*"Huram's efforts were not prompted by a desire to render his highest service to God. He served the god of this world--Mammon. The very fibers of his being had been inwrought with principles of selfishness, which were revealed in his grasping for the highest wages. And gradually these wrong principles came to be cherished by his associates. *"The baleful influences set in operation by the employment of this man of a grasping spirit, permeated all branches of the Lord's service, and extended throughout Solomon's kingdom. The high wages demanded and received gave many an opportunity to indulge in luxury and extravagance. In the far-reaching effects of these influences, may be traced one of the principal causes of the terrible apostasy of him who once was the wisest of mortals. The king was not alone in his apostasy. Extravagance and corruption were to be seen on every hand. The poor were oppressed by the rich; the spirit of self-sacrifice in God's service was well nigh lost."* **{Bible Commentary Volume 2, 1028.1}.**

## 1 Kings 7


### Summary

Furnishings for the Temple and Solomon palace.


### Key Lessons


**The construction of the temple versus the palace**


The temple was finished in 7 years (1 Kings 6:37, 38) yet the palace took 13 years.

One of the causes of Solomon's later apostasy was this very fact that the palace took twice as much as the temple to build.

It would be advisable to pour in as much of our energies on God's work as in our personal pursuits.

. The influence of Huram the craftsman*

Huram was a son of an Israelite woman from Naphtali and a father from Tyre.

*"The Phenician king responded by sending Huram, "a cunning man, endued with understanding, . . . the son of a woman of the daughters of Dan, and his father was a man of Tyre." This master workman, Huram, was a descendant, on his mother's side, of Aholiab, to whom, hundreds of years before, God had given special wisdom for the construction of the tabernacle. Thus at the head of Solomon's company of workmen there was placed an unsanctified man, who demanded large wages because of his unusual skill."* **{2BC 1027.6}**

This unequal yoking of Huram's parents can in no wise be ignored in moulding of his negative character (his unsanctified and materialistic demeanor) which made him to overcharge for his work.

For those who are yet to get married, we would do well to take heed to the counsel we are given not to be unequally yoked with unbelievers

. The Magnificence of the Temple*

Solomon built a magnificent temple which was breath taking.

*"Of surpassing beauty and unrivaled splendor was the palatial building which Solomon and his associates erected for God and His worship. Garnished with precious stones, surrounded by spacious courts with magnificent approaches, and lined with carved cedar and burnished gold, the temple structure, with its broidered hangings and rich furnishings, was a fit emblem of the living church of God on earth, which through the ages has been building in accordance with the divine pattern...."* **{PK 36}**

We can learn from Solomon to do nothing but the best for God.

. The God of details*

God saw it fit that the Holy Writ include minute details of the construction and furnishings of the temple.

God is into details. He is very particular and we can't afford to be otherwise

God expects us to follow his Word and His instructions. Let none think that God just accepts anything.

The standard is so high and we need God's grace to fall in line.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father in heaven, empower us to start and finish erecting centers of worship. Amen

## 1 Kings 8


### Summary

Bringing of the ark and Dedication of the temple.


### Key Lessons


**The transfer of the Ark**


After the completion of the temple, the ark was transferred to the new building.

The Ark of Covenant contained the Ten Commandments, thus it's bringing into the temple was a gesture indicative of Israel welcoming her God with His Holy and lofty character as embedded in His Law

*"The ark was merely a receptacle for the tables of the law, and the presence of these divine precepts gave to [the ark] its value and sacredness. When the temple of God was opened in heaven, the ark of His testament was seen. Within the holy of holies, in the sanctuary in heaven, the divine law is sacredly enshrinedthe law that was spoken by God Himself amid the thunders of Sinai and written with His own finger on the tables of stone."* **(Ellen G. White: Great Controversy 433, 434)**

Similarly we are to invite God in our hearts.

Dedication of the temple*

The King led out in the dedication of the temple and offered innumerable sacrifices unto the Lord.

Solomon's dedicatory prayer was offered in great humility, awe and joy unto God

Leaders are to point people to God and to instill reverence to the Almighty King.

God's Forgiveness Solicited*

Solomon pleaded with God in prayer for forgiveness to those who would repent and offer their petitions to the Lord in this Temple.

This was an acknowledgement of the sinfulness of humanity and of God's long suffering and pardoning nature.

If we confess our sins, God is faithful and just to forgive us.

God's Glory fills the Temple*

As a token of acknowledgement, Jehovah showed His glory veiled in a cloud and filled the temple

The Lord accepts our efforts and service towards Him if rendered with humble, earnest and honest hearts.

*"It is impossible to describe the beauty and splendor of this sanctuary. Into this place the sacred ark was borne with solemn reverence by the priests, and set in its place beneath the wings of the two stately cherubim that stood upon the floor.* **(SDA Bible Commentary Volume 2, 1030.5)**

*The sacred choir lifted their voices in praise to God, and the melody of their voices was accompanied by all kinds of musical instruments. And while the courts of the temple resounded with praise, the cloud of God's glory took possession of the house, as it had formerly filled the wilderness tabernacle."* **(Ellen G. White: Review and Herald Nov. 9, 1905).** **(SDA Bible Commentary Volume 2, 1030.6 and 1030.8)**


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our gracious heavenly Father, we humbly bow before thy lofty throne in adoration and awe of your holiness and goodness. Fill us with your glory, Amen.

## 1 Kings 9


### Summary

God reappears to Solomon with reassurances and promises. Solomon's further developments.


### Key Lessons


**God appears to Solomon again**


The Lord once more spoke to Solomon reaffirming his promise to all the house of David and acknowledging the temple that had been built by Solomon.

If God can acknowledge a good work done, we too should commend and acknowledge anything good done for and to us.

The Conditional Nature of promises*

All the promises given to Solomon were to be fulfilled only on condition of obedience on the part of the Israelites.

The house of David would reign always only if they would follow God's law.

Some promises are conditional, and we will only realize them if we adhere to the principles and the Word of God.

Giving away one's inheritance*

As part of the payment for the  construction materials given by Hiram king of Tyre (cedar, cypress and gold: vs 10-13), Solomon parceled out to him 20 cities in the land of Galilee.

Under no circumstances was Solomon supposed to give the land away forever according to the Mosaic law **(Leviticus 25:23).**

Nevertheless due to his association with the unbelievers he ended up setting aside the code of conduct.

Providentially, Hiram was not pleased with those cities and it seems he did not take them, since Solomon later populated them with the Jews **(2 Chronicles 8:2).**

Our interactions with worldly company be it physically or through the media, will break down our spiritual guard and alertness.

Solomon's further Works*

Solomon went on to embark on more developments, building and strengthening cities.

All this he did after completing the temple. What a lesson to us all! To seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness, and all these other things will be added unto us.

Nevertheless, the levy taxed on all Israel and the labor force from the Canaanites which was used for all these developments were heavy on the people and they had to request for a reprieve to Solomon's son when he became king.

In our leadership let us not burden the people lest they suffer in our hands.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father in heaven, thank you for your acknowledgement of our efforts when we do good, help us to always be obedient unto Thee. Amen

## 1 Kings 11


### Summary

Solomon turns away from God


### Key Lessons


**The pitfalls of unequal yoking**


Solomon amassed women to himself, 1000 in number

He indulged in two sins, polygamy and unequal yoking (taking unbelieving women).

These foreign women led him astray

*"Many like the king of Israel, follow their own carnal desires, and enter into unsanctified marriages. Many who started out in life with as fair and promising a morning, in their limited sphere, as Solomon had in his exalted station, through one false and irrevocable step in the marriage relation, lose their souls, and draw others down to ruin with them. As Solomon's wives turned his heart away from God to idolatry, so do frivolous companions, who have no depth of principle, turn away the hearts of those who were once noble and true, to vanity, corrupting pleasures, and downright vice* **(Ellen G. White: Health Reformer, May, 1878; SDA Bible Commentary Volume 2 1031.3)**

God's Judgements on Solomon*

The Lord removed His protection over the king and allowed some adversaries to vex him all the days of his life.

This was a way that God used to awaken the king from the path of perdition.

Sometimes our misfortunes in life could be God's wake up up call for us to return to the paths of righteousness.

*"The lesson for us to learn from the history of this perverted life is the necessity of continual dependence upon the counsels of God; to carefully watch the tendency of our course, and to reform every habit calculated to draw us from God. It teaches us that great caution, watchfulness, and prayer are needed to keep undefiled the simplicity and purity of our faith. If we would rise to the highest moral excellence, and attain to the perfection of religious character, what discrimination should be used in the formation of friendships, and the choice of a companion for life!"* **(SDA Bible Commentary Volume 2 1031.2)**

The broken Covenant*

Solomon broke the Covenant, as a result, God reversed His promises.

The Kingdom would be torn and the larger portion be given to others other than the house of David.

We forfeit God's promised blessings whenever we disobey.

*"Why God Broke His Covenant With Solomon.--[1 Kings 11:4-6 quoted.] Solomon lost his connection with heaven, and set Israel an example so misleading that God could not vindicate him. God broke His covenant with Solomon because Solomon was disloyal. Had Solomon heeded the instruction given him, God would have worked through him to reveal to the world His power and majesty."* **(SDA Bible Commentary Volume 2 1032.2)**


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Forbid it Lord that we should wander away from the way of truth, keep us safe in Thine arms, Amen.

## 1 Kings 12


### Summary

Israel splits into 2 kingdoms


### Key Lessons


**Effects of wrong advice**


Rehoboam the son and successor to Solomon, chose to reject counsel from the elderly and followed bad advice from his peers which led to a permanent split of the kingdom

We need to be careful of those whom we allow to give us advices, lest we be led astray.

Importance of proper Parenting*

The mistakes of Rehoboam can be traced back to the indulgences, and pampering of his father.

Parents are called upon to model up their children for heaven and in the mean time for use here on earth.

*"Although Solomon had longed to prepare the mind of Rehoboam, his chosen successor, to meet with wisdom the crisis foretold by the prophet of God, he had never been able to exert a strong molding influence for good over the mind of his son, whose early training had been so grossly neglected. Rehoboam had received from his mother, an Ammonitess, the stamp of a vacillating character.... In the mistakes of Rehoboams life and in his final apostasy is revealed the fearful result of Solomons union with idolatrous women."* **(Ellen G. White: Prophets and Kings 88)**

Servant Leadership, a must*

Rehoboam chose the boss-subjects approach at the expense of the servant leadership one, and this costed him.

Had he spoken gently to the people and humbly hearkened to their requests, much harm would have been avoided.

Leaders are called upon to serve their followers and not to exert themselves on the people.

The danger of the popularity syndrome*

The nation having been divided into two, with Rehoboam only left with 2 tribes and Jeroboam with ten, came the most idolatrous practices.

Jeroboam wanting to cling to power and prevent any possible return of people to Jerusalem for worship lest they reunite, introduced new places of worship and a new priesthood and led the country into idolatry.

For the sake of expedience and popularity, Jeroboam turned his back on God.

May we not be found wanting as did Jeroboam.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father in heaven, teach us to follow wise and Biblically sound counsels and to follow them. Amen.

## 1 Kings 13


### Summary

A Prophecy against Israel by the man of God. The death of the man of God.


### Key Lessons


**Boldness in Mission**


The man of God fearlessly delivered unto him by God.

We are to boldly warn the world of her sins.

*"How firmly the man of God rebuked the king! And this firmness was essential; in no other way could the existing evils have been rebuked. The Lord gave His servant boldness, that an abiding impression might be made on those who heard. The messengers of the Lord are never to fear the face of man, but are to stand unflinchingly for the right. So long as they put their trust in God, they need not fear; for He who gives them their commission gives them also the assurance of His protecting care."* **(Ellen G. White: Prophets and Kings 105)**

The fatality of disobedience*

The man of God after delivering his God sent message, went ahead and disobeyed the Lord, leading to his death.

His death was a further warning to king and all Israel, yet the king and the people continued in their idolatry

## 1 Kings 15


### Summary

The evil reign of the kings of Judah and Israel.


### Key Lessons


**Parents' Influence over their children**


Abijam succeeded his father on the throne of Judah and he did evil just as his father Rehoboam had done.

The evil and idolatrous practices that he grew up witnessing from his parents, the same manifested in him as he began to reign.

What a great responsibility bestowed upon parents to model their children. More often than not, children emulate the lifestyles of their parents.

God's Tolerance*

For the sake of the promise God made to David, he just tolerated this evil king on the throne, of course he reigned only 3 years.

Sometimes God does things for us or through us for the integrity of His name and His mission and not because we are right before Him.

Sometimes God tolerates us for His name's sake and uses us in spite of ourselves. But may we all grab that opportunity to repent and not lull and deceive ourselves in equating His tolerance as His endorsement of our evil deeds.

Beware of partial reforms*

Asa reigned after his father Abijam's death.

Asa did that which was right in the eyes of God, unlike his father.

In as much as parents have great influence on their children, still everyone has freedom of choice.

One can choose to follow or reject the bad or good example set by parents.

None will have an excuse, therefore we can choose to break away from the bad practices of those around us as did Asa.

Nevertheless, instead of wholly relying on God for his security and protection from the wars he had with Israel, he decided to strip the temple treasuries and give them to Benhadad king of Syria in making a treaty with him.

Even though politically Asa seemed to have succeeded in stopping the invasions of Judah, yet he manifested great lack of faith, and the wealth dedicated to God was taken to an idolatrous king, what a shame.

God expects of us total reformation and transformation, not only partial reforms.

When brethren fight...*

How embarrassing that siblings (Judah and Israel) were fighting and worse still, both were inviting strangers to help them destroy each other.

Is this not our condition today, that we fight each other as families, relatives and church mates and invite strangers and onlookers to come help fight our own. God have mercy on us, and restrain us from such sordid perdition.

Baasha king of Israel had made a league with Benhadad king of Syria to destroy Judah, then Asa king of Judah takes God's treasuries in the temple and makes a treaty with the same king of Syria to break his ties with Baasha, so that he could help him fight back, and Benhadad did just that.

What a mess this whole story is, there is sibling rivalry, unfaithfulness and lack of faith in God from both kings of Judah and Israel, treachery (Benhadad betraying Baasha in favor of a new treaty with Asa), no wonder why the old cliche says politics is a dirty game.

### Summary

Elijah enters the scene to reprove the idolatrous course


### Key Lessons


**Drought Prophesied**


Elijah announces a drought before king Ahab for his apostasy and that of the nation

What a risky task was his to go before the king with such a message.

We are to courageously carry out our God given responsibilities

*"If the minister's face is not flint, if he has not indomitable faith and courage, if his heart is not made strong by constant communion with God, he will begin to shape his testimony to please the unsanctified ears and hearts of those whom he is addressing. Crying for the salvation of the sinners*

Elijah in witnessing the increasing depravity of Israel, daily was pained and longed for a change of the evil tide.

We are to grieve and mourn for the deepening wickedness rife and rampant all around us and be willing to warn the world.

How sad that nature hearkens to the voice of the Master and His children do not.

God has his children scattered everywhere and may we be the Elijahs sent to them with the life giving Word.

Secondly she received her son back to life again from death after Elijah's prayer

We are not to forget to be hospitable to strangers

## 1 Kings 18


### Summary

Elijah appears before Ahab. The showdown on Mount Carmel.


### Key Lessons


**The Encounter of Elijah and Obadiah**


One could question why Elijah did not appear to Ahab first, than going to Obadiah then instruct him to go call the king.

Elijah's name means Jehovah is my God and Obadiah means servant/worshipper of Yahweh. What befitting names to the 2 who stood for God in such a fallen environment.

The two's encounter help highlight the severity of the famine that the king had to leave stately duties and personally began to look for pastures.

Secondly, meeting Obadiah before Ahab helped in affirming and encouraging Obadiah's faithful part

Thirdly it would remind Elijah that he was not the only one still faithful in Israel.

Fourthly, it gave opportunity for Obadiah to pour out his fears and have them allayed and given assurance that Elijah would not disappear.

Ahab meets Elijah*

Ahab with all the sins he led Israel into, still had the audacity to point a blaming finger on the man of God Elijah.

Elijah did not passively accept the undue blame, but returned the real responsibility to where it belonged

It is a great virtue to acknowledge our sins and be accountable for our shortcomings, without trying to blame our failures on other people.

_"Rebels Blame Others for Troubles.--Those who refuse to receive reproof and to be corrected, will manifest enmity, malice, and hatred against the instrument that God has used. They will leave no means untried to cast stigma upon the one who bore to them the message. They will feel as did Ahab toward Elijah, that God's servant is the one who is the hindrance, the curse. Said Ahab, "Art thou he that troubleth Israel?" **(Ellen G. White: Review and Herald January 8, 1884: SDA Bible Commentary Volume 2, 1034.4)**

The Showdown at Mount Carmel*

Elijah inquires from Israel how long she would continue to falter between two opinions (following God vs Baal).

The word translated falter has the idea of one limping between two options yet without being fully satisfied with any.

It bespeaks of a double minded person

_"The people answer him not a word. Not one in that vast assembly dare reveal loyalty to Jehovah... Each departure from right doing, each refusal to repent, had deepened their guilt and driven them farther from Heaven. And now, in this crisis, they persisted in refusing to take their stand for God. The Lord abhors indifference and disloyalty in a time of crisis in His work." **(Ellen G. White: Prophets and Kings 147, 148)**

It is interesting to note that the same Hebrew word (pasakh), used for the false prophets leaping the dancing as they called for Baal to rain fire on the sacrifice was used by Elijah as he asked how long Israel would limp (falter) between two opinions.

Hence their dance (limping) prefigured their condition of hoping from one point to another and none of the two materialized.

Baal was believed to be the god of rain and thunder, yet he failed to bring rain during the famine and similarly could not respond by fire on the altar.

There is no other God than Creator of the universe.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, we choose you today and forsake all other false gods. Amen.

## 1 Kings 19

Elijah flees from Jezebel and is recommissioned by God at Mount Horeb.

**When faith gives way to fear**


The great man of faith, in the face of Jezebel's threat of death, chickened out and lost his faith due to fear and fled away.

He should have told the messenger that the God in whom he trusted would protect him against the hatred of the queen. Only a few hours had passed since he had witnessed a wonderful manifestation of divine power, and this should have given him assurance that he would not now be forsaken. . Encouraging the discouraged*

Out of despair, Elijah wished for death, but an angel ministered to him.

God understands us when we are at our lowest and picks us up

May we likewise lend a helping hand and a listening ear to the crushed and the hopeless and encourage them along the way.

. Where are you?*

When Adam sinned and hid, God called him and asked him where he was. When Cain murdered his brother Abel, God asked where Abel was, and He is asking Elijah the runaway prophet where he was.

God is still calling each one of us. Will you hearken to his voice and return to your path of duty.

Elijah claims to be the only one left at Mount Carmel, even though Obadiah had told him he had hidden 100 prophets. Now again twice Elijah bemoans that he is the only one left.

He desired to teach Elijah that it is not always the work that makes the greatest demonstration that is most successful in accomplishing His purpose. While Elijah waited for the revelation of the Lord, a tempest rolled, the lightnings flashed, and a devouring fire swept by; but God was not in all this. Then there came a still, small voice, and the prophet covered his head before the presence of the Lord. His petulance was silenced, his spirit softened and subdued. He now knew that a quiet trust, a firm reliance on God, would ever find for him a present help in time of need. It is not always the most learned presentation of God's truth that convicts and converts the soul. Not by eloquence or logic are men's hearts reached, but by the sweet influences of the Holy Spirit, which operate quietly yet surely in transforming and developing character."* **{PK 168, 169}.**
