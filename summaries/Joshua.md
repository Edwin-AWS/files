# Joshua

## Introduction

🆕 Book* Joshua
  
Joshua Introductory Remarks
 The book of Joshua is pivotal to the historical narrative of the Journey of the Children of Israel. The fulfilment of the Promised Land which was first promised to Abraham is penned in this book. 

✍🏽 Name and Authorship
The book named Joshua is believed to have been authored around 1451 BC. The Author is unknown.  Scholars do not converge on who the author of this book was. While it is a collection of the works of Joshua, it may not be so that he was the author.

Main Character
The main Character in the book is the leader Joshua meaning God is Salvation. His original name was Hoshea which means salvation. Joshua was prepared as far as the slavery period under the mentorship of Moses. Overall, we see a leader who starts with God, depends on God, is guided by the book of law, and who ultimately ends with God. 
One cannot read the narrative and not fast forward and meditate on the eschatological Canaan as the Promised Land in Christ as the believers Joshua. 

🤝 Main Themes
#️⃣ It is in this book where:
(i) we experience faith and grace in the story and the salvation of Rahab. 
(ii) The the sin of Achan highlights the aspect of disobedience with the defeat of Israelites.
(iii) We find the narrative of the sun standing still in order for battles to be conquered. 
(iv) We find stories of victory and the dramatic falling of the walls of Jericho.
(v) The land distribution is outlined, including the setting up of Cities of refuge.
(vi) Joshua makes a call to serve the Lord. 
(vii) God’s people were duped by the Gibeonites, which was huge mistake as the Israelites entered into a treaty without consulting God.

🏹 Thrust
My focus on the summaries of the book is to provide practical applications to the contemporary believer. We pray that the Lord may make the book of Joshua alive and applicable to our lives today.

🛐 Prayer
Lord open our hearts that we see you in the narrative of Joshua. Amen.

#️⃣ The Great Controversy is real. The record of Joshua’s warfare demonstrates that Spiritual Israel is ever in warfare and the enemies are plenty.

We go not in the force of the flesh nor the pride of technology but in the force of the Holy Spirit. When you are on God’s side, there is no weapon nor ammunition formed against you that will prosper.

#️⃣ Numbers are not synonymous with victory. The side you belong to determines your victory. Those who belong to God are in the winning team and if God be on our side, who can be against us (Romans 8 v 31). Greater is He that is with us than he that is with the world.

#️⃣ Just as the kings, their armies and their territory were completely destroyed, this must remind the reader that a day of the complete annihilation of sin is going to come.

#️⃣ Your giants are weakened by God ahead of time (v 22). The giants from the Anaks who were a threat to the Children of Israel were conquered. God has a way of dealing with our potential threats ahead of time.

#️⃣ Some battles will be long (v 18). It took Joshua a long period waging war. Equally, some battles with cancer, disease and sin will take longer times. Faith alone and patience grounded firmly in God’s trust shall be our hope and wedge to victory.

#️⃣ Zelophehad had no sons but daughters, and they also had their portion in the inheritance.

**All people matter**


2️⃣ All people matter

#️⃣ The inclusion of women in inheritance is an object lesson to God’s church never to protect the undermined, the weak and overlooked.

⏩ Everyone has a place in God’s Kingdom and no one is segregated in the ultimate plan of redemption.

**Partial obedience leads to future trap**


3️⃣ Partial obedience leads to future trap

#️⃣ It is probable that the iron chariots intimidated them in clearing the Canaanites. In like manner, many of us have partial occupation of our blessing because of fear.

⏩ No matter how the enemy may project himself, we must never be intimidated. We must hold on to God’s promises, and move forward in faith.

**Clear the forest in your lives and occupy more territory**


4️⃣ Clear the forest in your lives and occupy more territory

#️⃣ The response of Joshua to a complaint by the descendants of Joseph has great object lessons.

**Take advantage of you numbers**


5️⃣ Take advantage of you numbers

#️⃣ Joshua reminds them that there is power in numbers, and that principle is true even today. An African proverb says, if you want to go far, go together.

⏩ There is so much we can do if we would in mission we if we could only take advantage of our numbers.

## Joshua 21


### Key Lessons


**Ask and it shall be given, Seek and you shall find (Matthew 7:7)**

#️⃣ Just as the Levites asked for their portion and claimed it, God’s children must hinge on prayer and hang on to God’s promises of providence.

**When we approach God we must not be afraid.**


2️⃣ When we approach God we must not be afraid.

#️⃣ In approaching Eleazar, the Levites were not afraid in putting their claim, because they too were children of Israel and thus partakers of the inheritance.

⏩ Has God not promised that to them who believe, they shall be sons and daughters of God (John 1 v 12).

**Being God’s Ministers does mean you must suffer.**


3️⃣ Being God’s Ministers does mean you must suffer.

#️⃣ The Levites who were ministers at the tabernacle were given 48 cities, and their grazing lands.

⏩ Such allocation implied that in God’s promises, there were to have descent accommodation in the cities, and a place to grow their wealth in cattle or sheep in the grazing land.

⏩ Those who serve in the temple too must also enjoy their allotted portion as prescribed by God.

**The portion of the Levites was given fro the Israelites share (v 3).**


4️⃣ The portion of the Levites was given fro the Israelites share (v 3).

#️⃣ First, the Israelites were given, and they too were to give back part back to ministers in the temple.

⏩ God has ordained a system by which His ministers must be supported. Through tithes and offerings, the nakedness of His ministers is to be covered.

5️⃣ God expects us to give back part of what we are given

#️⃣ God expected the Israelites to give back what He (God), had blessed them. Equally, providence precedes obedience. God gives and he asks you to obey in giving back.

⏩ God is expecting you just to give part, not all. Israel was asked to give was just a portion of their blessing.

⏩ When God asks us to support his mission by a tithe as a portion of His providence, God is not being greedy and unreasonable.
