# Deuteronomy

## Introduction

🆕 Book* Deuteronomy 
  
INTRODUCING DEUTERONOMY
In Deuteronomy, the last book of the Pentateuch, Moses repeated the law in a series of sermons given before his imminent death. The old generation had perished in 40 years of wandering in the wilderness and the new generation needed reorientation and empowerment to possess God's promises.

Name
#️⃣ The name of the book is derived from its content since the author didn't supply a name. In Hebrew the book is titled Haddebharim - 'The Words' from the opening phrase (1:1). It is also titled Mishneh Hattorah - 'Repetition of the Law' implied in a thematic phrase (17:18). The English title is derived from Greek 'deuteros' meaning -second-  and 'nomos' meaning - law.

✍🏽 Author
The book identifies Moses as its author in various passages (1:1-5; 4:44-46; 29:1; 31:9; 31:24-26). Other Bible books also confirm Moses as the author Joshua 1:7-8; Judges 3:4; Malachi 4:4; Acts 3:22; Romans 10:19 including Jesus Christ (Matthew 19: 8-9; Mark 7:10). 

🤝 Main Themes and Sections
#️⃣ The book can be divided into 7 sections with the following themes:-

a) Recollection of the Exodus to the present location (1 to 4).

b) Repetition of the Decalogue (5-11).

c) Retracing expectations of worship and practical living in Canaan (12-26) 

d) Recital of conditional promise - life on obedience and death on disobedience to God's law (27-30).

e) Appointment and commissioning of Joshua as a successor (31).

f) Moses's songs of blessing to the nation (32-33).

g) Supplementary account of Moses's unique death (34).

🗝️ Nuggets from Deuteronomy

#️⃣ The book has valuable nuggets such as:-

1. God demonstrated love for the family by giving 'military leave' to newlyweds (Deuteronomy 24:5).

2. Exclusive ‘Love of God’ is at the core of worship (Deuteronomy 6:4-6 and Matthew 22:37).

3. We are given weapons to fight Satan - Jesus used 3 quotations from Deuteronomy to fight the tempter See Matthew 4:4 (Deuteronomy 8:3); Matthew 4:7 (Deuteronomy 6:16); Matthew 4:10  (Deuteronomy. 6:13).

4. We are given prophecies pointing to Jesus Compare (Deuteronomy 18:15 with Acts 3:22)

5. The truth must be taught diligently and faithfully to the next generation (See Deuteronomy 6:5)

6. We must choose God’s ways and enjoy eternal life (See Deuteronomy 30:19.)

7. Wealth and prosperity come from God – (Deuteronomy 8:18).

‼️ Key Message
Moses faithfully records God’s faithfulness and men’s appalling unfaithfulness in their covenant relationship. Moses, who talked to God face to face, closes his legacy by preparing the next generation for the bliss he couldn’t enjoy.

⏩ The book of Deuteronomy should be carefully studied by those living on the earth today. It contains a record of the instruction given to Moses to give to the children of Israel. In it the law is repeated.... [SDA Bible Commentary Vol. 1, 1117.5]

Have mercy on us and give us your Spirit so we can better serve you. Prepare us -your remnant- to enter heavenly Canaan, we pray in the name of Jesus Christ. Amen

## Deuteronomy 1 — The History of Israel


### Summary

Moses assembled Israel after 40 years of wandering and prepares them to enter Canaan. He recited their rebellion and ungrateful behaviours which were met with God’s punishment.


### Key Lessons


**God’s Promises to Israel at Sinai (1:1-8).**


1️⃣ God’s Promises to Israel at Sinai (1:1-8).

#️⃣ God did not want Israel to wander indefinitely in the wilderness. God’s punishment comes with time limits. We must learn to give seasons of repentance and reconciliation.

**The Appointment of Judges (1:9-18).**


2️⃣ The Appointment of Judges (1:9-18).

#️⃣ Leaders must learn to delegate duties to faithful juniors.

⏩ Church responsibilities must be shared with Elders and Deacons who are grounded in the faith (1 Timothy 3:1-13).

**Spies Sent out to Canaan (1:19-33).**


3️⃣ Spies Sent out to Canaan (1:19-33).

#️⃣ The spies were sent to motivate the nation to occupy the promised land but they demotivated the nation.

⏩ We must encourage each other on the way to heaven and not spread sentiments of unbelief (Hebrews 4:1).

**God punishes Unbelief (1:34-46)**


4️⃣ God punishes Unbelief (1:34-46)

#️⃣ The generation that discouraged Israel were punished in the wilderness and never entered the promised land. If we are happy with the pitiful rewards on earth God will not get us into heaven.

⏩ Believers who emulate Joshua and Caleb are the right candidates of inheriting heavenly promises.

⏩ "The Lord God of heaven is our Leader. He is a leader whom we can safely follow; for He never makes a mistake. Let us honour God and His Son Jesus Christ, through whom He communicates with the world. It was Christ who gave to Moses the instruction that He gave to the children of Israel. It was Christ who delivered the Israelites from Egyptian bondage. Moses and Aaron were the visible leaders of the people. To Moses instruction was given by their invisible Leader, to be repeated to them". [SDA Bible Commentary Vol 1. p 1117.9]


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father in heaven, we thank you for guiding us through this earthly wilderness. Prepare us -your remnant- to enter heavenly Canaan, we pray in the name of Jesus Christ. Amen

## Deuteronomy 2 — WANDERING IN THE WILDERNESS


### Summary

God was with Israel when they were wandering in the wilderness. God directed Israel to avoid war with the descendants of Esau and Moab because they were their close relatives. When Sihon king of Heshbon confronted Israel they were destroyed in battle.


### Key Lessons


**Avoiding Confrontation with Edomites (2:1-15).**


1️⃣ Avoiding Confrontation with Edomites (2:1-15).

#️⃣ God led Israel on a long route so they could avoid confrontation with Edom. We must strive to maintain peace with our neighbours and relatives (1 Peter 3:11).

**Avoiding Confrontation with Moabites (2:16-25).**


2️⃣ Avoiding Confrontation with Moabites (2:16-25).

#️⃣ Israel were directed to compass the descendants of Lot because they were their relatives. Family relationships must be protected and we must take care of their needs (1 Timothy 5:8).

**Destruction of Sihon (2:26-37).**


3️⃣ Destruction of Sihon (2:26-37).

#️⃣ Sihon were given the hand of peace but obstinately confronted Israel.  The nation was destroyed because of their evil attitude towards God’s people.

⏩ Those who will perish in hell-fire are destroyed because they refused to make peace with Jesus (Isaiah 27:5).

⏩ "Everyone who seeks to follow the path of duty will at times be assailed by doubt and unbelief. The way will sometimes be so barred by obstacles, apparently insurmountable, as to dishearten those who will yield to discouragement; but God is saying to such, Go forward. Do your duty at any cost. The difficulties that seem so formidable, that fill your soul with dread, will vanish as you move forward in the path of obedience, humbly trusting in God". [Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets  437.2]


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father in heaven, guide us as we face obstacles on our journey to heaven. Prepare us -your remnant- to enter heavenly Canaan, we pray in the name of Jesus Christ. Amen

## Deuteronomy 3 — Victories recited


### Summary

The encounter with Og was a sweet song of victory. The settlement of the tribe of Reuben, Gad and the half tribe of Manasseh brought encouragement to those waiting for their turn. Moses closes the chapter with the sad news of his unanswered prayer request to see Canaan.


### Key Lessons


**Defeat of Og (3:1-11)**


1️⃣ Defeat of Og (3:1-11)

#️⃣ Og like Bashan had an obstinate heart and confronted the pilgrims.

⏩ We are not to fear our enemies when we are doing God's business (Isaiah 41:10).

⏩ God will give us victory through our Lord Jesus Christ (1 Corinthians 15:57-58).

**Settlement of two-and-half tribes (3:12-22)**


2️⃣ Settlement of two-and-half tribes (3:12-22)

#️⃣ Reubenites and Gadites occupied the vacant taken from the enemy.

⏩ We are to take possession of the what God has given us (Acts 7:45).

⏩ Soldiers from the resettled tribes were to accompany their brethren into all battles on the way to full resettlement. We are not to relax but like Paul fight the enemy to the bitter end (2 Timothy 4:7-8).

**Moses's unanswered prayer (3:23-29).**


3️⃣ Moses's unanswered prayer (3:23-29).

#️⃣ Moses wanted to enter Canaan too and prayed that God may reverse His punishment. God refused to entertain the prayer and Moses was content with God’s answer.

⏩ God is our Master and retains the right to say 'yes', 'wait' or 'no' to our prayer requests. Whatever answer we are given we must be contend and grateful (1 Timothy 6:6).

‼️ Not mighty giants nor walled cities, armed hosts nor rocky fortresses, could stand before the Captain of the Lord’s host. The Lord led the army; the Lord discomfited the enemy; the Lord conquered in behalf of Israel. The giant king and his army were destroyed, and the Israelites soon took possession of the whole country. Thus was blotted from the earth that strange people who had given themselves up to iniquity and abominable idolatry. [Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets 436.2]


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father in heaven, remind of the mighty victories we have experienced in personal growth. May our victory empower us to fight the onslaught of demonic powers blocking the way to heaven. Amen

## Deuteronomy 4 — Handling the Law


### Summary

Moses gives a passionate appeal to Israel to love and respect God's Law. The nation was privileged to have a personal visit from God. They were to respond by jealously guarding the Law from the possibility of future forgetfulness and apostasy.


### Key Lessons


**Moses's exhortation to obedience (4:1-8).**


1️⃣ Moses's exhortation to obedience (4:1-8).

#️⃣ The instructions given under divine inspiration were to be carefully preserved for future generations.

⏩ We are not to add or subtract from God's Word lest we risk being deleted from the book of life (Revelation 22:18-19).

⏩ Keeping God's law is the secret of our wisdom and survival among heathens.

**Reminder of theophany at Sinai (4:10-14).**


2️⃣ Reminder of theophany at Sinai (4:10-14).

#️⃣ The day God met Israel individually and collectively on Mount Sinai was to be remembered.

⏩ When we joyfully keep God's law we maintain unique connections with God’s goodness and holiness (Romans 7:12).

**Warning against Idolatry (4:15-40).**


3️⃣ Warning against Idolatry (4:15-40).

#️⃣ Israel were not to be enticed to copy the worship of visible deities worshipped by other idolatrous nations.

⏩ We are to avoid idolatry which will bar us from heavenly Canaan (1 John 5:21).

**Appointment of Cities of Refuge (4:41-43).**


4️⃣ Appointment of Cities of Refuge (4:41-43).

#️⃣ Bezer, Ramoth-Gilead and Golan-Bashan were set up as cities of refuge for the two-and-half tribes who settled before crossing Jordan.

⏩ Jesus is our refuge in times of trouble (Psalm 9:9).

**Moses recounts the Law (4:44-49).**


5️⃣ Moses recounts the Law (4:44-49).

#️⃣ Emphasis is given to laws, testimonies and statutes given by Moses before they crossed Jordan.

⏩ Leaders are needed who instruct and exhort converts with fatherly care (1 Thessalonians 2:11).

‼️ Moses stood before the people to repeat his last warnings and admonitions. His face was illumined with a holy light. His hair was white with age; but his form was erect, his countenance expressed the unabated vigor of health, and his eye was clear and undimmed. It was an important occasion, and with deep feeling he portrayed the love and mercy of their Almighty Protector (Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets 463.3)


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father in heaven, give us your Spirit so we are constantly reminded of your requirements as journey to heavenly Canaan. We pray in the name of Jesus Christ our Lord and Saviour. Amen

## Deuteronomy 5 — Recital of Sinai


### Summary

Moses reminded Israel about their experiences on Mount Sinai.  He wanted Israel to remember God's Law especially the younger generation born in the wilderness.


### Key Lessons


**Recital of the Decalogue (5:1-21).**


1️⃣ Recital of the Decalogue (5:1-21).

#️⃣ Moses called all Israel and repeated the Decalogue as given by God on Mount Sinai.

⏩ God’s Law is to be faithfully heard, put into personal practice and taught to others (Ezra 7:10).

⏩ Moses reminded Israel to keep the Sabbath holy. He recited the  commandment and linked Sabbath observance with liberation from Egyptian bondage (5:15).

✍🏽 'No other institution which was committed to the Jews tended so fully to distinguish them from surrounding nations as did the Sabbath. God designed that its observance should designate them as His worshipers. It was to be a token of their separation from idolatry, and their connection with the true God. But in order to keep the Sabbath holy, men must themselves be holy. Through faith they must become partakers of the righteousness of Christ. ... [Ellen G. White: Desire of Ages 283.3]

**Recital of the response of Israel (5:22-33).**


2️⃣ Recital of the response of Israel (5:22-33).

#️⃣ Israel greatly feared and quacked at Sinai and requested Moses to be their mediator. We must endure the manifestations of God's presence lest we fear to enter heaven where there is greater thunder than at Sinai (Hebrews 12:18-22).

‼️ God accompanied the proclamation of His law with exhibitions of His power and glory, that His people might never forget the scene, and that they might be impressed with profound veneration for the Author of the law, the Creator of heaven and earth. He would also show to all men the sacredness, the importance, and the permanence of His law. [Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets 309.6]


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father in heaven, put away the fear in our hearts so we can stand in your presence. Teach us to love your Law and help us teach others it's requirements.  We pray in the name of Jesus Christ our Lord and Saviour. Amen

## Deuteronomy 6 — Foundations of Faith


### Summary

The chapter forms the fundamental declaration of the Hebraic faith and is repeated every morning and evening at prayer time (Shema). Jesus quoted the chapter and confirmed that our 1st duty is to love God with all our heart, soul and strength (Deuteronomy 6:5 and Matthew 22:37). Moses declared that love for God was exclusive and no other deities can be accommodated.


### Key Lessons


**The Greatest Commandment (6:1-9).**


1️⃣ The Greatest Commandment (6:1-9).

#️⃣ Moses taught Israel to be totally dedicated to God. Total dedication is an indication of undivided love.

⏩ The first 4 commandments were written on the 1st tablet of the Decalogue and teach us to love God wholeheartedly (Mark 12:28-30).

**Keeping the monotheistic worship pure (6:10-25).**


2️⃣ Keeping the monotheistic worship pure (6:10-25).

#️⃣ Israelites were to fear God and worship Him only. Jesus dismissed Satan on the mount of temptations by demonstrating undivided obedience to God (6:13 & Matthew 4:10).

⏩ Israelites were to diligently keep God's commandments and resist acting presumptuously.

⏩ Jesus refused to sin presumptuously on the Mount of Temptation (6:16 & Matthew 4:7).

✍🏽 The law of God requires that man shall love God supremely, and his neighbor as himself. When through the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ, this is perfectly done, we shall be complete in Christ. [SDA Bible Commentary Vol 5, 1097.8]


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father in heaven, give us your Spirit so we can love you with undivided hearts. Forgive us our sins, and help us grow in love daily. We pray in the name of Jesus Christ.  Amen.

## Deuteronomy 7 — Obedience motivated by love


### Summary

Moses pleads with Israel not to frustrate God’s love by entering into intimate relationships with the inhabitants of Canaan. The heathen nations were to be destroyed including their worship systems. The removal of obstinate cultures would preserve pure worship of Jehovah.


### Key Lessons


**Destruction of 7 evil nations required (7:1-5).**


1️⃣ Destruction of 7 evil nations required (7:1-5).

#️⃣ God wanted Israel to completely destroy 7 nations inhabiting  Canaan. The evil practices of these nations had been noted from the days of Abraham and for more than 400 years no change in practices had happened (Genesis 15:19-21).

⏩ We are to quickly repent and not abuse God’s long-suffering mercies (Romans 2:4).

⏩ Intermarriages with idolatrous nations were strictly forbidden (v 3). We are not to be unequally yoked with unbelievers (2 Corinthians 6:14-17).

**Maintaining special privileges (7:6-11).**


2️⃣ Maintaining special privileges (7:6-11).

#️⃣ Israel's priviledged status was a product of God’s love - He left giants and picked up a poor downtrodden enslaved people. We are poor and worthless outside God’s grace ( Ephesians 2:8-10).

**The blessings on Obedience (7:12-26).**


3️⃣ The blessings on Obedience (7:12-26).

#️⃣ God’s covenant relationship with Israel was conditional on Israel's obedience. If we truly love God we will keep God's commandments (John 14:15).

⏩ God’s commandments are not grievous (1 John 5:3).


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father in heaven, give us your Spirit so we are constantly reminded of your requirements as we journey to heavenly Canaan. We pray in the name of Jesus Christ our Lord and Saviour. Amen

## Deuteronomy 8 — Chastened for a purpose


### Summary

After their exodus from Egypt, Israel faced hardships in the wilderness for 40 years (v 2). The hardships were God’s tools in refining their characters. We see Christ faced similar hardships on earth;  taking refuge in Egypt  (Matthew 2:13)...  being called out of Egypt  ... (Matthew 2:15)  ... spending 40 days in the wilderness (Matthew 4:1). Christ unlike Israel overcame Satan because He fed on the Word of God (Matthew 4:4). We too must overcome Satan in our terrible wildernesses. We must develop symmetrical characters that will soon fellowship with angels (Zechariah 3:7).


### Key Lessons


**Israel sustained from heaven (8:1-5).**


1️⃣ Israel sustained from heaven (8:1-5).

#️⃣ God raised a generation in the wilderness that would enter Canaan.  ⏩ God is now in this earthly wilderness preparing a generation that will enter heaven.

⏩ The environment in the wilderness was hard with the only sustenance coming from heaven. Our sustenance comes from heaven and is found in God's word (Matthew 4:4).

**The glories of Canaan (8:6-10).**


2️⃣ The glories of Canaan (8:6-10).

#️⃣ Israel were constantly reminded that they were going to a glorious land. We too must constantly be reminded of the unimaginable glories ahead (1 Corinthians 2:9).

**Warning against Forgetting the Lord (8:11-20).**


3️⃣ Warning against Forgetting the Lord (8:11-20).

#️⃣ Israel were admonished not to forget God on arrival in Canaan. They were to remember the trials on the way for they were God’s agencies in character building.

⏩ We are to remember the afflictions which have refined our characters (Isaiah 48:10).

⏩ God gives us wealth and it is important that we retain a faithful tithe into His treasury (Malachi 3:10).

✍🏽 In reviewing our past history, having traveled over every step of advance to our present standing, I can say, Praise God! As I see what the Lord has wrought, I am filled with astonishment, and with confidence in Christ as leader. We have nothing to fear for the future, except as we shall forget the way the Lord has led us, and His teaching in our past history. [Ellen G. White: Life Sketches 196.2]


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father in heaven, we thank you for the afflictions which you allow to shape our characters. We pray for continued Christian growth in the name of Jesus Christ. Amen

Israel did not deserve God's mercies but were redeemed because God loved them. Israel prospered because of God's covenant with their ancestors - Abraham,  Isaac and Jacob. We also benefit from God’s blessings given through Jesus Christ. (John 14:13).

**God leads in warfare (9:1-5).**


1️⃣ God leads in warfare (9:1-5).

#️⃣ There were giants on the way, but God would lead in battles to displace the enemy.

⏩ We face a barrage of demonic powers on our way. If we stand in Christ's righteousness we will destroy formidable forces of evil. (Ephesians 6:10-13).

**Recital of apostasy at Sinai (9:6-24).**


2️⃣ Recital of apostasy at Sinai (9:6-24).

#️⃣ Israel had an appalling history of apostasy we must never emulate. They forgot God's commandments while they were still camped at the base of Mount Sinai.  They made a golden calf that provoked God's anger.

⏩ We should not repeat Israel's apostasy and ingratitude (1 Corinthians 10:1-6).

**Recital of Intercessory prayers on Sinai (9:25-29).**


3️⃣ Recital of Intercessory prayers on Sinai (9:25-29).

#️⃣ Moses recited his prayers for Israel so they would learn how to approach God.

⏩ Jesus left us a record of prayer so we too may know how to approach God (Luke 11:1-4).

✍🏽 All true obedience comes from the heart. It was heart work with Christ. And if we consent, He will so identify Himself with our thoughts and aims, so blend our hearts and minds into conformity to His will, that when obeying Him we shall be but carrying out our own impulses. The will, refined and sanctified, will find its highest delight in doing His service. When we know God as it is our privilege to know Him, our life will be a life of continual obedience. Through an appreciation of the character of Christ, through communion with God, sin will become hateful to us. [Ellen G. White: Desire of Ages 668.3]

God restored His relationship with Israel and rewrote the Decalogue. Our restoration to God’s favour does not change the law but our hearts.

**Second set of Tablets (10:1-11).**


1️⃣ Second set of Tablets (10:1-11).

#️⃣ God spoke His commandments to the congregation, wrote them on the 1st set of tables of stone that Moses broke and repeated them word for word on the 2nd set.

‼️ God is not a man that changes his laws willy-nilly (Numbers 23:19).

**Heart surgery (10:12-22).**


2️⃣ Heart surgery (10:12-22).

#️⃣ God wanted Israel to worship Him with all their hearts. Their hearts were to be circumcised and softened.

⏩ We are to pray for a change of hearts so we can faithfully worship God (Ezekiel 36:26-27).

✍🏽 He who has God’s law written in the heart will obey God rather than men, and will sooner disobey all men than deviate in the least from the commandment of God. God’s people, taught by the inspiration of truth, and led by a good conscience to live by every word of God, will take His law, written in their hearts, as the only authority which they can acknowledge or consent to obey. The wisdom and authority of the divine law are supreme. [Ellen G. White: Testimonies Volume 1, 361.1]

## Deuteronomy 12 — Safeguarding Monotheism


### Summary

Moses gives guidelines to safeguard monotheism on arrival into Canaan. In the presence of Moses and reputable leaders like Joshua Israel worshipped God faithfully (Joshua 24:31). Later leaders would be like grievous wolves scattering the flock (Acts 20:29). Moses as did Paul later gave valuable instructions that would keep Israel united.


### Key Lessons


**Only one place of worship (12:1-19).**


1️⃣ Only one place of worship (12:1-19).

#️⃣ Israel were instructed to destroy heathenism in Canaan. We are also to destroy artefacts and literature that were used in satanic worship (Acts 19:19).

⏩ Israel were to use only one place of worship - initially Shiloh was appointed (Joshua 18:1).

⏩ A united worship system coordinated by God is a powerful witness for the truth (John 17:21).

**Dietary expectations (12:20-28).**


2️⃣ Dietary expectations (12:20-28).

#️⃣ Israel were given the privilege to eat meat but they were to follow guidelines on the animals to be eaten and how they were slaughtered.

⏩ Christians were given similar privileges and comparable instructions e.g. we are not to eat blood (Acts 15:20).

**Warning against Idolatry (12:29-32).**


3️⃣ Warning against Idolatry (12:29-32).

#️⃣ Israel were to hate polytheism which was typical in their surrounding idolatrous neighbours. We are to hate the world and its transitory lifestyles which are soon to be destroyed in hell-fire (1 John 2:15-17).

‼️ As the lovers of the world make religion subservient to the world, God requires His worshipers to subordinate the world to religion. The things of the world, that perish with the using, are not to be made the first consideration; these are not the golden currency of heaven. God has not stamped upon them His image and superscription. [SDA Bible Commentary Vol 7 949.5]


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father in heaven, teach us to love unity and purity in worship. May your Spirit train us to hate worldly pleasures. We pray in the name of Jesus Christ. Amen

## Deuteronomy 13 — Testing Prophets


### Summary

A prophet is a spokesperson for men (Nehemiah 6:7) or deities (Deuteronomy 18:20). Moses gave divine counsel on identifying the source of information more than testing the display of miraculous powers.


### Key Lessons

## Deuteronomy 29 — Covenant in Moab


### Summary

♻️ _Moses mediates a covenant between Israel and God 40 years after a similar covenant on Mount Sinai (Exodus 24:8). In the 40 year interval God has proved faithful and Israel has proved themselves very unfaithful. Thus the 2nd Covenant has warnings of destruction not found in the 1st. Most of the old people have perished and the new generation is being prepared to enter Canaan. We must regularly refresh our memories and renew our commitments to serve God faithfully.


### Key Lessons


**Review of God’s gracious deliverance (v 1-9).**


1️⃣ Review of God’s gracious deliverance (v 1-9).

#️⃣ It was by grace that Israel was delivered from Egypt, led for 40 years in the wilderness and conquered mighty kings *(Psalms 136:10-22).

⏩ God’s everlasting grace saved us from the dominionship of sin and sustains us daily (Ephesians 2:8).

**Call to cooperate obedience (v 10-21).**


2️⃣ Call to cooperate obedience (v 10-21).

#️⃣ Israel's prosperity was conditional on them remaining united in serving God.

⏩ Christians likewise must remain as one body headed by Christ (1 Corinthians 12:12).

**Disobedience leads to destruction (v 22-28).**


3️⃣ Disobedience leads to destruction (v 22-28).

#️⃣ Should Israel disobey God, they will suffer utter destruction like that of Sodom and Gomorrah.

⏩ Jesus warned that universal disobedience at the close of earth’s history will result in destruction similar to that of Sodom (Luke 17:26-31).

**Sovereignty of God (v 29).**


4️⃣ Sovereignty of God (v 29).

#️⃣ God is Omniscient and there are sovereign mysteries He has not revealed to humanity.

The Lord expects no less of the soul now than He expected of man in Paradise, perfect obedience, unblemished righteousness. The requirement under the covenant of grace is just as broad as the requirement made in Eden—harmony with God’s law, which is holy, just, and good. [Ellen G. White: Christ Object Lessons 391.2]

**God's amazing perfection (v 1-4).**

2️⃣ God's dealing with corrupted mankind (v 5-8).

#️⃣ God is dealing with corrupted nations who have nearly obliterated His image originally invested in Adam (Genesis 1:26-28).

**God's unique choice of an inheritance (v 9-14).**


3️⃣ God's unique choice of an inheritance (v 9-14).

#️⃣ Israel became complacent, forgot about God and joined the rebellion against God.
