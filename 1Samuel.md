# 1 Samuel

## Introduction

** Book** 1 Samuel 
  
**Introducing the Books: 1st and 2nd Samuel**
 
 **Authorship**
Tradition attributes the two books of 1 and 2 Samuel to Samuel, Gad and Nathan, and these were written between BC1100 and BC1011. This was the time when Israel was transitioning from judges oversight to monarchy rule. 

 **Major Themes**
**(a)** **Gods Sovereignty in History**
This is the major theme, emphasizing that God is the true King of Israel, and any human king serves only as His deputy.

**(b)** Leadership and God's Calling

**(c)** Obedience vs. Rebellion

**(d)** Faith vs. Fear

**(e)** Worship, Reverence, and the Presence of God

**Contemporary Application**
**(a)**	God Still Calls People Today
God speaks through Scripture, conscience, and providence, calling the youth and adults alike.

**(b)**	Obedience Is Central to End-Time Faithfulness 
Sauls disobedience echoes the end-time struggle between those who obey Gods commandments and those who follow human tradition **(Revelation. 12:17, 14:12);** reminding believers to prioritize Gods word over convenience and rejecting compromise even under pressure.

**(c)**	Spiritual Leadership in the Church
The remnant church requires leaders who are Spirit-led, courageous, and faithful to mission.

**(d)**	Trusting God in Crisis
Faith is strengthened by remembering God's past guidance.

**(e)**	The Importance of True Worship
Worship should be reverent, Christ-cantered, and rooted in Scripture.

> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for the lessons from the book of 1 Samuellessons of calling, obedience, leadership, and faith. Teach us to listen like Samuel, to obey like David, and to depend on Your strength in every challenge. May Your Spirit guide us to live faithfully in these last days. In Jesus name, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 1


### Summary

Elkanahs family dynamics are introduced, and the misperceived prayer of Hanah recorded and answered. Hanahs child of prayer is given back as promised.


### Key Lessons


**Faithfulness Over Comfort (vv. 3, 7)**


Elkanah and his family faithfully went up to Shiloh year after year to worship, despite the domestic strife and the likely corruption of the priests.

We must maintain our commitment to communal and personal worship even when circumstances (domestic struggles, institutional flaws, or personal pain) make it difficult or uncomfortable.

**The Power of Earnest, Afflicted Prayer (vv. 1013, 15)**


Hannah's prayer was one of deep distress, pouring out her soul silently to God. It changed her countenance from sorrowful to hopeful.

When criticized or provoked because of some lack, do you seek resolution through human means, or do you pour out directly to God, as Hannah did?

**The Danger of Misjudging Others (vv. 1314, 17)**


Eli wrongly and harshly judged Hannah's silent, fervent prayer as drunkenness.

We must exercise charity and carefulness when observing the spiritual distress or peculiar actions of others.

We should seek to understand and bless rather than condemn, knowing that true devotion often looks unconventional to the casual observer.

**Dedication and Vows (vv. 11, 24-28)**


Hannah made a solemn, specific vow to dedicate her future son to God as a Nazirite, which vow she fulfilled upon receiving the blessing.

When God blesses us, we must honour our commitments to Him.

**The Power of a Mothers Influence (1 Samuel 1:27-28)**


Adventist homes are called to be centres of spiritual formationpreparing youth for service, integrity, and mission.

Parents have a sacred responsibility to nurture their children for Gods service.

Hannahs faithfulness shapes Samuels destiny as a prophet and leader in Israel.

*"The influence of a praying mother is lasting; her work does not end with her life."* **(Ellen G. White: The Adventist Home, 245).**


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Heavenly Father, thank You for the example of Hannahs faith. Teach us to bring every burden to You in sincere and humble prayer. Help us trust You even before the answer comes. Make our worship reverent, our homes places of spiritual growth, and our lives consecrated to Your service. In Jesus name we pray, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 2


### Summary

This chapter details the following: (i)Hannahs song in thankfulness. (ii) The sin of Elis sons. (iii) Samuels ministry. (iv) By Elis blessing Hannah is more fruitful. (v) Elis reproveth his sons. (vi) A prophecy against Elis house.


### Key Lessons


**God's Sovereign Reversal (Hannah's Song) (vv. 110)**


Hannah responds to Gods faithfulness with a powerful song of worship, exalting His sovereignty, justice, and reversal of human fortunes.

True gratitude should lead us to worship God publicly, acknowledging His power in our lives.

How often do we pause to praise God for His blessings rather than just asking for more?

**Contempt for Sacred Things (vv. 1217)**


Eli's sons treated the Lord's offerings with contempt, putting their own greed and pleasure ahead of God's prescribed worship.

Treating church resources, worship time, or Biblical truth lightly is a modern form of despising the Lord's offering.

Spiritual leaders must live with integrity; hypocrisy and abuse of power bring judgment.

*"When those who profess to serve God follow their own selfish desires, they make sacred things appear common."* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, 576).**

**The Failure of Parental Discipline (vv. 29)**


God condemned Eli for honouring his sons more than God and failing to restrain their sin. This was a sin of passive negligence.

Parents and spiritual leaders have a solemn duty to restrain sin in their households and spheres of influence.

Allowing sin out of misplaced love or fear of confrontation dishonours God and invites judgment.

**Integrity in a Corrupt Environment (vv. 18, 26)**


Young Samuel ministered before the Lord in the Tabernacle while surrounded by the scandalous corruption of Eli's adult sons.

Personal faithfulness is possible and necessary even when institutional or surrounding influences are deeply corrupt.

We are individually accountable for our own integrity, regardless of others' failures.

**The Enduring Faithfulness of a Mother (vv. 19)**


Hannah brought Samuel a new robe yearly, demonstrating her continued love and commitment to his dedication, supporting his service from afar.

Consistent, prayerful parental supportespecially for those in ministry or serviceis a lifelong duty.

Our support (through prayer, encouragement, and practical means) sustains the called ones even when they are physically separated from us.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank you answering our prayers. May our hearts overflow with praises for your mighty works in our lives. Help us to always honour You as the God who lifts up the humble and judges righteously. In Jesus name, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 3


### Summary

The young boy Samuel hears a voice calling him one night and, mistakenly believing it was the old priest Eli, runs to him three times. Eli finally realizing the Lord was summoning Samuel and tells the boy how to answer, leading Samuel to receive a divine message of judgment against Eli's family due to his sons' wickedness and Eli's failure to discipline them. Samuel courageously delivers the difficult prophecy to Eli, and as he grows, all of Israel recognized him as a true prophet and respected his leadership.


### Key Lessons


**Rare Word (vv. 1-3)**


Under Eli's compromised leadership, "the word of the Lord was rare and visions were infrequent, highlighting the spiritual drought in Israel during his tenure.

It would be unfortunate that our homes, churches and communities may be suffer spiritual decline under our oversight  while we sleep.

*God could not communicate with the high priest and his sons; their sins, like a thick cloud, had shut out the presence of His Holy Spirit.* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 581)**

**Hearing God's Voice Takes Training (vv. 710)**


Samuel did not initially recognize God's voice; it took Eli's instruction (despite his faults) for him to learn how to listen and respond.

Recognizing Gods guidance requires spiritual mentorship and practice.

We must seek guidance from mature believers (like Eli, despite their faults) so we may repeatedly position ourselves to listen and respond aright.

**The High Cost of Parental Negligence (vv. 1114)**


God confirms that Eli's failure to restrain his sons was so BIG a corporate sin that it could not be redeemed, leading to the destruction of his priestly lineage.

God views failure to confront known sin (negligence) as a direct offense.

As spiritual leaders and parents, we must prioritize God's honour over family comfort or emotional convenience.

**Faithfulness in Difficult Communication (vv. 15, 18)**


Samuel, as a child, faithfully delivered a terrifying message of judgment to his loving mentor, Eli facing inevitable, difficult consequences, humbly surrendered to Gods sovereign justice and will.

Faithfulness and courage must be maintained in communicating God's truth, even when the message is difficult, painful, or directed at people we love and respect.

Eli, Truth must be delivered with integrity and received with humility.

How do you respond when Gods truth challenges my comfort or exposes areas needing change?

**Obedience, not office bearing (vv. 1, 2021)**


God bypassed the formal, corrupted priestly system (Eli's sons) to raise up a new voice of authority through the prophet Samuel, demonstrating His preference for a listening heart over mere office.

We must follow genuine spiritual authority (God's communicated will) over mere institutional title or tradition, for God is no respecter of descent or domain.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for patiently holding out for us. Help us to listen for Your voice and obey Your call. Give us courage to speak truth and faithfulness to grow in Your service. In Jesus name we pray, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 4


### Summary

Israel goes out to battle and suffers defeat. Instead of seeking repentance or Gods guidance, the elders decide to bring the Ark from Shiloh, assuming its presence will guarantee victory. Hophni and Phinehas, accompanying the Ark, are killed along with 30,000 soldiers and the ark is captured. On receiving the bad news, Eli falls, breaks his neck and dies. His daughter-in-law, upon learning the same news, gives birth prematurely, names her son Ichabod and dies shortly afterward.


### Key Lessons


**Confusing Symbols with Substance (vv. 34)**


Israel treated the Ark of the Covenanta symbol of God's presenceas a good luck charm or a tool to manipulate God into giving them victory, instead of repenting and seeking His will.

We must avoid superstition or ritualismconfusing external religious practices (e.g., carrying a Bible, attending church, wearing a cross) with a genuine, obedient relationship with God.

True power is in the presence and will of God, not in the object.

*The forms of religion, without the power, are worthless.* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 584.)**

**The Danger of Presumption (vv. 5)**


The Israelites rejoiced loudly upon the Ark's arrival, presuming victory was guaranteed by the object itself, rather than by Gods favour.

Presumptionbelieving God must act for us based on our status or rituals, rather than His commitment to our repentance and obedienceis a dangerous form of unbelief that leads to spiritual defeat.

**The Reproach of God's People (vv. 9)**


The Philistines, though initially terrified by the Ark's arrival, rallied with courage, recognizing they were in a desperate fight against the power of God (v 9).

Sometimes, the presence of truth or God's people in the world can provoke the adversary to fight even harder.

When facing intense opposition, do we assume that God has abandoned us, or do we recognize that our commitment to God may have simply provoked a greater resistance from the forces of darkness, requiring us to be "strong and resilient?

**Divine Judgment and Prophecy Fulfilled (vv. 11, 1718)**


The deaths of Hophni and Phinehas, and the death of Eli, fulfil the precise judgment God delivered to the family.

The Bible reminds us that God's word is always sure. We should not treat divine warnings lightly.

Judgment, though terrible, is always an act of God's justice and is necessary to clear the path for spiritual renewal (like Samuel's ministry).

**Spiritual Loss Over Physical Loss (vv. 18, 2122)**


Eli's fatal shock and Phinehas' wife's dying words focused not on the 30,000 dead soldiers or the loss of their family, but on the capture of the Arkthe loss of God's glory (Ichabod).

Gods power is experienced in homes and churches where prayer is sincere, obedience is practiced, humility is present, and sin is not excused.

*The withdrawal of Gods blessing is always proportional to the departure from His law.* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets 587)**


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank you for your forbearance that gives a chance even when we wander away. Help us not to rely on outward forms but on a genuine, obedient walk with You. Teach us to honour what is sacred, to lead with integrity, and to seek Your presence with reverence and humility. In Jesus name we pray, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 5


### Summary

The Philistines place the captured Ark in Dagons temple, but their idol falls before it. Subsequently, plagues also strike the Philistines. Realizing the Ark brings divine judgment, the Philistines cry out and decide to send it back.


### Key Lessons


**God Cannot Be Contained or Controlled (vv. 12)**


The Philistines thought they could capture God's power and place it in a subordinate position in Dagon's temple.

Dagons fall and the plagues demonstrated that His presence exposes and overturns anything that replaces Him.

Gods presence works according to His will, often in ways that destroy our comfortable religious structures and false gods.

When we feel overwhelmed or even big-headed, may we remember that God didnt need an army to prove Himself to the Philistines; He can fight His own battles.

**The Supremacy of God Over Idols (vv. 35)**


The idol Dagon was literally dismantled in its own temple by the unseen power of God. The Ark needed no defender; God defended His own honour.

We must recognize the absolute supremacy of God over all modern "idols"materialism, power, secular philosophies, or personal achievementswhich will ultimately fall apart when confronted by His power and presence.

**Consequences of Irreverence (vv. 6, 9)**


Eli and his sons died, and the Philistines were punished with a severe plague for treating the holy Ark irreverently. The presence of God demands reverence and respect.

Carelessness or irreverence toward the sacred (His Word, His Church, His name) carries consequences, whether practiced by those inside or outside the covenant community.

*All that is connected with God should be treated with reverence.*  **(Ellen G. White: Testimonies to the Church vol. 5, p. 491)**

**Recognition of Divine Power (5:7, 11)**


The Philistines, though pagan, quickly and clearly connected their suffering to the Ark, recognizing the hand of the God of Israel.

God's power often manifests in ways that compel recognition from the sceptical world.

Our conduct and the presence of God in our lives should lead others to acknowledge a power beyond the natural.

**Judgment Brings Panic and Rejection (vv. 11-12)**


The people of Ekron were so terrified that they demanded the Ark be removed, fearing universal death.

Unfortunately, this fear of divine judgment did not lead to repentance, but just a desperate desire to get rid of God's presence and the reminder of His holiness.

It is not enough to just fear judgement, a recognition of God demands an evident change of heart, not just the environment.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank you for showing that you are sovereign and over all else. Help us to cleanse our lives of everything that competes with your glory, teach us to walk with reverence and obedience to your will. In Jesus name, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 6: The Ark Returns to Israel


### Summary

After some consultations, the Philistines return the Ark with a guilt offering after experiencing plagues. The calving cows yoked to carry the ark head straight to Beth-shemesh, revealing Gods guiding providence. 70 men who peeked into the Ark are struck, underscoring holiness and reverence.


### Key Lessons


**Worldly Wisdom Meets Divine Holiness (vv. 1-5)**


The Philistines devise a "test" using milk cows to return the Ark, yet they are not moved to turn to the confirmed God who had visited judgements over their god and self.

It is not enough to recognize God's power; true repentance is total surrender to His will.

Where might you be applying worldly logic to spiritual matters rather than simple obedience?

**Gods Sovereignty (vv. 7-12)**


The cows miraculously head straight to Israel despite every natural impulse to return to their calves. God directs even animals to fulfil His purposes; trust His leading in our uncertainties.

_"God's providence works in ways that contradict human reasoning,

proving His divine hand"_ **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, 589).**

**Irreverence Among Israelites:(vv. 13-19)**


The Israelites celebrate the Ark's return, but some, out of curiosity, peep into it unlawfully and are struck dead.

Have we lost our sense of awe in worship, mishandling  holy things - scripture, ordinances, tithe, marriage - as common?

How can we cultivate deeper reverence in our personal and corporate worship?

**Holiness and Reverence (vv. 1920)**


Gods holiness demands reverence. Worship must be conducted in purity and obedience, the otherwise becomes an abomination that leads to condemnation.

*"The nearer we come to God, the more we should sense our unworthiness and His majesty"* **(Ellen G. White: Steps to Christ, 99).**

**The Ark Finds Rest (vv. 21)**


Because of a lack in reverence, Beth-Shemesh could not host the Ark. Kiriath-Jearim received it because they accepted the responsibility reverently.

Gods presence rests where His Word is respected, His will is honoured, His law is cherished, and His character is reflected.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank you for Your perfect holiness. Forgive us for moments of irreverence and presumption. Give us discernment to recognize Your guidance even when it defies human logic. Prepare our hearts to properly receive and honour Your presence. May we never lose the holy fear that accompanies true intimacy with You. In Jesus' name, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 7


### Summary

20 years after the arks return, Samuel calls Israel to put away idols and return to the Lord. At Mizpah, repentance leads to victory over the Philistines. Samuel judges Israel faithfully, and Ebenezer commemorates Gods help.


### Key Lessons


**The Call to Wholehearted Repentance (vv. 3-4)**


Samuel challenges Israel to "return to the Lord with ALL your hearts" by removing foreign gods and preparing their hearts.

True revival begins with COMPLETE spiritual surrender and removal of competing loyalties, demanding not just emotion BUT action.

*"Half-hearted repentance bears no fruit. God requires the whole heart or none"* **(Ellen G. White: Testimonies, vol. 5, 157).**

**Corporate Prayer as Spiritual Warfare (vv. 5-6)**


Samuel gathers Israel at Mizpah for prayer, fasting, and confession - turning a military threat into a spiritual confrontation.

The church's greatest power lies in united prayer, which invites God into our daily struggles, rather than human strategies.

Before you fight, PRAY!

Do we prioritize corporate prayer in our spiritual battles today?

**God's Miraculous Deliverance (vv. 9-11)**


As Samuel offers sacrifice after the corporate repentance, God answers with supernatural thunder to rout the Philistines who had gathered against Israel.

Divine intervention follows faithful intercession and obedience. Gods blessing is often on the other side of honest repentance.

*"God waits to work mightily when His people depend wholly on Him rather than human strength"* **(Ellen G. White: Prophets and Kings, 156).**

**The Ebenezer Memorial (vv. 12)**


Samuel sets up a stone called "Ebenezer" (Thus far the Lord has helped us) to commemorate God's faithfulness.

We must intentionally remember and testify of God's interventions, for intentional remembrance nurtures faith.

What "Ebenezers" have you established to remember God's faithfulness?

**A True Shepherd (vv. 15-17)**


Samuel's travel across Israel brought consistent spiritual guidance, established justice and worship throughout Israel.

Godly leadership provides stability and direction in times of transition.

He also built an altar at home, a call to every believer to make their home a a place of daily prayer and worship.

*"True shepherds  pastors, elders, parents - maintain spiritual vigilance, regularly ministering to God's flock"* **(Ellen G. White: Gospel Workers, 184).**


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, we return to You with full surrender, removing every rival for our devotion. As we unite in prayer, fight our battles for us. Help us remember Your past faithfulness when facing new challenges. May we be worthy ministers to your people around us. In Jesus' name we pray, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 8: Israel Demands a King


### Summary

Samuel, now old, appoints his sons as judges in Beersheba who, unlike him, took bribes and perverted justice. Subsequently, Israel demands a king like all nations. Samuel is aggrieved but God pacifies him and allows it with solemn caution.


### Key Lessons


**Spiritual heritage does not automatically guarantee spiritual character (vv. 1-3)**


Godly character must be intentionally cultivated, not assumed.

Care should be taken to prevent parental neglect and lack of discipline that can produce children unworthy of our legacy and even fomenting divergent conduct amongst brethren.

**Rejecting God's Leadership (vv. 4-5)**


Israels demands for conformity was a rejection of God's unique model and compromised their God-given identity and mission.

The Remnant Church is called to be distinct, not conformed to the world.

By wanting to imitate surrounding cultures, Gods people lose their mission and purpose.

Where are we trading divine guidance for worldly security structures?

**The Reason Behind the Request (vv.7-8)**


God reveals to Samuel: "They have rejected me as their king" - exposing deeper spiritual rebellion.

Rejecting God's divinely established methods (e.g., the authority of Scripture, the call to radical service, specific doctrines) is, ultimately, a rejection of God's sovereignty. Surface-level requests often mask heart-level idolatry.

*"God reads the motives behind our prayers. What we call 'reasonable requests' may spring from unbelief."* **(Ellen G. White: Testimonies, vol. 4, 213)**

**Rejecting Gods authority brings unintended consequences (vv. 10-18)**


Gods ways protect us, human alternatives often complicate life. Israel thought having a king would solve their problems but it brought burdens and sorrow.

Every departure from God carries its own wages,  not punishment arbitrarily inflicted.

Worldly solutions always come with hidden costs to spiritual freedom.

**God's Permissive Will (vv. 19-22)**


Not everything God allows is Gods ideal. In His non-coercive love, God sometimes allows harmful choices to teach vital lessons.

How do I respond when Gods counsel is different from what I want?

What painful lessons might we be learning because we insisted on our own way?

*"When God's warnings go unheeded, He may permit suffering to become our teacher"* **(Ellen G. White: Prophets and Kings, 417).**


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank you for your perfect will for our lives. Forgive us for rejecting Your leadership for our desires. Open our eyes to the hidden costs of self-reliance and help us learn quickly from the consequences of our stubbornness. In Jesus name we pray. Amen.

## 1 Samuel 9


### Summary

Saul searches for lost donkeys and by some seemingly chance meets Samuel to whom God had revealed Saul as Israels chosen king. Saul is assured of the safety of his fathers donkeys, is informed of his honour and gets preferential treatment.


### Key Lessons


**Dont despise the Ordinary Tasks (vv. 3-6)**


Saul's search for lost donkeys becomes the unlikely path to his anointing, showing God's hand in everyday events.

Saul was just looking for lost donkeys but God was orchestrating a kingdom appointment behind the scenes.

What seemingly insignificant events in your life might God be using to direct you?

**Dont Despise subordinate Counsel (vv. 6-10)**


Saul's servant wisely suggests consulting Samuel, Saul accepted the counsel that put him in the direct path of his destiny. He also gets help from the maidens on where to find Samuel.

God places people in our lives to direct us toward His purposes.

What if Saul had chosen to dismiss the counsel? How well do we take the counsel of our subordinates at the office? At home?

**The Willing Helpers to the Honest Seeker (vv. 11-14)**


The maidens clearly provide details and even urge Saul to make haste or miss the prophet.

Being approachable and willing to offer clear guidance will help those we encounter to connect with Christ. Additionally, we often find our way by listening to those around us, regardless of their status.

**God Prepares Both Leader and Prophet (vv. 15-17)**


While Sauls search for the lost donkeys  and the servants suggestion  led him to Samuel, God had prepared Samuel for this divine appointment.

Nothing ever happens by chance; God synchronizes circumstances to fulfil His plans.

*"As the wheel-like complications were under the guidance of the hand beneath the wings of the cherubim, so the complicated play of human events is under divine control."* **(Ellen G. White: Education, 178).**

**God Confirms His Call (vv. 18-24)**


Samuel gave Saul three confirmations: the donkeys being found, he being chosen by God and publicly honouring him.

God does not call us blindly - He provides assurance to confirm His call.

Who in your sphere needs encouragement and affirmation to step into Gods calling?


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, Thank You for the counsellors You place in our path. Open our eyes to see Your hand in life's ordinary moments. Synchronize our lives with Your perfect timing and transform our natural abilities into spiritual gifts. May we walk humbly in the assignments You give. In Jesus name we pray. Amen.

## 1 Samuel 10: Saul's Anointing and Transformation


### Summary

Samuel anoints Saul privately, gives signs of affirmation from God, and presents him publicly for installation as king. Some accept, while others despise him still.


### Key Lessons


**The Divine Anointing (v. 1)**


Samuel anoints Saul as king, symbolizing Gods choice and empowerment for leadership.

True leadership is not self-appointed but God-ordained and Spirit-empowered.

Whilst ambition is good, it must be subservient to Gods revealed will.

How can we discern between human ambition and divine calling in leadership today?

**Confirmation Through Circumstances (vv. 2-9)**


Samuel provides specific signs to confirm Sauls calling, proving Gods hand in his journey.

God often confirms His will through aligned circumstances and godly counsel. Confirmatory signs are meant to build our faith, not feed our pride.

_"As Saul went on his way, all came to pass as the prophet had said." **(Ellen G. White: Patriachs and Prophets, p. 610.2).**

**Spiritual transformation precedes effective leadership (vv. 6, 9)**


God gives Saul a new heart, equipping him with prophetic inspiration and courage.

God works on our character before entrusting us with ministry, authority, or influence.

*"Unless the heart is renewed by divine grace, human talent will prove a snare"* **(Ellen G.White:  Testimonies, vol. 4, 535).**

Have we sought Gods inner transformation to match our outward responsibilities?

**Humility or Lack of Faith (vv. 16, 22)**


Saul initially hides among the baggage, showing reluctance rather than readiness for his new role even after the anointing and confirmation.

While true leaders serve with humility before stepping into prominence, hesitancy may indicate a lack of faith or an attempt to avoid a known duty.

True humility is obedience to the call, not evasion.

**Mixed Responses to Gods Choice (vv. 24-27)**


While many celebrate Sauls kingship, some doubters despise him, foreshadowing future conflict.

Even God-appointed leaders face opposition. Just remember: faithfulness matters more than approval. When criticized or rejected, respond to contempt with composure and trust in Gods opportune time.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Heavenly Father, thank You for calling and equipping leaders by Your Spirit. Transform our hearts to serve with humility and courage. Help us to honor Your choices, even when others doubt. May we walk in Your anointing, not our own strength. In Jesus name we pray, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 11: Sauls First Victory


### Summary

The Amorites mobilise against Jabesh Gilead and spurn a servitude offer with even harsher treatment to spite Israel as a whole. The Gileadites appeal to their kinsmen, and Saul inspired of God, mobilises and organises Israel to fight the Amorites. Mercy and unity mark the celebration, rather than reprisal and bloodshed.


### Key Lessons


**Fighting God by Fighting His People (vv. 1-3)**


Nahash went against Jabesh-Gilead to spite Israel, Israel was Gods.

Some troubles that come our way are but the adversary trying to get at whose-we-are. They may not necessarily be a consequence of our own design.

What is our perception or reality of the misfortune we experience today?

**A Leader Rises to the Challenge (vv. 11:5-6)**


Upon hearing of the Ammonite threat against Jabesh-Gilead, the freshly anointed Saul filled with the Spirit of God comes into righteous anger and resolve.

True leadership emerges in moments of crisis when Gods people are in need.

Are there injustices or spiritual battles that should stir us to action today?

Gods chosen leaders are not idle in times of perilthey are moved with divine urgency.

**Unity Through Shared Purpose (vv. 7-8)**


Saul rallies Israel by sending a dramatic warning (cutting up oxen), and the people respond with fear of the Lord and unity.

Gods people accomplish great things when united under a clear, God-given mission.

When hearts are knit together in holy purpose, no enemy can stand.

What would it take for you to unite with the church with such conviction today?

**Spiritual battles are won through divine power, not human strength (vv. 11:11)**


Saul was ordinary until Gods Spirit enabled him to act decisively. In moments of personal, family, or community crisis, God equips those who trust Him with courage, wisdom, and ability.

_" it is not possible for him [one called by God] to be placed in a position for which God has not already made abundant provision." **(Ellen G. White: SDA Bible Commentary, Samuel).**

**Mercy in Victory (vv. 12-13)**


After victory, Saul refuses to execute his critics, showing restraint and grace.

Refusing revenge cultivates unity and reflects Christs character, and true strength is shown in mercy, not vengeance.

Do we respond to opposition with retaliation or Christlike grace?

**Public Renewal of Commitment (vv. 14-15)**


Samuel leads Israel to reaffirm Sauls kingship at Gilgal, combining celebration with spiritual renewal.

Spiritual victories should lead to fresh dedication to God.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank you for empowering us when we avail ourselves. Unite us in holy purpose and fight through us for Your glory. Teach us to handle victory with humility and mercy. May every triumph lead us back to You in fresh surrender. In Jesus name we pray, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 12: Samuel's Farewell Address


### Summary

Samuel bids farewell to Israel, recounting Gods dealing with them, calling them to faithfulness, and warning them against rebellion.


### Key Lessons


**Transparent leaders foster trust; blamelessness honours God (vv. 1-5)**


Samuel publicly puts his record of integrity up for examination before God and man, there was none to challenge or accuse him of wrongdoing.

A life of consistent righteousness is the most powerful testimony a leader  pastor, elder, deacon, brother/sister - can bear for God.

**Remembering God's Faithfulness (1 Samuel 12:6-11)**


Samuel recounts Israel's history of deliverance to remind them of God's constant care in the face of Nahashs pending attacks.

Reviewing God's past mercies strengthens our faith emboldening us for present and future crises.

*We have nothing to fear for the future, except as we shall forget the way the Lord has led us, and his teaching in our past history.*  **(Ellen G. White: Testimonies for the Church, vol. 9 p. 10.3)**

**Obedience brings blessings (vv. 12-15)**


Samuel stresses that Israels choices have results, both individually and collectively.

Our choices  academic, social, or spiritual  carry consequences, both temporal and eternal.

Obedience to God aligns us with His protection and blessing for the Lord desires obedience more than sacrifice.

**God's Reminders (1 Samuel 12:16-18)**


The thunderstorm during harvest that Samuel called for was designed to bring conviction of sin and a return to holiness.

Afraid of the manifested power of God, Israel begs for Samuel not to cease interceding for them.

## 1 Samuel 13 — Saul's Disobedience and the Cost of Impatience

### Summary

Saul, instructed by Samuel to wait for him to offer sacrifice before the battle with the Philistines, prematurely offers the sacrifice fearing desertion by his soldiers, violating God's command. Samuel rebukes him and announces the kingdom will go to a man after God's own heart.

### Key Lessons

**Impatience Leads to Disaster** *(vv. 8–9)*

Saul's fear of desertion and impatience toward Samuel's arrival prompted him to act prematurely. Rushing ahead without prayer or counsel can have serious consequences in school, work, or ministry. Impatience often leads to unauthorised shortcuts in God's work — we must rely on God's timetable in decisions, promotions, and problem-solving.

**The Excuse of Circumstances** *(vv. 11–12)*

Saul justifies his disobedience by blaming the disappearing troops and Samuel's delay. Difficult circumstances can never justify a disregard of God's clear commands. When we sin, is our first response genuine confession ("I have sinned"), or do we immediately resort to explaining, excusing, or blaming circumstances and others for our disobedience?

**The High Cost of Presumption** *(vv. 13–14)*

God's desire in leadership is not charisma or military skill, but a heart committed to unconditional obedience that aligns its desires with God's will. Samuel declares Saul's kingdom won't endure because he failed God's test of obedience. Temporary gains from disobedience lead to permanent losses in God's plan. What future blessings might we forfeit through present compromises?

**The Consequences of Failed Leadership** *(vv. 15–17)*

Saul's disobedience affected not only himself but also Israel's future. As parents, teachers, and leaders, we must understand that our decisions impact those we are called to protect, and that we are accountable to God.

> *"When leaders fail spiritually, their people suffer materially."*

**The Weapon-less Army** *(vv. 17–23)*

The Philistines disarm Israel, leaving them defenceless — a direct result of rejecting God's protection through disobedience. When we disobey, spiritual power and the "weapons of war" (Scripture, faith, prayer) can feel inadequate or inaccessible, leaving us paralysed by fear and relying only on primitive human efforts.

> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for Your unfailing promises. Forgive our impulsive attempts to "help" Your work. Strengthen us to wait faithfully when tested. Remove all excuses for disobedience from our hearts. In Jesus' name we pray, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 14: Faith Versus Fear

**Faith vs. Fear (vv. 1.6)**


Whilst Saul and the rest of Israel waited in Gibeah paralyzed by fear, Jonathan, acting on a simple faith, slipped out to attack the Philistines.

**Courageous Faith defies Impossible Odds (vv. 6-7)**


Jonathan declares his faith and steps out against the Philistines.

Sauls army is frozen in fear until they see Gods hand at work through Jonathan.

Fear immobilizes Gods people, but faith-filled action inspires others to follow.

When Sauls oath threatens Jonathans life, the people intervene and save him.

How can we balance respect for authority with the courage to challenge unrighteous decisions?

In Jesus name we pray, Amen.

David waited for God to act rather than taking matters into his own hands.

When wronged, we must resist the urge to take matters into our own hands.

God, the righteous ultimate Judge, has appointed a day when He will judge the world in righteousness. Until then, we must trust His justice.

**Persuasion by Righteousness (v. 1620)**


True strength is measured by moral integrity and self-control, not by ability to dominate or retaliate.

Righteous living and unexpected mercy are far more powerful tools for convicting the guilty than any verbal accusation or calculated confrontation.

*A consistent life, characterized by integrity and kindness, is a powerful witness for truth.* **(Ellen G. White: Testimonies for the Church, Vol. 4, p. 610, Counsels on Health, p. 482.)**

**Trust Gods Timing for Promotion (vv. 20-22)**


David waited for Gods timing rather than seizing power.

Patience and trust in Gods timing prevent harm and ensure His purposes are fulfilled. His plans know no haste and no delay, in the fulness of time, it all shall come to pass.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for the example of Davids restraint and trust in You. Teach us to respond to injustice with mercy, to listen to our conscience, and to wait patiently for Your timing. Help us to live with integrity and to reflect Your character in every situation. In Jesus name we pray, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 25: David, Nabal and Abigail


### Summary

Samuel dies, is mourned and buried by all Israel. David request provisions from Nabal, a wealthy but harsh man, who had benefited from his protection. Nabal rudely refuses, provoking David to prepare for retaliation. However, Abigail, Nabals wise and discerning wife, intervenes with humility and gifts, preventing bloodshed. Later, Nabal dies suddenly after hearing what nearly happened, and David then takes Abigail as his wife.


### Key Lessons


**Recognizing the End of Eras (v. 1)**


The death of Samuel marked the end of an era; no longer could Isrrael have recourse to the faithful and devoted prophet.

Power and responsibility are transferred to the next generation.

Faithful leaders must learn to finish well, and then yield when their time is complete, recognizing that God's work continues.

Do we focus on training the "Davids" to carry on God's work, or cling to my position even when our era has passed?

**Nabals Folly (vv 2-12, 14-17, 36-38)**


Nabal enjoyed safety he didn't realize was being provided by others, and represents the folly of selfishness, ingratitude, and arrogance, that leads to ruin.

The success and safety we enjoy is built on the invisible labour and protection of others (parents, mentors, or civic workers), prudence is acknowledging the same.

If your employees or family are afraid to bring you the truth because of your temper or pride, you are isolated from what could save from ruin.

**Anger Can Lead to Rash Decisions (v. 13, 2122)**


David, God's anointed, was moments away from committing mass murder out of personal insult and rage.

Anger tempts even godly people to act rashly. It is prudent to exercise self-restraint, to pause and allow the Lord to execute judgment.

_He who is slow to anger is better than the mighty. **(Ellen G. White:  Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 656)**

**Godly Wisdom Can Prevent Disaster (v. 1836)**


Abigails prompt, humble and wise intervention saved unnecessary bloodshed.

Prayerful wisdom and discernment can resolve disputes and avert harmful outcomes in family, school, or community situations.

God uses unexpected counsellors to protect lives and reputations.

How open are we to divine correction and timely counsel even when it comes from an unexpected or seemingly lesser source (a child, a subordinate, a person whose appearance I dismiss)?

**God Honors Those Who Act with Integrity (v. 3235, 3942)**


Nabals death and Abigails actions demonstrate Gods providential care in fulfilling His purposes. When we act with wisdom and integrity, God honours and elevates us in due time.

*Divine providence overrules human folly to preserve the faithful and execute His purposes.* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets 656.)**


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for the example of Abigails courage and wisdom. Teach us to respond to conflict with grace, to pause before acting in anger, and to trust You with justice. Help us to be peacemakers and people of integrity, reflecting Your character in every situation. In Jesus name we pray, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 26 - David spares Sauls life again


### Summary

After the Ziphites inform Saul of Davids location again, Saul pursues him with 3,000 men. David and Abishai sneak into Sauls camp at night and find him sleeping. Though Abishai urges him to kill Saul, David says he will not harm the Lords anointed but takes Sauls spear and water jug as proof that he could have killed Saul but chose mercy. Saul acknowledges Davids righteousness and blesses him instead.


### Key Lessons


**Character is Consistent (vv. 1-4, 9, 6, 21-25)**


The Ziphites, perhaps for political expediency, betray David to Saul.

Saul, despite the emotional confession returns to his jealousy murderous pursuit.

David, despite the golden on-a-plate chance, refrains from harming the anointed king.

Character is the difference between a reaction (dictated by circumstances) and a response (dictated by values), a constant predisposition of our being.

**Working and Waiting (vv. 4-7, 12)**


David courageously made plans to establish and counter Sauls plans, and God actively intervened in the natural course of events to secure them and to prove the innocence of the righteous.

When we risk and avail ourselves for mission, providence will be availed for our success.

**Rejecting the Shortcut to Advancement (vv. 8-11)**


David rejected Abishais offer to kill Saul quickly and establish kingship, insisting that God would bring about his destiny in His own way and time.

Faithful patience ensures we act according to Gods will rather than impulsive human judgment.

Quick fixes (cheating to pass a test, lying to end a conflict, or cutting corners to get a promotion) usually lead to long-term regret.

**The Spear and the Jar (26:11-12)**


David took the spear (power) and the jar (sustenance) but did not touch Saul (life). He took only what was needed for evidence, not for vengeance.

In conflict, seek only enough "evidence" to restore the relationship or prove the truth.

Taking more than necessarylike destroying someones reputationmoves you from the position of a victim to the position of an aggressor.

**Faithful actions influence others (vv. 17-25)**


Davids respectfully spoken truth softened Sauls heart, causing him to acknowledge his wrongdoing.

The tongue can positively influence attitudes, relationships, and communities, the one who masters its craft can advance causes much wider and better.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for the example of Davids restraint and trust in You. Help us to walk in integrity, to show mercy when wronged, and to wait on Your perfect timing. Teach us to confront with grace and reflect Your justice, patience, and love. In Jesus name we pray, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 27: Settling in Enemy Territory


### Summary

Fearing Sauls pursuit, David goes to Achish, king of Gath, and presents himself as a loyal ally, misleading the Philistines about his raids. David settles in Ziklag, a Philistine town, and gains Achishs trust while continuing to protect Israelite interests privately.


### Key Lessons


**Fear Can Lead Us Away from Gods Promises (vv. 1-4)**


David looked at the sustained persecution and allowed human reasoning and fear to override divine promises.

When fear overrides faith, we may forget Gods hand in the past to seek solace, security, or solutions in the very camp of the enemy.

Trusting God is essential, even in prolonged trials.

*It is not the will of God that His people should be weighed down with care and fear.* **(Ellen G. White: Steps to Christ, p. 121)**

**Spiritual Dead Time (vv. 5-7)**


The year and four months David spent in Ziklag was a period where his kingship was not advanced, and his character was challenged.

It was marked by compromise, deceit, and separation from the Lords prophets and priests. Regardless, Gods mercy can transform our improper refuge into a kings citadel.

**Compromise Can Lead to Moral Complexity (v. 810)**


To keep the secret, David had to be ruthless, leaving no survivors.

One compromise often demands many others to maintain it.

God calls us to truth and integrity because every deviation from truth weakens the character and brings confusion to the soul. *"One wrong step prepares the way for another... If he [David] had cherished faith, he would have remained in the land of Judah and trusted the Lord to deliver him."* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 689).**

**The False Narrative (vv. 10-12)**


David used partial truths and geographical ambiguity to lead Achish to believe a lie that propped his survival.

We must be discerning about the "narratives" people present, for sometimes "what meets the eye" is just a performance.

In business, politics, or personal life, a persons report may be crafted to win trust while hiding their true allegiance.

We should gauge loyalty based on long-term fruit and core values, rather than immediate actions probably performed under duress or for strategic optics.

**The Illusion of Allegiance (v. 12)**


Achish based his trust on David's supposed "burnt bridges" with Israel, thinking David was trapped into loyalty.

True loyalty cannot be manufactured through isolation or desperation. Loyalty based only on desperation is a fragile allegiance built on circumstance, not character; it is but a mirage.

Is your allegiance to God true, or just mere desperation?


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for Your patience and protection, even when we make decisions out of fear. Help us to trust You fully, to walk in integrity, and to seek Your guidance in every plan. In Jesus name we pray, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 28 Sauls Demise


### Summary

Facing the Philistine army and desperate for guidance, Saul seeks counsel from a medium despite having previously banning such practices because God had stopped answering him through prophets or dreams, Disguised, he asks her to summon Samuels spirit. A spirit appears, claiming to be Samuel, and tells Saul that God has rejected him and that he and his sons will die in battle. Saul is overwhelmed with fear and collapses.


### Key Lessons


**Caught in the Middle (vv. 1-5)**


By convincingly seeking refuge in Philistine that he was a traitor to, Achish inadvertently expects David to fight against Gods  and his own people, Israel.

Be careful what you "audition" for. The requirements of a position gained by pretence will eventually force us to either commit a great sin or face a great exposure.

**Obedience is essential for guidance (vv. 6, 15, 18)**


Saul lost Gods guidance because of persistent disobedience.

If we would have Gods guidance, then we must be obedient to his bidding.

We cannot expect God to give us new instructions for the future if we have wilfully ignored the clear instructions He has already given us.

When you feel God is silent, do you turn to deeper repentance and study, or to worldly wisdom, social media, or "signs" that aren't from God?

*Gods people must seek counsel through His Word and His appointed channels, not by disobedience.* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets 663.)**

**Desperation without obedience leads to deception (v. 57)**


Bereft of God, Sauls fear of the Philistines drove him to desperation  even seeking help from the very spiritualists he had banned and killed.

When we, out of fear and desperation, are tempted to turn to dangerous, counterfeit spiritualism, occult practices, or non-biblical forms of guidance, may we remember to seek and solely rely on God, for all else is an attempt to gain control where God demands surrender.

**Samuels Appearance (vv. 7-19)**


*The spirit declared to have appeared to Saul was the work of Satan."* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 684).**

*Wherever an influence is exerted to cause men to forget God, there Satan is exercising his bewitching power.* **(Ellen G. White: Acts of the Apostles 290)**

**Disobedience has consequences (vv. 20-25)**


Sauls rebellion led to the prophecy of his downfall, which prophecy couldnt be changed by any other means.

Persistent disobedience or turning away from God leads to serious spiritual and personal consequences. The answer is repentance, not innovation.

*Rejection of Gods guidance brings loss of blessing and opens the way for calamity.* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets 664)**


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, Thank You for being our true source of wisdom and guidance. Forgive us for the times weve sought answers apart from You. Teach us to wait on You with trust, to repent sincerely, and to walk in obedience. May we seek not just Your answers, but Your presence. In Jesus name we pray, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 29: Davids Providential Escape


### Summary

Achishs reasonable expectation of Davids expression of allegiance put David in a precarious position where he had to fight his people. Providentially, the Philistine commanders suspicion forces Achish to force David prevents him from compromising his integrity and mission.


### Key Lessons


**The Futility of Two Masters (vv. 1-3)**


David who had sought refuge in Philistine, even feigned allegiance by the false raids on Israel, is now forced to fight for one against the other.

The statement "No man can serve two masters" **(Matthew 6:24)** is not just a moral command but a psychological and spiritual reality.

Will you serve God, or Satan?

**Reputation Matters (vv. 35)**


Davids past victories and character were known even among the Philistines.

Our witness should be so consistent that even unbelievers recognize integrity.

If the world looked at our current associations, would they ask, "What is this Christian doing here?" Do our presence in certain circles cause confusion about our true allegiance?

A consistent life, pure and holy, is the most powerful argument in favour of the truth. **(Ellen G. White: Testimonies for the Church, vol. 6, p. 47)**

**Gods Protective Providence (vv. 47)**


God coordinates circumstances to prevent David from fighting against his own people, using the Achishs commanders to close out David from a compromising alliance.

Even when we make questionable choices, Gods mercy can redirect us from moral compromise. God sometimes closes doors to keep us from moral conflict.

**The Cost of Compromise (vv. 810)**


Davids deception forces him to plead for the chance to commit a sin he wants to avoid. When we live a lie, we eventually find ourselves "campaigning" for things that would destroy us.

The cost of compromise is a divided mind - a "battleground" where one must argue against their own soul.

Be true and faithful, you save yourself internal conflict.

**Returning to Mission (v. 11)**


David returns to Ziklag, preserved him his true responsibilities.

God carefully directs the affairs of His people in accordance with His purposes, redirecting us to our proper sphere of service; stay focused on His mission for your life.

Where might God, through the changes in your life, be redirecting you to fulfil His mission today?


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for Your protective providence that shields us from compromise. Teach us to value integrity, respond with grace in disappointment, and embrace Your redirection toward mission. Help us to trust Your plans above our own. In Jesus name we pray, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 30: God Restores and Affirms


### Summary

David and his men return to Ziklag to find it destroyed by the Amalekites, their families taken captive. In deep distress, David seeks Gods guidance, pursues the enemy, and recovers all that was lost.


### Key Lessons


**The Exposed Home (vv. 1-5)**


Davids demonstration of allegiance left his home unguarded.

Our attempts to impress "Achish" (the boss, the social circle, or the secular world), often leave our "Ziklag" (our marriage, children, and spiritual health) vulnerable to the enemy's attack.

The enemy always waits for the moment we are distracted "proving ourselves" elsewhere.

**Turning to God in Crisis (vv. 6)**


Facing great personal loss and possible mutiny, David strengthened himself in the Lord his God. In moments of despair, rather than succumbing to fear or blame, we can find strength by seeking Gods presence and promises.

* cut off from every human support  and threatened [him] even with death  David, instead of permitting his mind to dwell upon these painful circumstances, looked earnestly to God for help.* **(Ellen G. White: Patriachs and Prophets 692)**

**Seek Divine Guidance Before Action (vv. 6-10)**


He had motivated, experienced men-of-war, but David inquires of the Lord before pursuing the Amalekites.

Before making major decisions, especially in conflict or recovery, we should seek divine wisdom through prayer and Scripture.

*In every emergency, Christ is near. We may have His help and guidance if we seek it.* **(Ellen G. White: Ministry of Healing, p. 248)**

**Help from the Helpless (vv. 11-15)**


Caring for the starving, abandoned Egyptian slave led to the critical intelligence that secured the victory.

Mercy and humanitarian aid can providentially lead to success and breakthrough.

It is possible that our key breakthroughs are in the abandoned, abused and vulnerable in our sphere.

**Restoration and Recovery (vv. 19-26)**


True to Gods word, David recovered all that had been looted and shared the spoils with the faint who had stayed away and the elders of Judah.

When we trust and obey, God can restore what has been lost - whether relationships, opportunities, or peace.

True leadership values every contribution, recognizing that support roles are as vital as front-line efforts. Sharing blessings with others fosters unity and gratitude.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for not abandoning us in our moments of despair and loss. Help us to seek Your guidance in every challenge, act courageously with faith, encourage those around us, and maintain integrity and fairness. In Jesus name we pray, Amen.

## 1 Samuel 31- Sauls Tragic End


### Summary

During the battle against the Philistines Saul, wounded and fearing capture, takes his own life - an act also copied by his armour bearer. His sons, including Jonathan, are killed. The Philistines desecrate their bodies, but the men of Jabesh-Gilead later retrieve and bury them honourably.


### Key Lessons


**Failed Life: The Consequences of Disobedience (vv. 4)**


*Thus the first king of Israel perished, with the guilt of self-murder upon his soul. His life had been a failure, and he went down in dishonour and despair, because he had set up his own perverse will against the will of God.* **(Ellen G. White: Patriachs and Prophets p. 682)**

**The Tragedy of Despair and Suicide (vv. 4)**


Overwhelmed by fear and hopelessness, Saul committed suicide.

In moments of despair, turning to God and seeking help is vital.

Mental and emotional struggles are real, but we have a sure helper in God and the His able servants to whom we can go for compassion and support.

*Christ is acquainted with all our trials, and He does not leave us to struggle alone.* **(Ellen G. White: Steps to Christ, p. 100)**

**There is ALWAYS a Spiritual Dimension (vv. 9-10)**


The Philistines presented Saul's armour to their idols as an expression of the triumph of paganism and evil over Israels God even though Saul had been disobedient.

Spiritual defeat is measured not just in human lives lost, but in the glory given to the false gods of the enemy. When we fall as individuals, it brings reproach to the cause of God.

_The enemy rejoices when those bearing the name of the Lord fall."' **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 690)**

**In Memory of the Good (vv.11-13)**


Remembering the good that Saul had done for them, the men of Jabesh-Gilead risked their lives to honour him and his sons with a dignified burial.

When a prominent leader, friend, or family member falls from grace or fails, choose to honour the covenant ties and the positive deeds they performed during their prime, rather than to condemn and ridicule their memory.

Prayer: Our heavenly Father, thank You for the lessons from Saul's life. Teach us to humbly walk in obedience, to lead with integrity, and to seek You in every season.
