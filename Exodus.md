# Exodus

## Introduction

**Introducing  the Exodus**

**Authorship**
The book of Exodus is a continuation of the book of Genesis, and likewise, its authorship is also attributed to Moses (Exodus 24:4, Deuteronomy 31:9), around 1440-1400BC. 

**Audience** 
#️⃣ Exodus was written primarily for the Israelites, God’s covenant people, particularly the generation following the exodus from Egypt.
⏩ It served to instruct them in their identity as a nation, their relationship with Yahweh, and their role as a “kingdom of priests” (Exodus 19:6).
⏩ However, it also speaks to all believers: “The lessons of Exodus are for God’s people in every age” **(Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 333).**

🗣️ **Theme**
#️⃣ The book’s chief purpose is to describe God's spectacular intervention to deliver His chosen people from slavery and His gracious act of entering into a covenant relationship with them.
⏩ The theme running through the book is that neither repeated unfaithfulness by the Israelites, nor opposition from the greatest nation on earth could thwart God's plan for them.
⏩ Its stories are meant to strengthen faith and call for confidence in God's leadership.
⏩ It closes with a manifestation of God's glory and power, paralleling how the history of the world will end with the descent of God's glory.

**Major highlights include:**

1.	The birth of Moses

2.	The spectacular deliverance from Egypt

3.	The Sinai Commandments presentation

4.	The Tabernacle Construction

5.	The Lord’s presence and guidance as cloud and pillar of fire.

> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank you for your evident grace as reflected in how you dealt with Israel of old. May we wait upon you, trusting you to stay with us and lead us through our present sojourn. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 1


### Summary

The descendants of Jacob (Israel) multiply greatly in Egypt. A new Pharaoh, "who did not know" Joseph, comes to power and, feeling threatened by the large Hebrew population, enslaves them and imposes harsh labour, in an attempt to suppress them. Despite the affliction, the Israelites multiply and grow. Pharaoh then orders the Hebrew midwives, Shiphrah and Puah, to kill all newborn Hebrew boys, but the midwives fear God and defy his order, preserving life. Pharaoh then commands that all Hebrew male infants be thrown into the Nile.


### Key Lessons


*The descendants of Jacob (Israel) multiply greatly in Egypt. A new Pharaoh, "who did not know" Joseph, comes to power and, feeling threatened by the large Hebrew population, enslaves them and imposes harsh labour, in an attempt to suppress them. Despite the affliction, the Israelites multiply and grow. Pharaoh then orders the Hebrew midwives, Shiphrah and Puah, to kill all newborn Hebrew boys, but the midwives fear God and defy his order, preserving life. Pharaoh then commands that all Hebrew male infants be thrown into the Nile.*

🗣️ **Talking Points**

**Our Ever-Faithful God**


The Israelites' growth testifies to the fulfilment of God’s promise to Abraham. We can trust Him to come through on His promises too, despite the optics.

Pharaoh perceives Israel’s blessings as unwelcome threats. Not everyone will celebrate another’s progress, even when the same is evidently heaven-sent.

**The Great Controversy**


There was more to the disdain that Israel suffered than what met the eye. There could be more to our circumstances than we may ever know.

*"But the more they afflicted them, the more they multiplied and grew". “Satan was the mover in this matter. He knew that a deliverer was to be raised up among the Israelites, and by leading the king to destroy their children, he hoped to defeat the divine purpose.”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 242)**

**Oppression as a Function of Fear**


Pharaoh's fear of the Israelites’ numbers highlights the importance of community and leads him to act unjustly toward them.

Fear can cause selfish or unjust actions.

⁉️ What role does community play in our lives, and how can we prioritize it? Are there areas in your life where fear is causing you to act unjustly or selfishly?

**God Honors Courageous Obedience**


The midwives demonstrate courage in standing up for God’s principles. Their defiance serves as an example of standing up for what is right even in the face of authority and defying orders that violate God's laws and the preservation of human life.

*"The midwives' defiance of Pharaoh's orders shows the importance of obeying God's laws and preserving human life."* **(The SDA Bible Commentary, vol. 1, p. 1085).**

**Ever-present God**


Despite the suffering, God was aware of the Israelites' situation and was present with them, setting the stage for His future intervention.

We can take comfort in God's presence and awareness of our suffering, trusting in His sovereignty and care.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Heavenly Father, thank you for always being with us. Give us the courage to stand for righteousness in the face of oppression. Help us to trust in Your promises, even when the world seems against us. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 2


### Summary

Moses is born to a Levite family during a time when Pharaoh has ordered all Hebrew baby boys to be killed. His mother hides him for three months and then sets him afloat in a basket on the Nile. Pharaoh's daughter finds him and raises him as her own, unknowingly hiring his own mother to nurse him. As an adult, Moses kills an Egyptian who is beating a Hebrew and flees to Midian. There, he defends the daughters of the priest of Midian, marries Zipporah, and becomes a shepherd. Meanwhile, the Israelites groan under slavery, and God hears their cries.


### Key Lessons


*Moses is born to a Levite family during a time when Pharaoh has ordered all Hebrew baby boys to be killed. His mother hides him for three months and then sets him afloat in a basket on the Nile. Pharaoh's daughter finds him and raises him as her own, unknowingly hiring his own mother to nurse him. As an adult, Moses kills an Egyptian who is beating a Hebrew and flees to Midian. There, he defends the daughters of the priest of Midian, marries Zipporah, and becomes a shepherd. Meanwhile, the Israelites groan under slavery, and God hears their cries.*

Talking Points*

**God’s Providence**


God orchestrated events so that Moses was raised in Pharaoh’s palace by his own mother – the basket was at the right place, Miriam was at the place with the right words, for the princess to do the right thing.

Pharaoh’s daughter ends up raising Israel’s future deliverer. God can use even our enemies to fulfil His plan. We should trust His working, even when it seems unlikely.

**Parental Faith Makes a Lasting Impact**


Moses’ mother instilled faith and identity in him during his early years. The spiritual foundation laid in childhood can guide us through life. Invest in godly parenting and mentorship.

"God had heard the mother's prayers; her faith had been rewarded. It was with deep gratitude that she entered upon her now safe and happy task." **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 243)**

**God-Guided Zeal**


Moses’ impulse to deliver his people led him to kill an Egyptian, forcing him to flee when his secret good-intentioned-murder resurfaces after he tries to mediate between two “brothers”.

Good intentions are not enough, they need to align with God’s plan and moral standards.

**God Prepares Leaders in Obscurity**


Moses spends 40 years in Midian before being called by God. Seasons of hiddenness and humility prepare us for future calling.

God can use our experiences to prepare us for service, just as Moses' experiences prepared him.

*"The education received by Moses in Egypt was a preparation for a more important work that he was yet to do... but it was in the desert that he learned the lessons of faith, humility, and patience."* — **(Ellen G. White:  Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 251)**

**A God who Hears**


"God heard their groaning, and God remembered His covenant." (v.24).

Despite all appearances, God saw and heard Israel's groaning.

God never forgets His promises; He is faithful to act in His time. No prayer is unheard. God is attentive to the cries of the suffering and acts in His perfect time.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for being a God who sees and hears; and for being attentive to the cries of the suffering. Help us trust Your timing and Your providence even in difficult or hidden seasons. May we follow Your leading and not our own impulses. In Jesus' name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 3


### Summary

While tending Jethro’s flock near Mount Horeb (or Sinai), Moses encounters a burning bush that is not consumed. God speaks to him from the bush, revealing His name as "I AM" and calling Moses to return to Egypt to deliver the Israelites from slavery. God promises to be with Moses and assures him of divine support, including signs and the promise of bringing the people to a land flowing with milk and honey.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**God’s Presence in the “every day”**


The burning bush appearance happened while Moses was doing ordinary work, showing that God can reveal Himself in our daily routines.

God knows us by name and calls us into purpose, even in ordinary places.

⁉️ How can we be more alert to God's presence and voice in everyday life?

**Holy Ground Requires Reverence (v. 5)**


Moses is instructed: “Take off your sandals, for the place where you are standing is holy ground.”

We must approach God, wherever/whatever place that might be, with humility and reverence, recognizing His holiness.

It is God’s presence that makes a place, time, or institution holy. All that is related to Him is holy, and we ought to reverently treat it so.

*“In all our intercourse with God, we are to maintain the deepest reverence”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 252).**

**The Great Revelation**


God’s self-identification as "I AM WHO I AM" signifies His eternal, self-existent, and unchanging nature.

Studying God's names deepens our understanding of His character. In uncertain times, we can trust the God who never changes.

*“The name ‘I AM’ is a pledge of God’s eternal presence and power”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 253)**

**God Calls and Equips**


Moses felt inadequate, but God called and equipped him. God does not ask about our ability but our availability.

He equips those He calls – providing both His presence and His power.

Obedience to God's call is important, even when it seems daunting, for we are never alone in His assignments.

**Promises Anchor Our Faith**


To our inadequacy-driven hesitancy, God's promises of presence, signs, and deliverance provide assurance.

Through trusting God’s promises, we can overcome our fears and doubts.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for being the eternal "I AM"—unchanging, faithful, and ever-present. Help us to be alert to Your voice, in our everyday life. Equip us for the tasks You call us to, and give us the courage to obey. May we reflect Your compassion to those who suffer and trust that You are with us always. In Jesus' name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 4


### Summary

Moses continues to express doubt about his calling, questioning whether the Israelites will believe him. God gives him three signs and when Moses still hesitates, citing his lack of eloquence, reassures him and appoints Aaron, his brother, as his spokesperson. Moses returns to Egypt and is met by Aaron for the mission to Pharaoh.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**God Provides Signs to Strengthen Faith (v. 1–9)**


To Moses’ expressed doubts and fears, God repeatedly provided tangible signs to confirm His calling and reassure his servant.

When we are open and sincere about our doubts, fears and ignorance, God will provide evidence through His Word, peace, and providence to remove all doubt from our minds.

**God Uses What’s in Your Hand (v. 2)**


*“What is that in your hand?”* – Moses’ simple staff, when availed for the mission, became a divine instrument.

Ordinary things surrendered to God will achieve extraordinary results. ⁉️ What “ordinary” things in your life might God use for His purposes?

**God Is Patient with Our Weakness (v. 10–14)**


Moses’ insecurity about public speaking didn’t disqualify him from service, God provided help.

Our weaknesses are not obstacles to God, but may be opportunities for His grace to be revealed. He will equip and provide the support we need to accomplish His mission through us.

*“God might have chosen angels as His messengers, but He chose men”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 254).**

**Obedience is Evident (v. 18–20)**


Despite significant doubts and protests, Moses eventually accepts God’s call: promptly recusing from Jethro’s employ and returning to Egypt, along with his family.

Faith must be followed by action, even when the path may still be uncertain.

_“Moses did not delay to obey the divine command” **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 255).**

**Delayed Obedience is Disobedience (v. 18–20)**


Moses had neglected to circumcise his son, contravening God's covenant with Abraham (Genesis 17:10-14).

The Lord's fierce reaction shows how seriously He views His covenant promises. Ignoring God’s express dictates can bring catastrophic spiritual consequences.

Zipporah acted to save Moses by fulfilling the covenant requirement.

In marriage or close relationships, sometimes the other partner must step in to correct a spiritual oversight—even when it may be uncomfortable.

**God’s Sovereignty Over Human Hearts (v. 21)**


God is sovereign even over resistance; His purposes will prevail. We can draw peace from knowing that though we may face resistance or opposition, God’s will for our lives will come through.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for using ordinary people and ordinary things to accomplish extraordinary purposes. Help us to trust You when we feel inadequate, and to move forward in obedience even when the path is unclear. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 5


### Summary

Moses and Aaron meet with Pharaoh and request that Pharaoh let the Israelites go to worship God. However, Pharaoh refuses and increases the workload of the Israelites, leading to their suffering and frustration. Moses questions God's plan, feeling unsuccessful in his mission.


### Key Lessons


Key Points*

**True to Duty (vv. 1-2)**


Moses and Aaron did not shy away from approaching Pharaoh with a message that could easily be misinterpreted as disrespectful, if not treasonous.

When duty calls, ours is to stand up and be counted, even in the face of adversity.

**Darkest before Dawn (vv. 6-9)**


Pharaoh’s response to the emissary’s request was to make things worse for the Israelites. This teaches us that sometimes things get worse before they get better — faith must endure.

The Israelites' experience teaches us that even when circumstances seem bleak, it is important to persevere in adversity and trust in God's plan.

**God's Power and Authority**


Though Pharaoh refused to listen to Moses and Aaron, God did not change the plans He had for Israel.

God's power and authority are not limited by human opposition, and we may trust His sovereignty and plan for our lives, even when circumstances seem bleak.

**Leadership: God’s Approval, not man’s: (vv. 20-21)**


The Israelite leaders turned on Moses and Aaron even though they were doing as they had been instructed.

Leadership often involves bearing criticism, even when obeying God. Spiritual leadership often involves being misunderstood or criticized.

**God’s Plan Is Not Always Immediately Clear (v. 22–23)**


After the seemingly failed approach, Moses questioned God’s purpose and timing. We can bring our doubts to God — He can handle our questions.

*“He who sees the end from the beginning permitted this trial to come upon His people”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 260).**


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Heavenly Father, help me to trust in Your sovereignty and plan for my life, even when circumstances seem bleak. Give us the courage to stand up for what's right, and the wisdom to persevere in adversity. May Your presence be with us always, and may we be a blessing to others. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 6


### Summary

God reassures Moses by declaring His name, Jehovah, and reaffirms His covenant to deliver Israel from bondage. Discouraged by their increased suffering, the Israelites do not listen to Moses. Moses again questions his ability to speak, but God instructs both him and Aaron to lead the people and confront Pharaoh, promising to bring them out of Egypt with mighty acts.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**God reveals Himself Progressively**


*“I appeared to Abraham… but by My name, JEHOVAH, I was not known to them”* (v. 3). In seasons of difficulty, God deepens our understanding of His nature. Thus, trials are often platforms for revelation.

**God Never Forgets His Covenant**


God remembers the promises He made to Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. Even when deliverance seems delayed, God is faithful to His promises.

His Word is bankable, we can rely and trust Him. In times of despair, we must anchor ourselves in God’s unchanging promises.

*"God's covenant with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob was a sacred and binding agreement, and He would fulfil His promises to their descendants."* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 281)**

**Discouragement can block Faith**


The Israelites couldn’t hear Moses “because of their suffering.” Pain can make it hard to hear God’s promises — hardships can hinder faith. We must therefore encourage one another in faith.

**God uses imperfect messengers (v. 12, 30)**


Moses' renewed protest about his inability to speak is met with God reiterating the call and empowering him and Aaron.

God calls and equips those He chooses, regardless of perceived limitations. Avail yourself, focus on obedience, not ability.

**The Importance of Heritage**


Moses and Aaron’s genealogy reminds us of the importance of understanding our spiritual heritage and the significance of our identity as children of God.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for Your faithfulness to Your promises. Encourage us when we feel discouraged, and help us to trust in Your plan. In Jesus’ name, Amen.

## Exodus 7


### Summary

God empowers Moses and Aaron to confront Pharaoh, turning Aaron’s rod into a serpent and beginning the plagues with the turning of the Nile into blood. Pharaoh's magicians replicate some signs, but Pharaoh’s heart remains hardened, and he refuses to listen.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**Authority and Subordinacy**


Work was given to both Moses and Aaron, and God highlights the operational - functional hierarchy in the mission.

When God calls, He also delegates divine authority. Serving with Heaven’s backing still respects order of processes and communication to smoothen operations.

**God Demonstrates Authority**


The staff-to-serpent sign demonstrates God's power over nature and the turning of the Nile – Egypt’s life source – into blood demonstrated His power over gods of Egypt.

We can trust in God's power to enable work and bring conviction in extraordinary ways.

**Counterfeits Exist**


Pharaoh's magicians could imitate the sign, showing that demonic power can mimic divine acts.

Not all supernatural events are from God — we should take care to distinguish truth from deception.

*“The magicians’ counterfeits served to confirm Pharaoh in his unbelief.”* — **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 264)**

**Hardening of Heart**


Upon seeing his magicians' imitations of Aaron’s signs, Pharaoh hardens his heart.

Repeatedly resisting revelations of God's power and warnings leads to spiritual insensitivity and ultimate condemnation.

*“Every display of infinite power rejected, left him more determined in his rebellion”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 268).**

**Victory is Certain**


Moses and Aaron’s obedience and commitment to duty didn’t instantly bring the results that they wanted, but it did set divine plans into motion.

Our best efforts in the mission field may not produce results today, but we can trust God’s process in the affairs of men.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for empowering us to stand boldly in Your name. Help us walk in obedience and discern truth amid deception. Keep our hearts soft and our eyes fixed on You, especially when idols around us fail. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 8


### Summary

God sends the next three plagues: frogs, gnats (lice), and flies. Pharaoh briefly pleads for relief but hardens his heart once each plague ends. The magicians can’t replicate all the plagues, and they acknowledge “this is the finger of God.” God begins to distinguish between Egypt and His people in the land of Goshen.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**God Has Power Over Nature**


The use of frogs, lice and flies to shake Egypt demonstrated God's power and authority over all creation.

God may use not only big things but even small things to bring down human pride and get our attention.

The magician’s inability to replicate other acts shows that Satan’s power is limited, only God is truly sovereign.

*“The magicians were compelled to acknowledge a power superior to their own.”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 265).**

**Repeated Warnings Reveal God’s Patience**


Despite Pharaoh's repeated refusal to let the Israelites go, God provides multiple opportunities for him to appreciate and experience His power.

While God is merciful and patient with us, the consequences of persistent rebellion can be severe. Therefore, it is essential to keep our hearts soft and receptive to God.

**True Acknowledgment of God's Power**


The Egyptian magicians, despite their initial ability to replicate some miracles, come to realize that the third plague is beyond their power and is truly "the finger of God."

However, recognizing God’s work is not enough; we must also acknowledge the God behind that work. Genuine repentance - demonstrated through consistent actions, not merely words - goes beyond just making bargains and is required.

**Consequences of Resistance**


Pharaoh's resistance to God's plan leads to greater suffering and hardship, not only for him but for all of Egypt.

Our rebellion may have dire consequences for generations and communities. We should respond to God's warnings with genuine change, and not confuse His patience with permission to continue in sin.

**Divine Distinction and Protection**


Goshen was spared from the flies. God knows how to protect His people even in widespread trouble.

Just as a clear distinction was made between the Israelites and the Egyptians, so shall it be today and at the end of time.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for Your patience and persistent calling. Help us respond with sincere hearts, true repentance, and trust in Your protection. Teach us to discern truth from deception and to walk humbly in obedience. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 9


### Summary

The God-sent plagues continue: livestock disease, boils, and devastating hail. Pharaoh hardens his heart again despite warnings and worsening conditions. God declares His power and purpose: that His name be proclaimed throughout the earth. Even some Egyptians begin to heed God’s warnings.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**God’s Mercy Is Seen Even in Judgment**


God warned the Pharaoh and the Egyptians before unleashing the plagues on them.

God always provides a way of escape — His judgments are mingled with mercy, allowing us the power of choice unto salvation or condemnation.

*“The Lord’s hand was stretched out in judgment, yet His mercy was still extended”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 267).**

**God Distinguishes Between His People and Others**


The plagues on livestock, boils, and hail specifically afflicted the Egyptians and spared the Israelites and their possessions in Goshen.

Even today, God's people are not exempt from all hardship but are often protected for His purposes.

**God's Purpose**


Everything God does is meant to reveal His character to the world. When God’s warnings and power are made known, even “unexpected hearts” can be reached.

The Great Commission calls us to be active participants in that mission, which may even see some “Egyptians” hearkening to God’s word.

*“Not all were equally hardened. Some among the Egyptians were beginning to recognize the power of the God of Israel.”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 269)**

**Persistent Rebellion Escalates Consequences**


Each plague escalates in severity and impacts Egypt's economic, physiological, and environmental well-being.

God seeks repentance, not destruction; however, ignoring His warnings results in more serious consequences.

We should respond to God’s warnings with genuine change and not confuse His patience with permission to persist in sin.

**God’s Judgments Target False Security**


Livestock, a sign of wealth and security, are struck, the field crops are destroyed and even the magicians are affected by the boils.

Earthly security is fragile, our trust must be in God, not in possessions.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, strip away every false security and teach us to trust You alone. May our lives declare Your name and power to all the earth. Soften our hearts to heed Your warnings, and use us to reach those who are beginning to listen. In Jesus’ name, we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 10


### Summary

God sends the eighth plague, locusts, which devour the remaining crops in Egypt. Pharaoh offered a partial concession, suggesting only the Israelite men could leave. Moses insists all Israelites, including women, children, and livestock, must go. Pharaoh refuses again, and God sends the ninth plague: a darkness that covers Egypt for three days. Pharaoh remains resistant, leading Moses to warn him of the final, most terrible plague: the death of every firstborn son in Egypt. Pharaoh's refusal despite the severity of the plagues is highlighted.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**Stubbornness**


Pharaoh's continued resistance despite counsel from his servants and escalating consequences demonstrates the danger of hardening one's heart against God.

A spirit of resistance and pride led Pharaoh to refuse every offer of release until judgment made obedience unavoidable.

**Going the Whole Mile**


Pharaoh offered partial compromises (letting only the men go, then later without livestock). Don’t offer God half-hearted obedience, He wants total surrender.

Where in life have you been tempted to offer God partial obedience or compromises instead of full surrender?

**God's demonstration of Power and Authority**


The plagues demonstrated God's power and authority over all creation and were not just for the moment.

*“That you may tell your children… what signs I have done… that you may know that I am the Lord.”* (vv. 1-2) They, like our experiences of His interventions, are testimonies for future generations and peoples.

**The Consequences of Resistance**


Pharaoh's resistance leads to greater suffering and hardship, not just for Him and the Egyptians, but even for animals and plant life.

Delayed repentance leads to harsher consequences. Respond early to God’s voice, you may save yourself the pain and grief.

*"Pharaoh's refusal to obey God's commands was a result of his pride and stubbornness, and it ultimately led to his downfall."* **(The SDA Bible Commentary, vol. 1, p. 1145).**

**Divine Enlightenment**


All the Israelites had light in the places where they lived, whilst the Egyptians were in total darkness.

Even in the darkest times, God’s people walk in His light, whilst spiritual darkness follows hardened hearts even as they experience manifestations of God.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank you for your warnings. Teach us full obedience. Help us not to harden our hearts but to respond quickly to Your Word. May we walk in Your light and trust Your power in every trial. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 11


### Summary

Moses delivers God's final warning to Pharaoh: the death of every firstborn of man and beast in Egypt. He emphasizes that the Israelites will definitely leave Egypt, and Pharaoh's continued refusal will result in a devastating blow to the Egyptians. Despite Pharaoh's hardened heart and resistance, God's plan for the Israelites' deliverance will not be thwarted.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**Pharaoh Forfeited Mercy**


By persistent obstinacy, Pharaoh had forfeited the mercy of God. God allows time for repentance but will ultimately act to deliver and judge.

Pharaoh's refusal leads to severe consequences, reminding us of the importance of obedience.

**God's warnings Are Clear and Specific**


Through recurrent warnings and demonstrations, Egypt received many chances to repent.

We can learn from God's warnings to Pharaoh and respond to His warnings in our lives.

Don't take God's patience for granted. Respond before it’s too late.

**God’s Glory Is Revealed**


It is through judgment that God's justice and care for His people are displayed.

He apportions differently according to one’s disposition to His bidding: condemnation or deliverance.

Trust in God’s character, even when He acts in sobering ways.

**God's sovereignty**


God's plan will not be thwarted by human resistance.

Though Pharaoh rejected the warnings and refused to acknowledge the God of Israel, what God had set out to do prevailed.

We can prudently trust in His sovereignty.

*"The final plague would be the most terrible of all, and it would be a demonstration of God's power and authority over all the gods of Egypt."* **(Ellen G. White.  Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 313).**

**Consequences of hardening one's heart**


Pharaoh's hardened heart leads to severe consequences: the loss of firstborn life for the Egyptians.

We must maintain a soft and receptive heart, for a time will come when Egypt shall mourn and Israel shall be preserved by a God who knows, judges, and delivers.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for Your mercy and justice. Help us not to delay when You speak. Keep us faithful under Your protection, and may we live in readiness for Your final deliverance. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 12


### Summary

God institutes the Passover, commanding Israel to sacrifice a lamb and apply its blood to their doorposts. That night, the firstborn of Egypt are struck down, but the Israelites are spared. Pharaoh finally releases the Israelites. They leave Egypt in haste, beginning their journey to freedom.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**God Provides Details**


The Passover involved specific attention to detail. Whilst salvation is free, we must follow God’s way—not our own—for deliverance.

The terms of salvation have been made plain, and are available to every honest seeker of life.

Make time to familiarise yourself with God’s terms of Salvation, and don’t let anyone fool you.

**Deliverance Calls for Obedience**


Every detail of the Passover had to be followed exactly. God honours obedience, especially in matters of faith and worship.

The instructions may have been simple, but those are what marked believers for salvation and protection.

*“The blood upon the doorpost was a symbol of the blood of Christ”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 277).**

⁉️ Has your life been marked by the blood of Jesus, the true lamb?

**God’s Judgment Is Just and Final**


At midnight the Lord struck down all the firstborns in Egypt. God’s justice is real — He is patient, but His judgment will come.

A day is coming, and it won’t be long. *“The destroying angel passed through the land… but no house of the Israelites was visited by death”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 279).**

**God Calls for Remembrance**


God instructed the Israelites to celebrate the Passover to commemorate their deliverance from slavery in Egypt.

Remembering God's works builds faith and anchors identity. We celebrate communion as a reminder of God’s redemptive work through Christ on Calvary.

**God Delivers Completely and Powerfully**


Pharaoh had dallied and bargained with the release of the children of Israel, giving his terms and conditions. But now he urgently sends the Israelites out, and they leave with wealth.

When God delivers, He does so fully—restoring what was lost.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Heavenly Father, thank You for Your grace and love. Help us to live under Your protection and to walk in the freedom You provide. May we always remember Your mighty acts of deliverance. In Jesus’ name, we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 13


### Summary

God commands Israel to consecrate every firstborn – human and animal - to Him and institutes the Feast of Unleavened Bread to commemorate their deliverance from Egyptian bondage. God doesn’t lead them through the shortest route but the safest, with His presence going before them as a pillar of cloud by day and fire by night.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**Remembering God's Deliverance**


God commanded the consecration of the firstborn and the observance of the Feast of Unleavened Bread as perpetual reminders of the Exodus.

Just like the Israelites, we should reflect on God's faithfulness and celebrate His deliverance in our lives, for this helps build our trust in Him.

**God's Visible Guidance**


The pillar of cloud and fire provided tangible proof of God's presence and direction.

We can trust that God guides us through life's challenges, just as He guided the Israelites. The pillar was a symbol of God's presence and guidance.

**God Guides with Purpose, Not Convenience**


*“God did not lead them on the road through the Philistine country… though it was shorter. God knew that they were not prepared for conflict with the Philistines”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 282).**

God’s path may not be the quickest, but it is always the wisest.

**Consecration and Dedication**


Consecrating the firstborn signifies that all life belongs to God because of His redemptive act.

We can dedicate our lives and resources to God, recognizing His sovereignty and provision.

What does consecrating your "first" (time, energy, resources) to God mean to you in a practical sense?

**Following God's Leadership**


The Israelites were to follow God's lead (represented by the pillar).

We should follow God's leadership, trusting in His plan and provision.

⁉️ How can we recognise and trust God's guidance in our lives today, even when we don't see a visible pillar?

**Faith Prepares for the Future**


Moses took Joseph’s bones with him, fulfilling a centuries-old promise. Faith looks forward, trusting God to fulfil His word across generations.

*“Joseph’s faith had been a constant reminder of the promised deliverance”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 273).**


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank you for delivering us from the bondage of sin. Help us to remember Your deliverance in our lives and celebrate Your faithfulness. Guide us in Your ways, and give us the courage to trust in Your provision and sovereignty. May Your presence ever be with us, and may we be a blessing to others. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 14


### Summary

God instructs Israel to camp by the Red Sea, knowing Pharaoh will pursue them. As the Egyptians approached, the Israelites panicked, but God parted the sea through Moses. Israel crosses safely, and the pursuing Egyptian army is destroyed. This miraculous deliverance causes Israel to fear and trust the Lord.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**God Orchestrates Deliverance**


God led the Israelites into a seemingly impossible situation (trapped by the sea with Pharaoh pursuing) to demonstrate His mighty power.

God may allow impossible situations to reveal His glory and strengthen our faith.

We can experience God's deliverance in our lives, whether from physical danger or spiritual bondage.

**(Trust in God) Faith, Not Fear**


The Israelites' fear and murmuring contrasted with Moses' call to stand still and see God's salvation.

In moments of fear, we must choose to trust God’s promises over our panic.

Trusting God's guidance is key, even when the path ahead seems uncertain.

⁉️ How can one cultivate a faith that allows them to "stand still" and trust God when surrounded by perceived threats?

**God Fights for His People**


The destruction of the Egyptian army demonstrates that God actively protects and fights for those who put their trust in Him.

We don’t always need to act — sometimes we need to trust and watch God work.

**God, the Sovereign**


The waters were divided, and the Israelites went through the sea on dry ground. Then, the waters were returned and Egypt was drowned.

God's sovereignty over all creation, and all do according to His bidding. There is no impossible before Him.

**God's Deliverance Inspires Faith and Worship**


Upon their deliverance, Israel believed in God and His servant Moses.

When we witness God’s power, our response should be worship and deeper trust, rather than ascribing acts of grace to personal piety.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for your deliverance. When we feel trapped, remind us that You make a way. Teach us to rest in Your power, trust in Your plans, and worship You through every season. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 15


### Summary

Following their miraculous deliverance, Moses and the Israelites sing a song of praise to the Lord for His victory over Pharaoh’s army. Miriam leads the women in joyful celebration. Later, the people reach Marah, where the water is bitter, but God makes it sweet, showing that He is their healer.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**Celebrate God's Deliverance**


Following their deliverance, the Israelites' first act was worship.

Prioritizing celebrating God's deliverance from whatever bondage - through heartfelt worship and testimony - reflects a recognition of our indebtedness to His hand and His place in our lives.

**Singing unto the Lord**


The song emphasises God's power over nature, His enemies, and His goodness towards humanity.

Even in their newfound freedom and joy, the song is of and about God.

*"The song of Moses and the children of Israel was a testimony to the power and goodness of God, and it has been an inspiration to countless thousands in every age."* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 339).**

⁉️ What stands out in our testimonies and songs?

**Sharing Our Testimony**


The song serves as a testimony to God's power and goodness. We can share our testimonies to inspire others.

*"The deliverance of the children of Israel from Egypt was a demonstration of God's power and love for His people, and it serves as a reminder of His ability to deliver us from any situation."* **(The SDA Bible Commentary, vol. 1, p. 1175).**

**Worship Is for Everyone**


Miriam led the women in worship with tambourines and dancing. Worship is communal and inclusive — both men (v.1) and women (vv. 20-21) may express joyful worship and leadership in the service of God.

**Faith Must Continue After the Miracle**


Shortly after celebrating deliverance, the people soon complained at Marah.

We mustn’t let temporary discomfort erase our trust in God's continued care.

**God Is Our Healer**


God revealed Himself as Yahweh-Rapha (the Lord who heals), providing refreshment and rest in the dry seasons.

Bring your physical, emotional, and spiritual wounds to God — He still heals today.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for Your victories and mercy. Teach us to worship in every season and to trust You as our healer. Sweeten the bitter places in our lives with Your grace. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 16


### Summary

In the wilderness of Sin, the Israelites grumble about the lack of food. God graciously provides manna in the morning and quail in the evening. He instructs them to gather only what they need each day, except a day before the Sabbath when they should gather double. God uses this to test their obedience and prepare them for a life of dependence on Him.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**God Provides Even When We Complain (v. 2–4, 11–13)**


Despite their grumbling, God demonstrates his love and patience by providing the Israelites with food.

God’s grace often meets us even when our attitudes fall short.

Cultivate gratitude and trust God’s provision, even in times of scarcity or uncertainty.

*“God in His great mercy opened the windows of heaven to supply their needs”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 295).**

**God'sFaithful Provision**


God provided daily manna and quail, demonstrating His ability to meet the needs of His people in the wilderness.

God wants us to trust Him for daily provision, not stockpile security.

In life’s lean seasons, learn to trust God more deeply.

**Obedience Matters (v. 19–20, 27–30)**


Disobedience led to spoiled manna (stock-piling), missed rest (Sabbath refrain) and no provision (no manna found).

Small acts of obedience reflect our trust in God’s wisdom.

**The Sabbath Requires Preparation(v. 22–30)**


The manna ceased on the Sabbath, reinforcing the sanctity of the seventh day as a day of rest and worship.

Gathering double on Preparation Day before the Sabbath was a test of faithfulness and a reflection of trust.

Honour the Sabbath by preparing and obeying God’s instructions. It is a divine rhythm for commemoration and worship.

**God’s Faithfulness Should Be Remembered (v. 32–36):**


A jar of manna was preserved as a testimony for future generations.

We must remember and share stories of God’s provision.

*“The memorial was to remind them of God’s care and their dependence on Him”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 296).**


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for Your daily provision. Teach us to trust You in every season and obey Your Word fully. Help us to honour Your Sabbath and grow in gratitude. In Jesus’ name, Amen.

## Exodus 17


### Summary

Journeying through the wilderness, Israel again complains — this time for water. God instructs Moses to strike a rock at Horeb, and water miraculously flows out. Later, Amalek attacks Israel, but Moses intercedes with uplifted hands with the help of Aaron and Hur while Joshua leads the battle. Israel prevails, and Moses builds an altar, naming it "The Lord is my Banner."


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**God Provides in Dry Places (vv. 1-6)**


God miraculously provided water from a rock in the desert wilderness.

He can bring provision from the most unlikely sources when we trust Him in our driest times or most challenging circumstances.

**Complaining Reveals a Lack of Trust (v. 2–3, 7)**


After the Red Sea parting, the manna, and the Marah waters, they still asked “Is the Lord among us or not?”

Grumbling often stems from forgetting God’s past faithfulness and not trusting Him enough to deal with our circumstances.

*“They distrusted the providence of God and murmured against His servant”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 297).**

**The Importance of Support (vv. 10-12)**


When Moses held up his staff (prayer) during the battle against Amalek, Israel would succeed. When he tired-up, Aaron and Hur came to brace him up.

Leaders, like everyone else,  also need the support of others to remain strong in their calling.

**Spiritual Struggles (vv. 11-13)**


When we face spiritual struggles, we can trust in God's power and authority to overcome them.

God's power is available to help us prevail against spiritual battles. ⏩ Prayer + Action = SUCCESS.

**A God Who Can Account (vv. 14-16)**


The Lord commanded that the victory be recorded, not for His own sake, but for Joshua's.

How remarkable: God is not too great to be concerned about having His promises remembered.

We can have confidence in a God who is unafraid to remind us of the promises He has made.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for being our Provider. Help us to pray instead of complain, and to uplift one another in the battles of life. May we trust You to bring life even from dry places. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 18


### Summary

Jethro, Moses' father-in-law, visits the Israelite camp, bringing Moses' wife and sons. After hearing about God’s deliverance, Jethro praises God and offers sacrifices in gratitude. Observing Moses overwhelmed with judging the people, Jethro advises him to delegate authority to capable men, establishing a system of leadership and justice among the Israelites.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**Family Relations Matter (vv. 1-7)**


Jethro visiting Moses and the Israelites, bringing Moses' wife and sons to him speaks to the amiable relationship between the in-laws, and possibly the importance of a family being together.

Whilst circumstances may bring separation, it is ideal that a family be together.

**Testimonies Inspire Faith (vv. 8-12)**


Jethro rejoiced and worshipped upon hearing God’s works for Israel.

Sharing testimonies of God’s interventions in our lives can lead others to worship and believe.

**Wisdom from Outside (vv. 13-26)**


Great leaders are teachable and open to wise correction.

Moses, God’s chosen leader, humbly received and implemented counsel from his non-Israelite father-in-law.

God often uses others to speak wisdom into our lives — humility receives it.

*"Moses' willingness to listen to Jethro's advice demonstrates the importance of humility and openness to guidance in leadership."*  **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 366)**

**Importance of Delegation (vv. 17-23)**


Moses was overburdened, and Jethro’s advice introduced a structure for judging different level of matters.

Delegating authority to capable individuals for greater efficacy benefits not only the leader and the led, but also empowers others for fulfilling service.

*"The system of leadership established by Moses was a demonstration of God's wisdom and guidance in the organization of His people."* **(Ellen G. White: The SDA Bible Commentary, vol. 1, p. 1200)**

**Establishing Justice (vv. 21-22)**


Integrity is essential in those entrusted with leadership.

The selection of “capable God-fearing, trustworthy, and dishonest-gain-hating men” facilitated timely justice and fairness among the Israelites.

Justice delayed is justice denied.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for the people You place in our lives to offer wisdom and support. Help us to be humble enough to listen, wise enough to delegate, and faithful enough to lead with integrity. Teach us to share our testimonies boldly and to build up others in their calling. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 20


### Summary

God speaks directly to Israel, giving the Ten Commandments - His moral law. The people, terrified by the divine presence, ask Moses to speak on God's behalf. God establishes His desire for holiness and reverence among His people.


### Key Lessons


**✍🏾Talking Points**


1️⃣.	The Ten Commandments - Universal and Eternal*

The Ten Commandments reflect God’s character and His will for humanity. They reflect His holiness, justice, and love; and are a transcript of God's character.

These principles are still relevant today as a guide for righteous living. Studying and living the commandments helps us grow in Christ-likeness.

2️⃣.	Prioritizing God*

The first four commandments emphasize exclusive worship of God and honouring Him above all. ⏩God demands exclusive devotion—idolatry in any form separates us from Him.

*“Anything which tends to abate our love for God or to interfere with the service due Him, becomes thereby an idol.”* **(Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 305).**

3️⃣.	Sabbath - A Gift of Rest and Relationships.*

The Sabbath is a weekly reminder of God’s sovereignty as Creator and Sustainer.

Create intentional Sabbath boundaries: unplug from work emails, social media, and secular distractions, dedicating the day to worship, family, and service; and focus on activities that bring God into conscious focus in a special way.

4️⃣.	Principles for Human Relationships (vv. 12-17)* #️⃣Commandments 5-10 provide foundational principles for a just and loving society, emphasizing respect for life, family, property, truth, and human dignity.

Love for God is reflected in our respecting others, including their lives, relationships, and property.

5️⃣.	Moral Integrity*

The commandments promote moral integrity, exposing sin and pointing to our need for Christ, our Savior. They show us who we are—sinners in need of a Savior. They are not a burden, but a gift that drives us to the cross, where grace abounds.

*"The law of God is a transcript of His character, and it is a guide for His people."* **(The SDA Bible Commentary, vol. 1, p. 1215).**

## Exodus 21


### Summary

God gives laws focusing on social justice. These include instructions on fair treatment of servants, consequences for personal injury, and property rights. These laws set principles of fairness, responsibility, and restitution within the community.


### Key Lessons


**✍🏾Talking Points**


1️⃣.	God’s Laws Build Communities*

The laws provided were not arbitrary; instead, they were intended to reflect God’s commitment to justice and fairness in everyday life, creating a just society.

By upholding God’s principles of justice, mercy and order in our interactions, we build healthy communities.

*“The laws given to Israel were to guard the rights of all and to promote peace and righteousness”* **(Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 311).**

2️⃣.	Servitude Must Be Tempered with Compassion (v. 1–11)*

In contrast to the prevailing practices of the time, Hebrew servants were to be treated with dignity and released in the seventh year.

God values freedom and fairness. Hence, the Hebrew system of servitude was designed to teach kindness and responsibility.

⁉️How do we treat those under our authority or care (employees, children, etc.)? Are we upholding principles of fairness and dignity?

3️⃣.	Life Is Sacred and Must Be Protected (v. 12–17)*

The laws concerning murder, assault, and parental respect show the value God places on life and the family circle.

God’s justice system upholds the sanctity of life and the importance of family order.

4️⃣.	Intent Matters in Justice (v. 13–14)*

Distinctions are made between accidental and intentional harm. God’s justice considers motive and context, not just outcomes.

5️⃣.	Restitution and Responsibility (v. 18–36)*

A just society requires personal responsibility and restitution. Whether through injury, negligence, or property damage, people are to be held responsible.

This teaches responsibility for our actions and the willingness to make things right when we cause harm.

*“The law taught that every man was to be held accountable for his actions”* **(Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 310).**

## Exodus 22


### Summary

God provides additional laws on protecting property, assisting the poor, social responsibility, and ethical behavior.


### Key Lessons


*God provides additional laws on protecting property, assisting the poor, social responsibility, and ethical behavior.*

Talking Points*

1️⃣.	Restitution for Wrongdoing (vv. 1-15)*

When we have wronged or caused harm to others, we should seek to make restitution, thereby promoting healing and reconciliation.

Justice encompasses not only punishment but also making things right.

2️⃣.	Personal Responsibility Builds Trust (v. 6–15)*

God holds people accountable for negligence or damage to others’ property.

We are to exercise due care for other people’s properties in our care or custody. This reflects our respect for their sweat and assets, translating into interpersonal trust.

3️⃣.	Holiness Affects Every Area of Life (v. 18–20, 31)*

God commands purity in worship, relationships, and daily conduct.

Holiness isn’t just spiritual - it touches how we live, work, and relate to our immediate and extended environment, be it human, animate or inanimate.

4️⃣.	Care for the Vulnerable (vv. 21-27)*

The commands to help widows, orphans, and strangers condemns the spirit of greed, extortion, and the passion for gain at the expense of others (Prophets and Kings, pp. 648-652).

We should be sympathetic to the needs of others and never ignore their cries for help. Instead of taking advantage of those facing difficulties, we should show compassion and provide practical assistance to marginalized individuals.

5️⃣.	Revering God and Respecting Authority (v. 28)*

Our words and actions should reflect a recognition of God and His supreme authority over our lives.

God establishes governing authorities (Romans 13:1–2), and while they are not above critique, outright rebellion or contempt can lead to chaos.

Christians are called to engage with leaders wisely - honouring just governance while standing firm against ungodly decrees (Acts 5:29).

6️⃣.	Generosity and Worship (vv. 29–30)*

God prioritizes devotion over possessions. Thus, He demands the first fruits as a sign of trust.

Ethical living includes stewardship - giving God our best (time, resources, worship) rather than leftovers.

## Exodus 23


### Summary

God outlines various laws that cover the just treatment of all individuals with justice regardless of status, showing compassion to the poor and vulnerable (foreigners, widows), observing the Sabbath's rest, and celebrating specific festivals. God promises to guide and bless the Israelites as they follow His laws.


### Key Lessons


**✍🏾Talking Points**


1️⃣	Justice and Fairness for All (vv. 1-9)*

God explicitly commands just and fair treatment for everyone, including foreigners.

For justice to prevail, prejudice must be avoided. We should treat others with respect and dignity regardless of economic status. A court of law is not the place for sentiment.

2️⃣.	Care for the Vulnerable (vv. 4-5, 9, 11-12)*

Specific instructions are provided to demonstrate kindness and compassion towards the poor and those in need.

We can show compassion to those in need by volunteering or being a good neighbour, even to those with whom we disagree.

3️⃣.	Sabbath Observance (vv. 10-12)*

God reaffirms the Sabbath as a day to pause from “our labour” for both people and animals, even extending to the land.

Observation of this rest reflects a recognition of God as Lord.

*“The Sabbath and the sabbatical year were designed to teach trust in God and care for others”* **(Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 311).**

4️⃣.	Community and Celebration (vv. 14-19)*

Observing festivals helps build relationships, and fosters community identity and remembrance of God's acts.

Engaging in regular, joyful worship keeps us connected to God and His provision.

5️⃣.	God's Promise of Guidance and Blessing(vv. 20-33)*

God promised that obedience to these laws would bring divine guidance and protection.

When we faithfully walk in obedience, we open up doors to His favour.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, Thank You for Your laws that teach us justice, mercy, and worship. Help us to live with integrity, to rest in Your provision, and to celebrate Your goodness. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 24


### Summary

Moses confirms God's covenant with Israel. The people promise to obey. Moses, Aaron, and the elders ascend the mountain and witness God's glory. Moses, with Joshua as his aide, remains on the mountain for 40 days to receive God’s laws.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**Covenant Ratification**


The covenant was sealed with blood, symbolizing consecration and commitment.

Jesus’ blood seals our new covenant; we are bound to Him by love and obedience.

We can make a personal commitment to follow God and obey His laws, deepening our relationship with Him.

**The Role of Scripture and Divine Law**


Moses writes down God’s words (v 4) and reads them to the people (v 7).

We uphold the authority of the permanent written Word **(Isaiah 8:20),** mirroring Moses’ recording of God’s laws.

Just as Moses publicly proclaimed God’s words, believers should prioritize expository preaching and Bible Study **(Acts 17:11).**

**Obedience and Commitment (vv. 3,7)**


The people solemnly pledged themselves to accept and obey all the conditions of the covenant.

A relationship with God involves both hearing His Word and committing to obey it.

⁉️ What does it mean for you to be in covenant with God today?

**Experiencing God With Us (vv. 9-11)**


Moses, Aaron, and the elders ascend the mountain, where they see a vision of God and experience a sense of awe and reverence.

God has always desired and sought an intimate relationship with His people.

Just as the elders glimpsed God’s glory, we also look forward to seeing Him face-to-face at the Second Coming **(Revelation 22:4).**

⁉️ What have you done to seek to experience God's presence in your life?

**Worship and Sacrifice**


Offerings symbolize commitment and seeking God's favour. We can offer sacrifices of praise, thanksgiving, and service.

⁉️ How can I prioritize God's will and plan for my life, especially when faced with decisions that require sacrifice or self-denial?

**Moses’ Commission (vv. 12-18)**


Moses is called higher into the cloud of God’s glory “to receive the tables stone”: to receive teachings and commandments for Israel.

God calls us up from our peers, so He may reveal Himself better for His purpose. The call to "come up" is not for privilege—but for greater service in the Great Controversy.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for inviting us into covenant with You. Help us take it seriously, trust in Christ’s sacrifice, and grow deeper in Your presence. Teach us to wait on You. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.

## Exodus 25


### Summary

God gives Moses detailed instructions for the construction of the Tabernacle, a sacred space for worship and communion with God. Specifics of the materials needed and the designs of specific items, including the Ark of the Covenant, the mercy seat, and the table of showbread are detailed. Israel is invited to contribute the materials needed for the construction.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**Worship Requires Willing Hearts (v. 1–2)**


The call for contributions of specific materials specifies that people would give willingly.

True worship and giving come from a willing, generous heart that recognizes all it has come from Him.

*“The Lord does not force the obedience or the gifts of His people. He accepts only that which is given freely and cheerfully”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 343).**

**God Desires to Dwell Among His People (v. 8, 22)**


The Tabernacle was designed as God's dwelling place among His people.

God’s ultimate desire is relationship — He wants to be with us.

**Every Detail Matters to God (v. 9-21, 23-340)**


The sanctuary design was precise and symbolic. God cares about excellence and intentionality in our worship and service.

Even our service to God should be done with attention to detail and excellence, not with an “at least something has been done” attitude.

**Sacred Objects Point to Jesus**


Each item in the sanctuary foreshadows Christ’s ministry. The sanctuary helps us understand salvation and Christ’s work for us today **(Hebrews 8:1–5).**

(i)** **The Ark: God’s Presence and Mercy( vv. 10-22)*

The Ark contained the law and was covered by the mercy seat. Justice and mercy are united in God’s character — He invites us to live likewise.

(ii)** **The Table of Showbread Symbolizes God’s Provision (v. 23–30)*

God continually provides for our physical and spiritual needs.

(iii)** **The Lampstand Reflects God’s Light and Presence (v. 31–40)*

God’s presence brings light to our lives and calls us to shine for others.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for desiring to dwell with us. Make our hearts Your sanctuary. Teach us to give joyfully, serve excellently, and reflect Your justice and mercy. In Jesus’ name, Amen.

## Exodus 27


### Summary

God provides instructions for constructing the bronze altar, the courtyard of the Tabernacle, and the oil for the lamp, specifying the materials and designs to be used.

Bronze, linen, and oil were common materials used for holy functions. God often uses the ordinary things dedicated to Him in extraordinary ways for His glory and mission.

## Exodus 32


### Summary

While Moses is on Mount Sinai, the people grow impatient and pressure Aaron to make a golden calf. They worship it, breaking their covenant with God. God saw their idolatry and rebellion and told Moses He would destroy them, but Moses interceded on their behalf. Moses returned to the camp, saw the calf and the revelry, became angry, broke the stone tablets, burned the calf, ground it to powder, and made the Israelites drink it. The Levites sided with Moses and executed about three thousand men. God sent a plague upon the people.


### Key Lessons


Talking Points*

**Impatience Leads to Sin (vv. 1-6)**


The Israelites’ impatience with Moses’ absence led them to ‘create a god in their own image’.

Spiritual impatience leads to compromise – seeking for quick fixes rather than waiting on God’s timing.

When we lose sight of God’s timing, we risk turning to substitutes - career, relationships, or materialism - for security and identity.

Faith is trusting God to come through for us even when we may not see Him.

**Leaders must stand firm**


Aaron’s compromise (vv. 2-5) shows how quickly religious leaders can falter under pressure **(SDA Bible Commentary Vol. 1, p. 659).**

Like Aaron, pastors, elders and spiritual leaders can water down truth to please crowds.

*“Aaron’s sin was in yielding to the demands of the people. He had been left as a guardian, yet he failed to restrain them.”* **(Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 323)**

**Intercession Matters (vv. 7-14)**


Moses’ fervent prayer deflected God’s wrath and secured mercy for Israel.

God’s justice is real, but His mercy is greater - He relented because of Moses’ prayer.

Intercessory prayer is powerful — like Moses, we must pray for others, even when they sin.

True leadership is selfless, pleading on behalf of the erring, just as Moses foreshadows Jesus mediation on our behalf in the heavenly sanctuary today.

**Sin has Consequences (vv. 15-29)**


The broken tablets, the plague, and the judgment show the serious consequences of rebellion against God.

God relented, but the guilty still faced judgment. Grace doesn’t cancel all earthly consequences.

We reap what we sow, but can still find mercy.

⁉️ How do you respond to the consequences of your past choices?

**Atonement and Sacrificial Love (vv. 30–35)**


Moses offers himself in place of the people - a foreshadowing of Christ.

True spiritual leadership involves sacrificial love.

⁉️ Are we willing to bear burdens for others?


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, forgive us for times we’ve doubted, compromised, or placed other things before You. Give us the courage to stand for truth and the love to intercede for others. Help us trust Your timing and honour Your holiness. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.

God honoured that desire. When we hunger for God, He reveals Himself more deeply.

*“Every morning a new, consecrate yourself to God for that day. Let this be your very first work.”* **(Ellen G. White: Steps to Christ, p. 70)**

**Obedience Brings God’s Presence (vv. 3-7, 19-33)**


Moses did all “as the Lord commanded,” and God's glory filled the tabernacle.

Obedient living invites the abiding presence of God into our lives.

*“Exact obedience is required, and those who say it is not necessary to keep the law of God are not acquainted with Christ.”* **(Ellen G. White: Faith and Works, p. 52)**

**Anointing and Consecration (vv. 9–16)**


The tabernacle and priests were anointed and set apart for holy use.

God calls us to be consecrated, set apart for His purposes.

Being a royal priesthood, are we living lives marked by spiritual dedication?

**God’s Glory Fills the Tabernacle (vv. 34–35)**


God’s presence was so powerful that even Moses couldn’t enter.

When we prepare a place for God with obedience and reverence, He fills it with His glory. Are we making space for His presence?

**Divine Guidance (vv. 36–38)**


The cloud of God’s presence led Israel through their journey. God still leads His people today.

*"As the Israelites journeyed, they were guided by the cloud. So the Christian is to move only as the Lord directs.”* **(Ellen G. White: The Desire of Ages, p. 208)**

⁉️ Are we watching for His direction and moving only when He moves?


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our heavenly Father, thank You for desiring to dwell among Your people. Consecrate our hearts and lives for Your glory. Help us to live obediently and walk in step with Your Spirit. May Your presence guide and sustain us always. In Jesus’ name we pray, Amen.
