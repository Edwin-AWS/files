# Leviticus

## Introduction

🆕 Book: Leviticus  
  
Introducing Leviticus
The Hebrew Title is Wayyiqra, meaning "And He Called" which is the first sentence of the book ("Now the Lord CALLED Moses") as was the custom then of naming a book after the first words of that book.

➡️ Leviticus is referred to as the "Law of the Priests" or "Law of the Offerings" in the Talmud (which is a Focal text of Rabbinic Judaism that discusses and interprets Jewish law, ethics and traditions. And comprises of the oral law (Mishnah), and commentaries and discussions (Gemara).
➡️The Latin Vulgate (a 4th Century Latin translation of the Bible, primarily translated by Jerome), derived the name Leviticus from the word Leuitikon, meaning "That which pertains to the Levites" in the Septuagint (which is the Greek Translation of the Old Testament/Hebrew Bible).

✍🏽 Author 
#️⃣ Moses wrote the book of Leviticus.

📆 Date
#️⃣ Around 1405 B.C.

🔥 Setting 
#️⃣ The book is penned while the Israelites were encamped at the foot of Mount Sinai and most likely finished off before Moses' death.

🗣️ Themes
(i) Holiness
The newly redeemed nation is trained on how to be righteous just as God is.
(ii) Worship and Sacrifice
God teaches Israelites how to fellowship with Him through offerings and obedience.
(iii) Priestly Duties
Roles and responsibilities of priests were outlined.
(iv) Community and Morality
Guidelines for social behavior, justice and moral conduct.

☦️ Christ in Leviticus 
#️⃣ The book has references to the Person and work of Jesus inclusive of:
1️⃣ The 5 Offerings 
(a) The burnt offering signified Christ's complete offering in submission to the Father
(b) The meal (grain/food) offering pointed to Jesus' sinless service
(c) Peace offering typified the fellowship believers have with God through the cross
(d) Sin offering symbolized the Savior as our guilt bearer 
(e) Trespass Offering prefigured the Redeemer's restitution for sin's effects 

2️⃣ The High Priest
#️⃣ Aaron the High Priest's role and duties typified our heavenly High Priest.

3️⃣ The Seven Feasts
(a) Passover pointed to the substitutionary death of the Lamb of God. Jesus died on the day of Passover.
(b) Unleavened Bread signified the blameless life of Christ and the holy walk of a believer (1 Corinthians 5:6-8).
(c) First fruits alluded to Christ's resurrection as the firstborn from the dead (1 Corinthians 15:20-23). Jesus rose on the day of First fruits.
(d) Pentecost shadowed the Holy Spirit's outpouring and the harvest of souls
(e) Trumpets forewarned the impending Day of Atonement hence typified the 3 Angel's Message (Revelation 14:6-12).
(f) Day of Atonement was a precursor to the Investigative/Pre-advent Judgement (1 Peter 4:17, 18).
(g) Tabernacles prefigured the Second Advent (we are sojourners on this earth) (1 Thessalonians 4:13-18).

💡 Overview
➡️ In Exodus, Israel is redeemed and established as a kingdom of priests and a holy nation. In Leviticus, Israel is trained to live up to their priestly call. 
➡️ They have been emancipated in Exodus and led into service and dedication in Leviticus. Hence Exodus is Israel's Redemption and Leviticus, is her Sanctification.
➡️ Leviticus is a guide for worship, service, and obedience to God.
*️⃣ It's stated that it took 1 night for God to get Israel out of Egypt, but it took Him 40 years to get Egypt out of the Israelites.

*️⃣ Having been redeemed, may we allow God to work His Sanctification in us.

#️⃣ Leviticus is divided into 2 Major sections:
1. Sacrifice (1-17)
2. Sanctification (18-27).

🛐 Prayer
Our dear heavenly Father, thank you for redeeming us on the cross. May you help us to live holy lives before you. Amen.

## Leviticus 1


### Summary

The Lord gives Moses instructions on Burnt Offerings.


### Key Lessons


**Offering Essence**


1️⃣ Offering Essence

➡️ Offering (Hebrew qorban from the verb qarab) meaning "to draw near" or "to approach."

*️⃣ Thus offerings are a sign of our drawing close to God as a response of Him having drawn closer to us first.

*️⃣ We love Him for He first loved us.

**Kinds of Burnt Offerings**


2️⃣ Kinds of Burnt Offerings

➡️ There were 2 types, obligatory and voluntary

➡️ Obligatory burnt offerings, were carried out at stated times by the priests on behalf of the congregation, e.g.

(i) morning and evening sacrifices (Exodus 29:38-42),

(ii) Sabbath burnt offerings (Numbers 28:9,10) and

(iii) on all feasts (Numbers 28:11-29:39).

*️⃣ These offerings were purchased by money collected from the whole of Israel and covered the sins of the whole nation up to the point that the individual comes in person with their own sacrifice.

➡️ Voluntary Burnt Offerings were presented by an individual at any time and had to adhere to the strict regulations given for sacrifices.

*️⃣ Christ has provided a way by which sinners can approach him and be saved.

**Sacrifices, a type of Christ**


3️⃣ Sacrifices, a type of Christ

➡️ When sacrificing Burnt Offerings, the whole animal was completely consumed by fire and no flesh was taken or eaten.

*️⃣ This represented that Christ would give himself wholly as a sacrifice on Calvary.

️⃣ We too are to consecrate our lives wholly to him as living sacrifices. (Romans 12:1)*

➡️ It was supposed to be a male animal without blemish and symbolized Christ our perfect and complete sacrifice.

**Equality in Sacrifices**


4️⃣ Equality in Sacrifices

➡️ Burnt Offerings could come in form of Bullocks, Lambs, goats or fowls (turtle doves or pigeons), depending with the ability of the offerer.

*️⃣ The wealthy could bring Bullocks while the poor could bring pigeons, and yet be equally accepted and forgiven by God.

*️⃣ Yet both rich and poor were to bring the very best of what they could afford and not maimed or defective gifts.

*️⃣ God requires of everyone our very best not left overs yet He accepts all regardless of financial standing in society

**The Consequences of sin**


5️⃣ The Consequences of sin

➡️ The sinner was to confess his sins over the offering and slaughter the offering himself before the priest would take over to make atonement of sin.

*️⃣ It is a lesson book to all that sin is expensive and as the innocent animal was slain on behalf of the sinner, so did Jesus though sinless die on behalf of the guilty.

**Continual Intercession**


6️⃣ Continual Intercession

➡️ The morning and evening sacrifices were done in such a way that the burnt offerings were put on a slow fire, such that the morning sacrifice would keep burning till the evening sacrifice. And in like manner the evening burnt offering would keep burning till the morning one.

*️⃣ Hence all day long, there was always an offering being burnt on behalf of all the Israelites.

*️⃣ Christ ever stands to make Intercession on our behalf. What great love and privilege❗


> 🙏 **Prayer:** In the name of the Father, Son and the Holy Spirit, may we present our bodies as living sacrifices wholly acceptable before the throne of mercy. Amen.

## Leviticus 2


### Summary

Prescriptions on grain offerings


### Key Lessons


**Grain Offerings**


1️⃣ Grain Offerings

➡️ Also called Food/Cereal/Meal Offerings.

➡️ It could be either flour or grain with oil and frankincense.

➡️ A handful of the offering was burnt on the altar and the rest was shared equally among the priests for their food.

*️⃣ The Lord never forgets the sustenance of his messengers.

**Types of Grain Offerings**


2️⃣ Types of Grain Offerings

➡️ Just like Burnt Offerings, grain offerings also had 2 types, obligatory (brought at designated times on behalf of the nation) and voluntary (at any time by individuals).

➡️ The shewbread was the most important obligatory food offering. These were 12 loaves of bread put while fresh every Sabbath (denoting the fresh Word of God that should permeate the holy hours and our pulpits).

➡️ The old bread would be taken out and eaten by the priests as the fresh one was put on the table to replace it.

*️⃣ Hence, just as there was always a burnt offering being sacrificed on the altar, so there was always the shewbread on the table.

*️⃣ The Word of God should always be presented to all mankind continually.

**Drink Offering**


3️⃣ Drink Offering

➡️ The Drink Offering was made with Morning and Evening Sacrifices.

➡️ Wine was used as a drink offering poured unto to the Lord. (Exodus 29:40; Numbers 15:5).

✍🏽 Significance of the offerings:

➡️ The shewbread, or "bread of the presence" pointed to Him who "ever liveth to make intercession" for us, and to "the living bread which came down from heaven" (Hebrews 7:25; John 6:51).

➡️ Wine represented Jesus' shed blood on the cross for the remission of our sins (1 Corinthians 11:24, 25).

➡️ Oil typified the Holy Spirit needed to subdue us unto salvation.

➡️ Fine flour represented humanity's lifework. Flour, a product of crushed grain, symbolized how Christ would be crushed as a sacrifice for our redemption. Similarly, we are perfected through the grinding mill of suffering, may we come out refined and humbled.

*️⃣ Just as grain is value added to produce fine flour, so our God given opportunities, gifts and talents are to be perfected, and value added as a sweet smelling aroma before the Lord.

➡️ Frankincense produced a sweet scent when burnt, to symbolize the acceptance of the offerings by the Lord.

*️⃣ The prayers and worship of the saints ascend as sweet swelling incense before the throne of God.

**Prohibition of leaven and honey (vs 11)**


4️⃣ Prohibition of leaven and honey (vs 11)

➡️ Both leaven and honey combined with offerings would bring fermentation, especially in the production of vinegar back then.

➡️ We are warned against the evil influences of sin, i.e. hypocrisy, malice and wickedness represented by fermentation (Luke 12:1; 1 Corinthians 5:8).

**Injunction on Salt (vs 13).**


5️⃣ Injunction on Salt (vs 13).

➡️ All the grain offerings were to be seasoned with salt.

➡️ Salt as a preservative and purifying agent against fermentation, corruption and decay, was mandatory in these sacrifices.

*️⃣ Thus, the purifying and preserving tenets of holiness and truth are a requirement in our daily walk with Christ.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father in heaven, thank you for Christ whose life was crushed (as did the grain offerings) and He offered to us the promise of life eternal, may we likewise present our lives as a sacrifice unto Thee. Amen.

## Leviticus 3


### Summary

God tells Moses on how Peace offerings were to be offered.


### Key Lessons


**Peace Offerings**


1️⃣ Peace Offerings

➡️ The offerer was to bring either a male or female lamb or goat without blemish. He would lay his hands on the animal and kill it, and the priest would make atonement by sprinkling the blood on the the base of the altar.

➡️ This offering pointed to Christ,  the Prince of Peace who is a perfect and blameless gift from heaven for the remission of our sins.

*️⃣ Peace Offering (Hebrew, shelem) which means "to make peace," "to be at peace" or to make restitution.

*️⃣ Hence the offering meant that one is now at peace with God and man.

*️⃣ We are to make peace with our maker and our fellow men.

⏩ "Christ is "the Prince of Peace" (Isaiah 9:6), and it is His mission to restore to earth and heaven the peace that sin has broken. "Being justified by faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ." (Romans 5:1). Whoever consents to renounce sin and open his heart to the love of Christ, becomes a partaker of this heavenly peace. here is no other ground of peace than this." {Ellen G. White: Thoughts From the Mount of Blessing p. 27}

**Celebrations of Unity**


2️⃣ Celebrations of Unity

➡️ Oftentimes, the peace offering was preceded by a sin offering and a burnt offering. Hence having been forgiven, one would offer an offering of peace, which in turn was followed by a communal meal which was held within the sanctuary court.

➡️ Having been justified, a celebration followed as the offerer invited friends, relatives, servants and Levites to eat with him in consummating a restored relationship between God and men and between man and man.

*️⃣ It is a cause of jubilation and exuberant joy when broken relationships are mended.

**Prohibition on Fat**


3️⃣ Prohibition on Fat

➡️ Having been instructed to burn all the fat in the offerings on the altar, God forbade the consumption of fat and blood.

➡️ For the life of the creatures is in the fat and blood.

*️⃣ Much untold suffering and sickness today is caused by overlooking this injunction and consuming that which the Lord prohibited.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father who art in heaven, we praise you for the peace granted us by the death of Jesus Christ. Amen.

## Leviticus 4


### Summary

The sin offering for different people.


### Key Lessons


**Sin offering**


1️⃣ Sin offering

➡️ Was meant for sins committed ignorantly and having known of the sin committed unintentionally, the individual was to bring a sin offering.

*️⃣ Similarly, when we sin unintentionally, having realized our error, we are to request for forgiveness

**As People Differ**


2️⃣ As People Differ

➡️ The anointed Priest (High Priest), just like the whole Israelite congregation required a young bullock for a sin offering.

➡️ A ruler was to bring a male kid of a goat. While any common person required a female goat or lamb.

*️⃣ The gravity of any sin is based on the light one has received.

*️⃣ With all the light at our disposal, let us then walk circumspectly.

**A shadow of Christ**


3️⃣ A shadow of Christ

➡️ The sinner, laid his hands on the animal and confessed his sins, hence transferring his sins to the innocent sacrifice.

️⃣ This was a pointer to Christ, who though he knew no sin, became sin for us (2 Corinthians 5:21).*

➡️ Having burnt all the fat on the altar, the whole animal would be carried out side the camp and burnt (Hebrews 13:11-12)

**Application of blood in our lives**


4️⃣ Application of blood in our lives

➡️ The slaughtering of the animals by the sinner, pointed to the fact that it was the sinner's fault and responsibility that caused our Savior to die.

*️⃣ The slaughtering of the animal alone was not enough, the process was only complete after the priest applied the blood on the horns of the altar or sprinkled it before the veil to make atonement.

*️⃣ In like manner Christ's death on the cross is only complete as he ministers in the heavenly sanctuary thus applying his blood to mediate for all the repentant sinners.

*️⃣ Without applying Christ's blood in our lives, we make of no effect his great sacrifice on the cross.

*️⃣ We are to submit and surrender our lives and allow the Savior to cleanse us of all unrighteousness. That's when and only when we make full use of the salvation so freely given to us.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father in heaven, may the sacrifice on the cross, and the mediatorial service in the heavenly sanctuary cleanse us from our transgressions. Amen.

## Leviticus 5


### Summary

Instructions concerning Trespass Offerings.


### Key Lessons


**The Sin of Silence**


1️⃣ The Sin of Silence

➡️ When one refuses to testify as a witness, yet has first hand information, it constituted sin and had to be atoned for with a tresspass offering.

*️⃣ Failure to speak out the truth for expediency, fear, quest for popularity etc is a transgression to be repented of.

**Commission of Unintentional Sins**


2️⃣ Commission of Unintentional Sins

➡️ When one sinned ignorantly, the moment he realized his error, he was supposed to bring a tresspass offering.

*️⃣ Ignorance is no excuse for sin.

*️⃣ Whenever we recognize that we have erred, may God help us to acknowledge our sin, repent and apologize.

**The Tresspass Offering**


3️⃣ The Tresspass Offering

➡️ One was to bring a lamb or a kid of a goat. If he could not afford, he would bring  2 turtle doves but if still unable, he would come with 2 pigeons, (one for a sin offering and the other for a burnt offering). In a case where one was unable to bring even the 2 birds, they were required to bring a small portion of fine flour (with no oil and frankincense, lest it would become a Food Offering).

*️⃣ God in His mercy simplified the process by which humanity could be saved regardless of one's social status. Therefore there is no excuse.

**The principle of Restitution**


4️⃣ The principle of Restitution

➡️ If one defrauded, stole or withheld another's belongings. The offender was to bring back to the owner and make restitution by adding a fifth (20%) to all he had unjustly taken. After which he was to bring a tresspass offering.

*️⃣ Genuine Repentance is shown by restoring what we withheld deceitfully, rectifying and mending all the broken relationships.

➡️ Herein is included the withholding of holy things, i.e. Tithes and Offerings, we need to make restitution and restore that which belongs to the Lord.

⏩ _"If in our ignorance we make missteps, the Saviour does not forsake us. We need never feel that we are alone.... There is not a sorrow, not a grievance, not a human weakness, for which He has not provided a remedy." {Ellen G. White: Ministry of Healing pp249}.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our dear heavenly Father,  open our hearts and mind to acknowledge our sins and to repent. Amen.

## Leviticus 7


### Summary

Laws for the Tresspass, Sin, and Peace Offerings.


### Key Lessons


**Instructions on Tresspass and Sin Offerings**


1️⃣ Instructions on Tresspass and Sin Offerings

➡️ The way these two offerings were offered was similar, with an exception on the ministration of the blood.

➡️ With the Sin Offering, blood was applied on the horns of the altar of burnt offerings, while for a Tresspass Offering, blood was sprinkled on the altar round about.

➡️ Sin offering (Hebrew: "chatta't"): was an offering for unintentional sins and impurities. The aim was atonement and purification.

➡️ Trespass offering or guilty offering (Hebrew: "asham"): was for intentional sin, and wrong doing against others, property, and sacred things, thus requiring restitution plus penalty.

*️⃣ All sin must be confessed, followed by repentance.

**Fellowship and Hospitality, twin blessings.**


2️⃣ Fellowship and Hospitality, twin blessings.

➡️ The flesh of the Peace Offering was to be eaten on the same day.

*️⃣ This was meant to encourage sanitation, so that the meat would be eaten while still fresh.

*️⃣ Secondly, the offerer would not be able to consume all that meat in one day alone, hence prompting him to invite others for a fellowship meal. Hence this was made a special family gathering time to eat and celebrate.

*️⃣ The stranger, the poor and the needy were also invited. The presence of a levite solemnized the occasion and gave him time to instruct all who were gathered in the paths of righteousness.

**Portions belonging to the Priests**


3️⃣ Portions belonging to the Priests

➡️ For the Tresspass Offering, every male among the priests could eat of the remaining meat after sacrificing the stipulated parts while in the court of the sanctuary where the priests gathered for their common meal, (vs 6).

➡️ For both the sin and the tresspass offerings, the officiating priest was to have the remainder of the meat after sacrificing it, (vs 7).

➡️ For the Burnt Offerings, the priest was to get the skin while burning the whole flesh for an offering, (vs 8).

➡️ For the Grain/Food offering that was baked or fried, it belonged to the officiating priest after the sacrifice (vs 9)

➡️ The grain offerings mixed with oil or dry were to be shared equally among the priests after sacrificing a handful of the offering (vs 10)

➡️ For Peace Offerings, if they were for thanksgiving, the offerer in addition would bring cakes of leavened bread, and present 1 of every offering as a contribution to the Lord, it belonged to the presiding priest (one on duty), (vs 14)

➡️ For the flesh of the thanksgiving peace offering, the offerer would eat with folk and friends, and the Levite (vs 15), compare with Deuteronomy 12:12, 19.

➡️ For the sacrifice of the peace offerings, the breast was for the priests (Lev 7:31).

➡️ Also for the Peace Offerings, the presiding priest would get the right thigh (vs 32).

*️⃣ The Lord concludes by categorically emphasizing that, "This is the portion of the anointing of Aaron, and of the anointing of his sons, out of the offerings of the LORD made by fire....... ." (vs 35, 36).

❗ Few realize how God deliberately took the welfare of his ministers and ministry very seriously. It goes without saying that, it is not God's design that the ministry and ministers languish in abject poverty, financially incapacitated, with broken down cars, no homes of their own, under funded for their evangelistic campaigns and the mission gets stalled for lack of funds while members across the globe idly watch on and stay aloof.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Lord help me to understand that provision for your ministers is on us. May I do my honest part as you help me. In Jesus' name, Amen.

## Leviticus 8


### Summary

The Consecration of Aaron and his sons to the priestly office.


### Key Lessons


**Atonement of Aaron and his sons**


1️⃣ Atonement of Aaron and his sons

➡️ Before the priests could minister on behalf of others, their sins had to be atoned for first.

*️⃣ Before we go out to spread the gospel far and wide, we first need the Divine touch and His cleansing.

**The Priest's Apparel**


2️⃣ The Priest's Apparel

➡️ Moses was instructed by God to wash and clothe the priests who were to assume office.

➡️ This symbolized that on their own they could not cleanse themselves, neither where their characters worthy to merit salvation, but they had to be clothed with Christ's righteousness.

*️⃣ Likewise, we need the Savior to wash us from all unrighteousness and clothe us with his robe of righteousness.

⏩ "The miter of the high priest consisted of the white linen turban, having attached to it by a lace of blue, a gold plate bearing the inscription, "Holiness to Jehovah." Everything connected with the apparel and deportment of the priests was to be such as to impress the beholder with a sense of the holiness of God, the sacredness of His worship, and the purity required of those who came into His presence," {Ellen G. White: Patriarchs and Prophets 351}.

**Consecration for God's Service**


3️⃣ Consecration for God's Service

➡️ From the blood of the sacrifice of consecration, Moses applied blood on these priests'

(a) right ear (symbolizing that they would hearken to God's commands).

(b) right thumb (pointing to their submission of the works of their hands to the Will of God).

(c) right toe (signifying that they would go on errands that are sanctioned by the Lord).

*️⃣ May it be our experience too, may our whole lives be consecrated to our Savior.

**Consecration and Meditation**


4️⃣ Consecration and Meditation

➡️ After the consecration service, the priest were to stay for 1 full week in the sanctuary before going back home.

➡️ This enabled them to study, pray, meditate and rehearse their duties.

*️⃣ We need to devote time to the study of Scriptures, Prayer and meditation.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our dear heavenly Father, consecrate us to Thyself now and forever more, Amen.

## Leviticus 10


### Summary

Nadab and Abihu are stricken dead by the Lord for introducing strange fire.


### Key Lessons


**Positions do not make holy**


1️⃣ Positions do not make holy

➡️ Nadàb and Abihu had no excuse, they had been with Moses and Aaron in the Mount of God. They had been instructed for a full week after their consecration as priests.

*️⃣ Yet even those elevated positions they held, could not break off the chains of sin in them.

*️⃣ We are to purpose in our hearts not to sin against God. It's not our positions that will save us.

**Strange Fire**


2️⃣ Strange Fire

➡️ Instead of using the holy fire that had been lighted by the Lord, they used common fire.

*️⃣ How often strange fire gets introduced in worship today? Music, dress code, teachings, diet etc all are proving to be strange fires in our day.

️⃣ The lamp symbolized Christ the light of the world. "Then spake Jesus again unto them, saying, I am the light of the world: he that followeth me shall not walk in darkness, but shall have the light of life." (John 8:12.)*

️⃣ "Light was before them. As the moon and the stars of the solar system shine by the reflected light of the sun, so, as far as their teaching is true, do the world's great thinkers reflect the rays of the Sun of Righteousness. Every gem of thought, every flash of the intellect, is from the Light of the world." {Ellen G. White: Desire of Ages 465}.*

**Shewbread**


2️⃣ Shewbread

➡️ 12 loaves of bread were always at the table.

️⃣ 12 represented the 12 tribes of Israel and the bread pointed to Christ the bread that came down from heaven. (John 6:51)*

#️⃣ "The Holy Spirit comes to the soul as a Comforter. By the transforming agency of His grace, the image of God is reproduced in the disciple; he becomes a new creature. Love takes the place of hatred, and the heart receives the divine similitude. This is what it means to live "by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God." This is eating the Bread that comes down from heaven." {Ellen G. White: Desire of Ages 391}.

**Punishment for blasphemy**


3️⃣ Punishment for blasphemy

➡️ The son of an Egyptian man and an Israelite woman, was not allowed within the camp but made his way nevertheless.

➡️ In addition to this, having quarrelled with an Israelite, he blasphemed God.

*️⃣ God meted death as his punishment and he was stoned.

➡️ We are to reverence God for He will not hold him guiltless who profanes His name or takes it in vain.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** In the name of the Father, Son and Holy Spirit, fill us with your Word and light. Amen.

## Leviticus 25


### Summary

The Sabbatical year and the Year of Jubilee


### Key Lessons


**Sabbatical Year**


1️⃣ Sabbatical Year

➡️ Every 7th year would be a Sabbath hence the whole nation would not cultivate that year.

*️⃣ With the introduction of modern day crop rotation and leaving the land fullow. Men are reverting back to the principles instituted by God to the Israelites.

⏩ "Men were to cooperate with God in restoring the diseased land to health, that it might be a praise and a glory to His name. And as the land they possessed would, if managed with skill and earnestness, produce its treasures, so their hearts, if controlled by God, would reflect His character. . . ." {SDA Bible Commentary 1112.6}.

**The Year of Jubilee**


2️⃣ The Year of Jubilee

➡️ During the Year of Jubilee, debts were cancelled and property returned to the owners and economical slaves were set free.

*️⃣ Here God, was balancing and leveling the ground for both poor and rich alike.

#️⃣ "God’s word sanctions no policy that will enrich one class by the oppression and suffering of another. In all our business transactions it teaches us to put ourselves in the place of those with whom we are dealing, to look not only on our own things, but also on the things of others." {Ellen G. White: Ministry of Healing 187}.

**The kinsman Redeemer**


3️⃣ The kinsman Redeemer

➡️ In the year of Jubilee, all those who had their fields and property attached as collateral or sold, had a chance to redeem them back.

➡️ Nevertheless, if one was poor and could not afford, then his kinsman would come and buy back his land on behalf of his relative.

*️⃣ The kinsman redeemer foreshadowed Christ who came to purchase for us life eternal and salvation which we could not afford on our own.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Dear Jesus, thank you for being our Kinsman Redeemer. We owe our all to you. Amen.

## Leviticus 27


### Summary

Redemption money for humans and animals


### Key Lessons


**1️⃣ Importance of Vows**


1️⃣ Importance of Vows

➡️ The chapter emphasizes the importance of keeping one's vows.

*️⃣ We are not to make vows of fools (that is in a rash manner or out of emotions, but later fail to fulfill them).

➡️ God makes a way of helping those who had made vows and later realized that they could not keep them, by redeeming with money the people or animals they had made vows on.

*️⃣ By so doing, one was not released from their obligation to fulfill their vow yet at the same time making it easier for one to do so.

**God, the owner of Tithe**


2️⃣ God, the owner of Tithe

➡️ God declared that all Tithe belongs to him of all our income and increase.

*️⃣ None should feel excused from this heaven made duty of returning to God what is due Him.

*️⃣ Tithe is our acknowledgement of God's ownership of the whole universe

*️⃣ Offering is our expression of love to our Creator and Redeemer.

**An antidote to Selfishness**


3️⃣ An antidote to Selfishness

➡️ Retaining Tithes and Offerings is a cure to pride and selfishness.

*️⃣ By acknowledging that we are mere stewards, and God the owner, (through retuning what is due to Him), we are healed of the malady of self seeking and pride.

*️⃣ The arch rebel (Satan) was sacked from heaven for wanting to usurp the position and authority of his Maker.


> 🙏 **Prayer:** Our Father who art in heaven, today and forever more we acknowledge and accept your ownership and rulership of the earth and all that in them is. Help us to be faithful to you always. Amen.
